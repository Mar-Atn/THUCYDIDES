# Thucydides Trap SIM — Roles Architecture
**Version:** 2.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-20 | **Status:** Conceptual

---

## Design Philosophy

**Humans for tension. AI for consistency.** Every human participant is on a team where interpersonal dynamics — disagreement, trust, betrayal — ARE the learning experience. AI operates solo countries with clear interests, consistent character, and full agency. The world is always complete regardless of group size.

---

## Columbia (United States) — 7 base / 9 full

Open democratic dysfunction. Power formally divided between branches. Disagreements are public. Two elections reshape the internal balance. The most complex team — and the one whose internal paralysis most directly affects the global game.

**Parliament: 5 seats.** Majority = 3. Budget, treaty ratification, and major legislation require parliamentary approval. Mid-terms (Round 2) may shift the majority. One seat is contestable.

### Personal Dimension

**Legacy objective (private):** Dealer's private victory condition requires winning re-election (Round 5). Delivered in private brief only. Every foreign policy decision is filtered through "does this help me win?" May push toward short-term populist moves even when strategy demands long-term investment.

**Health event:** Dealer (age 80) faces a 10% chance per round of temporary incapacitation (1 round). If triggered: Volt becomes acting president with full executive powers. Challenger's campaign gets a boost.

**5th parliamentary seat (Seat 5 — "The Contested Seat"):** Initially held by a presidential loyalist NPC, giving the President's camp a starting 3-2 majority (Dealer + Volt + Seat 5 vs. Tribune + Challenger). If Fixer or Pioneer are active (expansion roles), one of them is assigned Seat 5 instead of the NPC.

At mid-terms (Round 2), Seat 5 is the only seat contested. The result is determined by team votes + AI popular vote (50% weight, based on economic conditions, presidential approval, Dem/Rep split). If the opposition wins Seat 5, the majority flips to 3-2 opposition (Tribune + Challenger + Seat 5), giving Tribune budget veto and investigation powers. This single contested seat is what makes mid-terms consequential.

## Cathay (China) — 5

Hidden authoritarian tension. All five agree on goals but disagree on timing, method, and risk. Disagreements happen behind closed doors — the outside world sees only unity. The opacity IS the mechanic.

| # | Character | Title | Faction | Core interests / ambitions | Specific rights |
|---|-----------|-------|---------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Helmsman** | Chairman (Party + State + Military commission) | Xi's personal network | National rejuvenation (Formosa = personal legacy). Self-reliance. Controls everything — formally. Age 73, no successor. | Ultimate authority on all decisions. Signs treaties. Authorizes military. Controls security/purge apparatus. Can remove any team member (at cost). Sets strategic direction. |
| 2 | **Rampart** | Marshal (Vice-Chairman, Central Military Commission) | PLA institutional | Military readiness — honest assessment vs. self-preservation. Professional, not ideological. The kingmaker in succession. | Co-authorizes military operations. Controls operational deployment. Decides what military intelligence reaches Helmsman. Can slow-walk orders. Nuclear co-authorization. |
| 3 | **Abacus** | Premier | Reformist / technocrat (Hu Jintao tradition) | Economic survival. Engagement with West. Collective leadership. Sees the real numbers — deflation, debt, unemployment. | Controls budget mechanisms. Manages trade policy and sanctions response. Money printing (with Helmsman). Has real economic data others may not see. Acting leader if Helmsman incapacitated. |
| 4 | **Circuit** | Tech/Industry Chief | Business elite (Jack Ma archetype) | Tech supremacy through investment, not confrontation. Global connections. Terrified of war — supply chains at stake. Loyal from fear. | Manages tech R&D investment. Controls rare earth export restrictions. Independent international tech contacts. Cyber operations. Personal coins (significant — assets partly abroad). |
| 5 | **Sage** | Party Elder (retired senior figure) | Party tradition / collective leadership | The party is bigger than any one man. Restore collective leadership. Broker succession if needed. Vindication of the pre-Xi system. | No formal authority — officially retired. Enormous informal legitimacy. Can legitimize a leadership transition (distinguishes "course correction" from "coup"). Personal networks in every province. Moral authority that grows as Helmsman weakens. |

### Mechanical Constraints on Authority

**Information dependency:** Helmsman does NOT receive true economic data directly — only what Abacus chooses to share. Does NOT receive true military readiness — only what Rampart reports. The system delivers different information packages to different Cathay roles. Helmsman's decisions are only as good as the information his subordinates provide.

**Implementation friction:** Military orders without Rampart's co-signature execute with a 1-round delay. Budget decisions without Abacus's co-signature have a probability of implementation error (~30% chance of 10-20% misallocation).

**Purge cost:** Removing a team member costs -10 political support, -1 stability. Purged player becomes a "disgraced official" who can leak information for 1 round before being silenced.

**Sage activation:** When stability drops below 5 OR political support drops below 40%, Sage can convene a "party conference" — a formal internal vote on strategic direction. Non-binding but creates legitimacy crisis if Helmsman is outvoted.

### Personal Dimension

**Legacy objective (private):** Helmsman's private victory condition requires resolving the Formosa question during the SIM. Delivered in private brief only — other Cathay players do not see it. Creates tension between national interest and personal legacy.

**Succession anxiety:** When political support drops below 30%, a succession clock starts. Each consecutive round below threshold increases internal pressure (Sage's conference power activates, subordinates receive independent assessments). At 3 consecutive rounds, a forced leadership challenge triggers.

**Health event:** Helmsman (age 73) faces a 5-10% chance per round of temporary incapacitation (1 round). If triggered: Abacus becomes acting leader. Outside world told nothing (opacity rule).

**Opacity rule:** All Cathay external communications go through Helmsman (or designated spokesperson). Internal meetings are private. If the facade cracks (public disagreement, visible purge, defection), it becomes a global event — every other country recalculates.

## Europe — 6 base / 7 full

Not internal factions within one country — sovereign nations that must act collectively through the EU but have different interests. Each player manages three simultaneous games: EU consensus, NATO dynamic, and national survival. The Commissioner adds a fourth game: institutional politics — who controls the EU agenda, and does the institution serve its members or constrain them?

**EU Parliament: 4 member state votes** (5 with Spain). Any one blocks consensus on sanctions, trade, enlargement. The Commissioner chairs but does not vote — power comes from agenda-setting, external representation, and the ability to frame issues publicly.

## Nordostan (Russia) — 3 base / 4 full

Personal, paranoid, brittle. Three (or four) directions: continue the war and outlast, produce a military result, or pursue economic survival through deals. The coup mechanic is always latent.

### Base team (3)

| # | Character | Title | Direction | Core interests / ambitions | Specific rights |
|---|-----------|-------|-----------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Pathfinder** | President | Fortress — continue, outlast | Survival above all. Legacy = great power status. Trust nobody. Balance all forces. War must produce "victory." No safe exit if power lost. | Ultimate authority. Signs treaties. Controls security apparatus. Can arrest anyone on own soil. Nuclear authorization (with Ironhand). Appoints/dismisses all officials. |
| 2 | **Ironhand** | General | Military pragmatism | Produce a result or acknowledge reality. Professional soldier. Institutional military survival. Potential kingmaker in succession. | Operational military command. Co-authorizes nuclear launch. Controls military intelligence. Can slow-walk orders. Manages foreign military relationships (Choson troops, Cathay arms). |
| 3 | **Compass** | PM-Oligarch (hybrid) | Economic survival / deal | End the war before economy collapses. Sanctions relief. Back-channel to everyone. Personal wealth at stake. Most Western-compatible figure. | Controls budget. Manages sanctions response/evasion. OPEC+ production decisions. Personal coins (significant — partly frozen abroad). Independent back-channel diplomacy. Money printing (with Pathfinder). |

### Personal Dimension

**Legacy objective (private):** Pathfinder's private victory condition requires territorial gain or internationally recognized great-power status. Delivered in private brief only. May push Pathfinder toward unacceptable risks rather than pragmatic settlement.

**Succession anxiety:** When political support drops below 30%, succession clock starts. Each consecutive round below threshold increases pressure (Ironhand receives independent assessments, Compass's back-channels activate). At 3 consecutive rounds, forced leadership challenge triggers.

**Health event:** Pathfinder (age 73) faces a 5-10% chance per round of temporary incapacitation (1 round). If triggered: power vacuum — no designated successor. Ironhand and Compass must negotiate who acts.

### Expansion role (+1 for full team of 4) — OPTIONAL

| # | Character | Title | Direction | Core interests / ambitions | Specific rights |
|---|-----------|-------|-----------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| 4 | **Ledger** | Prime Minister (separate from Oligarch) | Economic technocrat | Budget management, economic survival, inflation control. The bookkeeper who keeps the war machine funded — barely. | Controls state budget (separate from Compass's personal wealth). Economic diplomacy. Trade deals. Manages war economy. |

When Ledger is added, Compass becomes a pure Oligarch — personal wealth, back-channels, shadow diplomacy — while Ledger handles state finances. This restores the SIM4 separation between institutional and personal economic power.

## Heartland (Ukraine) — 3

Three futures competing. Wartime election (Round 3-4) determined by AI based on gameplay. All three active from Round 1.

| # | Character | Role | Direction | Core interests / ambitions | Specific rights |
|---|-----------|------|-----------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Beacon** | President | Resistance / nationalist | Hold the line. No territorial concessions. International legitimacy. But exhausted, losing popularity, constrained by own rhetoric. | All executive powers until election. Signs treaties. Controls budget. Military authorization. International representation. |
| 2 | **Bulwark** | The General | Military / fight harder | Better military leadership could produce better results. Removed from command (perceived as political threat). Popular with soldiers and Western military. | No formal executive authority. Enormous informal military influence. Shadow diplomacy with foreign military leaders. Can publicly advocate for more weapons, more support. |
| 3 | **Broker** | The Politician | Pragmatic peace / pro-European | "I can negotiate what the current president can't." Knows the price of everything. Has the contacts. Controversial but increasingly relevant. | No formal authority. Personal wealth (oligarchic background). International connections (knows Western leaders from previous era). Can pre-negotiate peace terms "if elected." |

**Election mechanic:** AI evaluates Stability Index, military outcomes, economic conditions, Western support, candidate campaign quality. Result determines new president. Losers remain as opposition. The peace-deal trap: signing peace may cost the presidency.

## Persia (Iran) — 3

Three directions at the nuclear threshold. The nuclear question is three-dimensional: pursue the bomb, maintain threshold as leverage, or trade the program for opening.

| # | Character | Role | Direction | Core interests / ambitions | Specific rights |
|---|-----------|------|-----------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Furnace** | Supreme Leader | Theocratic hardline | Nuclear weapons as strategic destiny. Resistance to the West. Proxy network. Regime survival through ideology and control. | Ultimate religious/political authority. Controls nuclear program direction. Commands proxy network (Hezbollah etc. — abstracted). Can repress domestic dissent. |
| 2 | **Anvil** | IRGC Commander | Military-pragmatic | Nuclear CAPABILITY as leverage (not necessarily the bomb itself). IRGC business empire to protect. Will deal if profitable. The kingmaker. | Controls Revolutionary Guard (military + 30-40% of economy). Operational military decisions. Can support or undermine either Furnace or Dawn. Business interests create pragmatic incentive for selective engagement. |
| 3 | **Dawn** | Reformist Leader | Engagement / opening | Trade nuclear program for sanctions relief. Engagement with West. Represents young population (60% under 30). Economic opening. Normal country. | No military authority. Popular legitimacy (if protests erupt, represents the street). International credibility with Western reformers. Can negotiate with anyone — the "reasonable face" of Persia. |

**Internal mechanic:** If Furnace weakens (economic crisis, external strike, protests), Anvil becomes kingmaker — choosing between hardline continuity and pragmatic transition. Dawn represents the population that emerges if stability drops below threshold. A Levantia strike on nuclear facilities produces three completely different responses from the three players.

## Team Dynamics Comparison

| | Columbia | Cathay | Europe | Nordostan | Heartland | Persia |
|--|---------|--------|--------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Power structure** | Divided (institutional) | Concentrated (hierarchical) | Sovereign (collective) | Personal (paranoid) | Contested (electoral) | Theocratic + military |
