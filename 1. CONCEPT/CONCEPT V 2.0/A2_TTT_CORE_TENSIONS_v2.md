# Thucydides Trap SIM — Core Tension Statement
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-20

---

## The Central Dynamic

The growing economic, military (especially naval and nuclear), and technological power of China is creating structural tension with the United States and its alliance system. This is the Thucydides Trap: a power transition that history tells us ends in war 75% of the time.

What makes THIS transition uniquely dangerous is the personal dimension. China's leader has staked his legacy on "national rejuvenation" — including the Taiwan question. He is 73, has abolished succession mechanisms, and faces an economic slowdown his system has never experienced. The structural logic says "be patient, time is on China's side." The personal logic may say "my time is running out, and the one achievement that justifies permanent rule is slipping away." When structural opportunity meets personal desperation, that is when traps spring.

Every other actor in the system sees the shift and must recalculate. Japan asks: if Taiwan falls, can we remain demilitarized? Can we rely on America? Europe asks: is the US still committed to our defense, or are we on our own? India asks: which side do I join — or can I play both? Russia asks: am I China's partner or China's client? Saudi Arabia asks: who will dominate the next century, and where should I place my bet?

**The SIM's central question is not "will there be a war over Taiwan." It is: what happens to the entire global order when the power that built it can no longer sustain it alone — and the power replacing it offers a different vision of how the world should work?**

## The Focal Events

These are not scripted — they are **attractors** that the scenario gravitates toward based on participant decisions. Any combination may materialize. The number that activate is the measure of outcome.

### 1. The US Presidential Election (Most likely - final round of the SIM)
The structural climax and the central point of big ideas for the US and for the world order. Will the current president survive? Will the next one honor existing commitments and political vector? What type of leadership and ideas todays US (and world) needs? Every alliance, every deal, every military posture is haunted by the question: what happens after the election? 

### 2. Five Known Theaters of Potential Escalation

Each is a simmering situation that could explode. Each has different stakes for different actors. Any escalation — to nuclear, to full-scale war, to a new theater opening — raises the global tension level and makes all other situations more dangerous.

**Taiwan:** The ultimate attractor. Where the structural power shift meets geography, semiconductors, and nuclear risk. Not a starting crisis — but the situation that becomes increasingly likely as Cathay's military capability crosses thresholds and Columbia's attention is pulled elsewhere. If it ignites, it's civilization-level.

**Ukraine:** The legacy war, year 4+. Tests whether borders can be changed by force. Drains Western resources and attention. A peace deal, a frozen conflict, or an escalation — each reshapes the global landscape. The wartime election (three candidates, AI-judged) makes it personally dramatic.

**Middle East:** Iran's nuclear program approaching the weapons threshold. Israel's willingness to strike unilaterally. Oil as a geopolitical weapon. A regional escalation that drags Columbia deeper into a theater it's trying to leave — the classic overstretch trap.

**Caribbean:** The hegemon's own backyard. Russian and Chinese military foothold near US soil. Proves Columbia can't secure its near-abroad while managing a global transition. A provocation here is cheap for adversaries and expensive for Columbia — the asymmetry that small actors exploit.

**Greenland:** Not a war theater — a trust crisis. Columbia's president pursuing territorial ambitions against an ally's sovereign territory. Demonstrates the system is breaking from INSIDE: the hegemon that built the rules-based order is now violating it. Every ally watching the Greenland question asks: if America does this to friends, what are alliance guarantees worth?


### 3. The Global Alliances Reshaping
What will all other nations do? What will be Europe's role and actions? Where will other important nations (like India, Russia, Koreas, Japan, Brasil etc.) end up doing, when the world order is reshaking (political/idological, economic, technological and military)?


### 4. The Financial Architecture Fracture
The non-military path to the same outcome. BRICS+ currency union, alternative payment systems, de-dollarization — the moment the dollar loses its monopoly is the moment the old order ends without a shot fired. This is the "quiet catastrophe" that may matter more long-term than any war. It requires collective action by countries that struggle to agree on anything — making it uncertain, slow-building, but transformative if achieved.

### 5. The AI Breakthrough Moment
A potential technology breakthrough that could suddenly make the power balance question moot. Whoever reaches frontier AI first gains decisive advantages across ALL domains — economic productivity, military capability, intelligence, information warfare. This is the amplifier: it makes the Trap more dangerous (the lagging power faces a closing window) and more consequential (the winner may gain a permanent, structural advantage). The tech race IS the modern arms race — but with economic and societal effects that nuclear weapons never had.

---

**What could resolve it:** Extraordinary diplomatic creativity — a managed transition framework, extraordinary ladership, a new institutional architecture, a grand bargain between the great powers, regional settlement packages, crisis leading to new thinking and new behaviours of global actors. None of these is easy. All require leaders to accept outcomes their domestic politics punish. The SIM succeeds when participants discover — through experience — the complex dynamic of the system and its paterns, why escape is so hard, and what kind of leadership it requires.
