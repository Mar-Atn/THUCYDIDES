# THE THUCYDIDES TRAP — Concept Document

**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-25 | **Author:** MetaGames
**Status:** CONCEPT — Top of Design Hierarchy

---

## 1. The Premise

When a rising power threatens to displace a ruling power, the resulting structural stress makes war between them more likely — not because either side wants it, but because fear, miscalculation, and entanglement make it hard to avoid. Thucydides identified this dynamic 2,400 years ago. Graham Allison's research found it led to war in 12 of 16 historical cases.

Today, the rising power is China. The ruling power is the United States. And unlike any previous case, this power transition unfolds across an interconnected global system where economic interdependence, nuclear deterrence, AI technology, and dozens of other actors make the situation both more dangerous and more complex than any historical precedent.

**The central question of this simulation is not "will there be war over Taiwan."**

**It is: what happens to the entire global order when the power that built it can no longer sustain it alone — and the power rising to challenge it has a fundamentally different vision of how the world should work?**

---

## 2. The Three-Layer Trap

The simulation is built on the insight that the Thucydides Trap operates at three layers simultaneously — and the most dangerous moments occur when all three layers interact.

**Layer 1 — Structural:** The visible power balance is shifting. China leads in manufacturing, trade volume, and key technologies. The US leads in military power, financial systems, AI research, and alliances. Neither side has a decisive advantage. This ambiguity is itself destabilizing.

**Layer 2 — Situational:** Five simultaneous crises create a "too many fires" problem. A war in Eastern Europe draining Western resources and attention. A nuclear threshold state in the Middle East. A semiconductor chokepoint in the Taiwan Strait. An assertive hegemon testing its own rules in the Caribbean. A technology race that could give decisive advantage to whoever crosses the AI frontier first. Each crisis is manageable alone. Together, they overwhelm the system's capacity to respond.

**Layer 3 — Personal:** Three aging leaders face the intersection of national interest and personal survival. The Chairman of the rising power, 73, no successor, whose legacy depends on reunification with the island. The President of the revisionist nuclear power, 73, no successor, whose war must produce something that looks like victory. And the President of the declining hegemon, whose every decision is measured against an election two years away. The structural clock says patience. The personal clock says act now. When they contradict — that's when traps spring.

## 3. The World

The simulation uses fictional country names with transparent real-world parallels — close enough for instant recognition, distant enough for psychological freedom and political safety in corporate settings.

### Team Countries (multiple human players each)

| SIM Name | Parallel | Team Size | Core Tension |
|----------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Columbia** | USA | 7-9 | Declining hegemon with democratic dysfunction — president, opposition, and swing voters pulling in different directions while managing global commitments |
| **Cathay** | China | 5 | Rising challenger presenting unified facade while five internal factions disagree on timing, method, and acceptable risk |
| **Europe** (Gallia, Teutonia, Freeland, Ponte, Albion) | France, Germany, Poland, Italy, UK | 5-6 | Collective action failure between sovereign nations — strategic autonomy vs. Atlantic loyalty vs. China trade dependency |
| **Nordostan** | Russia | 3-4 | Paranoid autocracy where the war must produce "victory" but three internal camps disagree on what that means |
| **Heartland** | Ukraine | 3 | Nation at war facing an impossible choice — peace may cost the presidency, continued war may cost the nation |
| **Persia** | Iran | 3 | Three-way split at nuclear threshold — pursue the bomb, maintain ambiguity, or trade the program for opening |

### Solo Countries (1 human or AI each)

10 countries operated by individual human players or autonomous AI: Bharata (India), Levantia (Israel), Formosa (Taiwan), Phrygia (Turkey), Yamato (Japan), Solaria (Saudi Arabia), Choson (N. Korea), Hanguk (S. Korea), Caribe (Cuba+Venezuela), Mirage (UAE).

### Organizations

8 standing organizations with distinct decision rules — from NATO's consensus requirement to BRICS+'s loose coordination. Players may create new organizations during play. The EU requires unanimity (any member blocks). The UN Security Council has P5 vetoes. Columbia's parliament has majority voting with a contested swing seat.

## 4. What Participants Experience

### Format
- **Gameplay duration:** 8-12 hours (target: 8 hours of pure play for single-day format, enabled by AI-powered engine processing; up to 12 hours in two-day format with overnight)
- **Participants:** 27-39 humans + 10 AI-operated countries
- **Rounds:** 6-8 rounds, each representing 6 months of real time (H2 2026 to ~H1 2030)
- **Dramatic arc:** Act 1 (Setup, alliances form, mid-term elections) → Act 2 (Escalation, crises emerge, UNGA vote forces declarations) → Act 3 (Climax — presidential election, consequences)

### The Experience Loop
Each round, participants:
1. **Negotiate freely** — face-to-face, in corridors, in formal organization meetings, through back-channels (60-80 minutes)
2. **Take actions** — deploy military, set tariffs, impose sanctions, conduct covert operations, make public statements, transfer resources, sign treaties, arrest rivals, launch missiles — all processed in real-time by two live engines
3. **Receive consequences** — the World Model Engine processes all actions between rounds and returns an updated world state: new economic indicators, political stability shifts, military positions, technology breakthroughs, narrative events (5-12 minutes)
4. **Adapt** — new units deployed, new intelligence received, new crises emerging from consequences of prior decisions

### The Key Scheduled Events
- **Round 2:** Columbia mid-term elections (one contested parliamentary seat — if opposition wins, the president loses legislative majority)
- **Round 3-4:** Heartland wartime election (AI-judged based on actual SIM events), UNGA vote forcing public alliance declarations
- **Round 5:** Columbia presidential election — THE climactic event of the simulation. Weighted votes from team members + 50% AI-generated popular vote reflecting SIM events.
- **Overnight (2-day format):** The SIM stays "on." Secret deals, coup conspiracies, back-channel negotiations continue between days.

## 5. The Four Domains

Participants operate across four interconnected domains. The simulation's power comes from the cascading connections between them.

**Military** — Five unit types (ground, naval, tactical air, strategic missiles, air defense). RISK-based combat with modifiers. Nuclear weapons at three levels — from tactical to strategic MAD. Five potential theaters that activate dynamically (a new theater map appearing is itself a signal that containment has failed somewhere). Combat is catastrophic, unpredictable, and politically costly — never a clean strategic lever.

**Economy** — Coins as universal currency. National budgets balancing social spending, military production, and R&D. Tariffs (0-3 per sector), sanctions (coalition-based, -3 to +3 across four types), oil production decisions (OPEC+ prisoner's dilemma). Financial markets, inflation, trade flows. Personal wealth for key roles — creating conflicts between national and personal interest.

**Domestic Politics** — Stability (1-10) and Political Support (0-100%). Democratic countries face elections they can lose. Autocratic countries face coups they don't see coming. Low stability triggers protests, reduces military morale, and halves economic output. The four quadrants: high stability + high support = freedom to act; low stability + low support = collapse zone. Every foreign policy decision has domestic political cost.

**Technology** — Nuclear track (L0-3) and AI/Semiconductor track (L0-4). The semiconductor chokepoint: Formosa fabricates most advanced chips — blockade or invasion drops all dependent countries one tech level. AI breakthrough (L4) delivers +30% GDP and +2 combat dice — a potential game-changer that makes the tech race existential. R&D requires sustained investment through budget allocation.

**The Cascade:** Military action → economic cost → domestic pressure → political constraint on military. Economic warfare → domestic pain on both sides → pressure to escalate or de-escalate. Tech advantage → economic growth → military superiority → political freedom. Domestic crisis → desperate foreign policy → escalation → wider crisis.

## 6. What Makes This Unique

**AI participants that are peers, not assistants.** 10 solo countries operated by autonomous AI agents with their own goals, strategies, memories, and negotiation capabilities. They lie, pressure, surprise, and adapt. They participate in meetings, vote in organizations, deploy forces, and pursue agendas. Humans interact with them the same way they interact with other humans — and may not always know which is which.

**A world model that combines calculation with judgment.** The engine uses deterministic formulas for what can be calculated (budgets, production, trade), probabilistic models for what involves chance (combat, covert ops, elections), and AI-assisted assessment for what requires qualitative judgment (second-order economic effects, narrative generation, political sentiment). The moderator reviews and can adjust before results publish.

**Personal stakes inside structural dynamics.** Roles are designed with three layers: position/resources, interests/needs, and norms/values/identity. Several key roles have personal wealth that can conflict with national interest. The Columbia president faces re-election. The Cathay chairman has no successor. The Nordostan president has no safe exit. Personal survival calculations diverge from strategic rationality — and that's where the learning happens.

**No scripted outcomes.** Five focal attractors exist (Taiwan crisis, Columbia election, Heartland resolution, financial architecture fracture, AI breakthrough) but none is guaranteed. The simulation creates structural pressure and lets human decisions determine what actually happens. Different groups playing the same scenario produce radically different outcomes.

**A Navigator AI assistant** for every participant — a personal mentor across three phases (before, during, after the SIM) that helps players set learning goals, understand their situation, think strategically, and reflect on what happened. Not a gameplay advantage tool — a development companion.

## 7. The Learning

### For Participants

**I. Explore the Emerging New World Order.**
The simulation immerses CEOs, founders, and senior leaders in the dynamics that are actively reshaping the global system they operate in. Not as news consumers — as decision-makers inside the system. What does the US-China power transition mean for supply chains, alliances, technology access, and market structure? Participants don't study it. They live it.

**II. Develop Leadership Skills in Three Dimensions:**

- **Systems thinking under pressure.** Participants experience firsthand how decisions in one domain cascade into others — how a tariff decision affects alliance stability, how military mobilization changes domestic politics, how economic sanctions reshape technology access. No briefing teaches this. Only the experience of facing consequences you didn't anticipate from decisions you made under time pressure.

- **Leadership in dynamic complexity.** There is too much happening for anyone to control. Leaders must prioritize, delegate, negotiate under uncertainty, make irreversible decisions with incomplete information, and live with consequences they didn't intend. The simulation reveals each participant's instincts — and whether those instincts serve them.

- **Communication and dealing with conflicts.** Alliance management is not about shared interests — it's about managing divergent interests within a coalition while presenting unity to adversaries. Every team contains internal fractures. Every negotiation has audiences beyond the table. Participants discover how they communicate, persuade, and handle conflict when stakes are real and time is short.

**III. Explore AI-Human Interaction in Social Structure.**
10 AI participants operate at par with humans — negotiating, bluffing, forming alliances, pursuing agendas. Participants experience what it means to collaborate with, compete against, and be surprised by AI agents in a social system. This is not a demo or a thought experiment. It is a lived experience of the emerging reality of AI as a peer in complex human environments.

### For MetaGames

**I. Architectural Model for Future SIMs.** TTT is the most complex simulation in the portfolio — testing all major elements of the SIM taxonomy (roles, entities, time, actions, resources, collective decisions, public speaking, competition field, markets, game engine, contracts, press). It establishes the technology and design architecture that will serve as the foundation for a planned series of simulations.

**II. Marketing Blockbuster.** A geopolitical crisis simulation with AI participants, real-time engines, and 30+ players — designed for CEOs, founders, and entrepreneurs. This is the lead generator: an experience so engaging and distinctive that participants want to bring MetaGames into their organizations.

**III. Learning Organization.** Building TTT is itself the proving ground for a new way of working — human and AI teams cooperating at scale, across design, engineering, content, and testing. What we learn about this collaboration is as valuable as the product itself.

## 8. Scale & Configuration

| Parameter | Minimum | Standard | Maximum |
|-----------|---------|----------|---------|
| Human participants | 27 | 31 | 39+ |
| AI-operated countries | 10 | 10 | 10 |
| Rounds | 6 | 6 | 8 |
| Gameplay duration | 1 day (~8h) | 1.5 days (~10h) | 2 days (~12h) |
| Moderators needed | 1 + assistant | 1 + assistant | 2 + assistant |

The simulation scales by activating optional roles within team countries (Columbia expands from 7 to 9, Nordostan from 3 to 4, Europe adds a 5th EU vote) and by assigning solo countries to humans versus AI.
