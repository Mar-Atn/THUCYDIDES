# Thucydides Trap SIM — World-Building & Naming Concept
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-19 | **Status:** Conceptual — approach decided, names illustrative

---

## The Decision

The SIM uses **transparent fiction**: invented country names on a recognizable but rearranged geography, with all modern mechanics intact. This was chosen over four alternatives after analysis of participant experience, development feasibility, and alignment with MetaGames' mission.

### Options considered

| Option | Verdict | Key reason |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **Transparent fiction** (invented names, modern setting) | **Selected** | Best balance of emotional safety, modern relevance, creative freedom, and proven in Conflict SIM |
| Real names (actual countries and geography) | Rejected | Political sensitivity in international groups. Inhibits bold play. Factual nitpicking. Short shelf life |
| Historical parallel (Sparta/Athens, pre-WWI, Cold War) | Rejected | Every historical period sacrifices essential modern mechanics. Ancient Greece has no nuclear/economic warfare. Pre-WWI has no nuclear deterrence or trade interdependence. Cold War has no economic entanglement or semiconductor dependencies. No single historical setting supports all five gameplay domains |
| Fully invented world (no recognizable parallels) | Rejected | Destroys learning transfer. Massive onboarding cost with 20+ countries. Conflicts with MetaGames mission of "realistic decision environments" |

### Why transparent fiction wins

Validated through the Conflict SIM (SIM4) with live participants across multiple sessions:

- **Instant knowledge transfer.** Participants immediately recognize who each actor represents and bring real-world knowledge. No briefing needed to establish that the northern federation has nuclear weapons and is at war with its neighbor.
- **Permission to play.** The fictional frame liberates bold, creative, counter-intuitive decisions. This is where the deepest learning happens — participants do things they'd never do if they felt they were "representing" a real country.
- **Emotional safety.** In international corporate groups, no participant feels they must defend their real country's positions. The fiction is the safe container that enables genuine exploration.
- **Creative freedom for designers.** Simplifications and dramatizations feel like design choices, not factual errors. The scenario can be balanced for gameplay without real-world accuracy objections.
- **Longevity.** The SIM doesn't become dated when real events change. Replayable for years.

## Naming Approach

### Five principles

Every name must pass all five:

1. **Memorable** — Hearable once, recallable for 10 hours. Prefer 1-3 syllables. Every name must sound distinct from every other.
2. **Evocative** — Hints at the real country without being a pun or joke. Recognition should feel like discovery, not a wink.
3. **Respectful** — A participant from the real country would feel dignified, not mocked. Non-negotiable.
4. **Speakable** — Flows in fast negotiation: "I need to reach Cathay's leader" must feel natural after hundreds of repetitions.
5. **Mappable** — Looks good on a printed or digital map. Short names with distinctive letter shapes work best cartographically.

### Method: blended sources

No single naming system forced. Pick the best name for each country from any source — just as the real world's country names come from wildly different linguistic origins. Sources ranked by preference:

1. **Historical/classical names** — real alternative names once used for the country. Strongest category: real, known, respectful, historically weighted. (e.g., Persia, Cathay, Albion, Formosa)
2. **Countries' own names** — names the country uses for itself in its own language. Deeply respectful. (e.g., Bharata for India)
3. **Geographic derivations** — names from real geographic features. (e.g., Amazonia from the Amazon)
4. **Proven SIM4 names** — already tested with live participants. (Sarmatia, Ruthenia, Freeland, Phrygia)
5. **Evocative inventions** — new names that feel like they could be real. Last resort, only where no better option exists.

### Names flagged for further work

- **Columbia** — strong but Colombia overlap. Test in context.
- **Solaria** — feels science-fiction. Needs a better option for Saudi Arabia.
- **Levantia** — functional but lacks character. The Israel naming challenge is inherently difficult.
- **Choson / Hanguk** — respectful but potentially confusing as a pair for non-Korean speakers.
- **Austoria** — weakest name. Consider alternatives for South Africa.

## Organization Naming

### Hybrid option (recommended for exploration)

Use **fictional country names** but consider keeping **real or near-real organization names**. Organizations are structural and don't carry personal identity. Nobody feels represented by "NATO." This reduces cognitive load significantly.

| Real organization | Option A: Keep real | Option B: Light rename |
|---|---|---|
| NATO | NATO | Western Treaty Alliance (SIM4 precedent) |
| UN | United Nations | League of Nations (SIM4 precedent) |
| EU | Ereb Union | Western Hansa / Continental Union |
| BRICS+ | BRICS+ | The N.E.T. (SIM4 precedent) or new name |
| OPEC+ | OPEC+ | Energy Cartel / Oil Compact |
| G7 | G7 | Council of Seven / Western Forum |
| UN Security Council | UNSC | Security Council of the League |

Decision deferred. Both options viable. Option A minimizes learning burden. Option B maximizes fictional consistency.

## Geography Concept

### Map design principles

A full world map with invented geography: **recognizable but rearranged**. The Conflict SIM's fantasy-cartography style (terrain textures, stylized seas, artistic borders) sets the quality bar.

- **Recognizable continental shapes** — participants orient within seconds
- **Correct relative positions** — Columbia across an ocean, Cathay in the east, Sarmatia vast and northern, Formosa an island off Cathay's coast, Persia in the middle
- **Only SIM actors labeled** — other areas are hinterland, keeping the map clean
- **Maritime chokepoints preserved** — strait near Formosa, gulf near Persia/Solaria, straits near Phrygia, canal connecting seas
- **Centered on the contested middle** — not Atlantic-centered, not Pacific-centered. The Middle East / Central Asia / Indian Ocean in the center, two great powers on opposite edges

### Character naming (future decision)

The Conflict SIM used evocative character names (Pathfinder, Jocker, Moses, Exchequer). This adds another layer of immersion and emotional safety — participants play "Pathfinder," not "the person who is obviously Putin." Same naming principles apply: evocative, respectful, memorable, speakable. To be developed when role design begins.
