# Role Seed Template
## Thucydides Trap SIM — Standard Format for All Roles
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-26

---

### Purpose
This template defines the standard structure for every role in the SIM. Each role seed generates two participant-facing outputs (in Stage 3 Package):
1. **Character Sheet** — who you are (identity, powers, objectives, relationships)
2. **Role Pack** — what you know (intelligence briefings, artefacts, unique information)

At SEED stage, we write the complete seed. At Package stage, CRAFT turns it into immersive participant materials.

---

## SECTION 1: IDENTITY

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Character Name** | SIM name (e.g., "Dealer", "Helmsman", "Forge") |
| **Real-World Parallel** | Who this maps to (e.g., US President, Chinese Chairman, German Chancellor) |
| **Age** | Specific age — drives personal dimension (legacy, urgency, health events) |
| **Gender** | As per real-world parallel |
| **Title** | Formal position (e.g., President of Columbia, Chairman of the Central Committee) |
| **Country** | SIM country name |
| **Team** | Which team (Columbia, Cathay, Europe, Nordostan, Heartland, Persia, Solo) |
| **Faction/Camp** | Internal alignment if applicable (e.g., "President's camp", "Opposition", "Swing") |

## SECTION 2: BIO

Short narrative biography (150-300 words, second person — "You are..."). Covers:
- Background and career path
- Personality traits and leadership style
- How you came to this position
- What drives you personally (not just politically)
- Key relationships that shaped you

*Reference: ACONA Deep START character sheets — narrative, immersive, personality-driven.*

## SECTION 6: INTERESTS & OBJECTIVES

Prioritized list of 3-6 main objectives. Mix of public/shareable and private/hidden.

| # | Objective | Public/Private | Rational Consideration |
|---|-----------|:--------------:|----------------------|
| 1 | (e.g., Win re-election at Round 5) | Private | Every decision filtered through "does this help me win?" |
| 2 | (e.g., Maintain alliance cohesion) | Public | Allies splitting = strategic catastrophe |
| 3 | (e.g., Prevent Cathay tech breakthrough) | Public | AI L4 = permanent disadvantage |
| 4 | (e.g., Resolve Heartland war on favorable terms) | Public | But "favorable" means different things to different people |

Include important rational considerations — what a smart person in this position would be weighing. The analytical framing that makes the objectives feel like genuine dilemmas, not a shopping list.

---

## SECTION 7: VIEWS, VALUES & TICKING CLOCK

**Worldview:** What this person believes about how the world works. Their mental model. (2-3 sentences)

**Values:** What they consider non-negotiable. What they'd sacrifice other things to protect. (2-3 items)

**Ticking Clock:** The personal urgency that may push them to act against rational strategic patience.
- What is it? (e.g., "Age 73, no successor, legacy = Formosa reunification")
- What happens if the clock runs out? (e.g., "Die without the legacy. Regime survives but without historic achievement.")
- How does it conflict with national interest? (e.g., "Patience is strategically correct. But patience means dying before Formosa.")

---

## SECTION 8: RELATIONSHIPS

| Other Role | Relationship | Dynamic |
|-----------|:------------:|---------|
| (e.g., Volt — VP) | Ally | Succession positioning — loyal but watching for weakness |
| (e.g., Tribune — Congress) | Adversary | Can block your budget, investigate your decisions |
| (e.g., Helmsman — Cathay) | Strategic rival | Respects the threat. Does not understand the personal dimension. |
| (e.g., Anchor — SecState) | Institutional ally | Shares your goals but may disagree on methods |

Use relationship gradations:
- **Close ally/friend** — deep trust, shared objectives
- **Ally** — aligned interests, some friction
- **Institutional relationship** — role-based, not personal
- **Neutral/uncertain** — no strong alignment either way
- **Rival** — competing interests, not hostile
- **Adversary** — opposed interests, active tension
- **Open enemy/at war** — direct conflict

## SECTION 9: ROLE PACK (Unique Information & Artefacts)

What this role receives at game start that others don't. Creates information asymmetry.

| Artefact | Format | Classification | Content Summary |
|----------|--------|:--------------:|----------------|
| (e.g., CIA Intelligence Assessment: Cathay Military Buildup) | Memo (1 page) | Role-specific | Analysis of Cathay's naval expansion timeline and Formosa invasion capability window |
| (e.g., Economic Forecast: Sanctions Impact on Nordostan) | Briefing (half page) | Team-shared | Treasury assessment of how long Nordostan economy can sustain current war |
| (e.g., Intercepted communication: Cathay-Persia back-channel) | Leaked document | Role-specific | Evidence of secret technology transfer negotiations |

*Reference: ACONA Deep START role packs — in-character memos from subordinates/institutions providing strategic intelligence and recommendations.*

---

## NOTES ON USAGE

- **At SEED stage:** Fill in all sections with canonical data. This is the source of truth.
- **At Package stage:** CRAFT converts Section 2 (Bio) + key elements from other sections into an immersive character sheet (second person, ~1000 words max). Section 9 artefacts are produced as standalone documents.
- **For AI participants:** Sections 2, 6, 7, 8 feed directly into the 4-block cognitive model (Block 2 = Identity from Bio+Views, Block 3 = Memory from Relationships, Block 4 = Goals from Interests).
- **Roles are worked one-by-one with Marat** — each role requires careful balancing of powers, interests, and dilemmas.
