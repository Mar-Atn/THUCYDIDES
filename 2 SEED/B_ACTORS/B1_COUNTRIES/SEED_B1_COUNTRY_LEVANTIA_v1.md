## SECTION 4: HISTORY & CONTEXT

Levantia enters the SIM at war — and in its element. Operation Rising Lion, launched February 28, 2026, represents the culmination of decades of intelligence preparation against Persia's nuclear program. In coordination with Columbia's Operation Epic Fury, Levantia struck Persia's enrichment facility at Natanz, assassinated Supreme Leader Khamenei and senior military officials, destroyed portions of the ballistic missile arsenal, and degraded early-warning systems across the country. The IDF has struck over 3,000 targets in Persia in the first month. Simultaneously, Levantia launched a war in Lebanon against Hezbollah (since March 2), killing over 1,000 and displacing nearly a million — dismantling Persia's most capable proxy on Levantia's northern border.

The price has not been trivial. Persia retaliated with hundreds of drones and ballistic missiles targeting Levantia and Columbia bases across the Gulf. Levantia's air defense — the densest in the world per square kilometer — has intercepted most but not all. The wartime economy is under strain, with GDP growth suppressed and defense spending consuming an unprecedented share of output. Stability sits at 5 — functional but stressed. Public support for the war remains solid at 52% — Israelis view this as a fight for survival, not a war of choice.

Prime Minister Netanyahu declared Levantia "stronger than ever" in his first wartime address. Domestically, the judicial reform crisis of 2023-24 is suspended — wartime unity papers over deep political fractures that will resurface when fighting stops. The reservist mobilization has pulled hundreds of thousands from the civilian economy.

Levantia's nuclear posture is the elephant in the room. Officially undeclared, universally assumed. Nuclear L1 — a last-resort deterrent that has never been tested or acknowledged. If Persia's program survived the strikes and approaches breakout, Levantia's calculus shifts from conventional to existential.

What makes Levantia interesting to play: you are small, surrounded, and have already pulled the trigger. You have the region's best military and best intelligence service, an undeclared nuclear deterrent, and the unconditional backing of the world's most powerful nation — for now. Your survival depends on finishing what you started before Columbia's political will evaporates, before Persia regroups, and before the world's patience with your wars runs out.

## STRATEGIC ROLE IN THE SIM

Levantia is the region's decisive unilateral actor — a country that creates facts on the ground and forces everyone else to react. Playing Citadel means:

- **You already pulled the trigger.** The Persia war is underway. You are committed. The question is not whether to fight but how to finish — and what "finished" looks like. Nuclear facilities destroyed? Regime change? Ceasefire on your terms? Each outcome has different costs and different enemies.
- **Columbia is your lifeline — and your constraint.** Columbia's political support is currently unconditional, but Columbia is fighting its own domestic battle over the war. If Columbia's Dealer loses political support, your military supply chain and diplomatic cover are at risk. You need Columbia more than Columbia needs you — but you must never say this.
- **Multi-front operations are your reality.** Persia, Lebanon (Hezbollah), Gaza (Hamas residual), Syria, Yemen (Houthis). Each front consumes resources. 6 ground and 4 air units across 5 theaters is overstretch for a country of 10 million people.
- **The nuclear dimension is your ace.** Undeclared, ambiguous, and never tested. If Persia's program survived the strikes and approaches breakout, you face the decision the entire nuclear age was designed to prevent. Escalation to nuclear threat changes the game for every player in the SIM.
- **Isolation is your condition.** No formal organization membership. No votes in any body. Your influence comes through Columbia, through military capability, through intelligence, and through the willingness to act when others hesitate.

The Citadel player wins by achieving security through strength, maintaining Columbia's support, and finishing the Persia campaign before the window closes — knowing that every day of war costs more than the last.
