## SECTION 4: HISTORY & CONTEXT

Solaria enters mid-2026 as the world's energy pricing superpower under direct military threat for the first time in a generation. Crown Prince Wellspring — young, ambitious, transformational, and ruthless — has spent a decade remaking the kingdom: entertainment liberalization, women driving, tourism megaprojects, and Vision 2030's $1 trillion diversification program. The strategy rests on a simple bet: use oil wealth now to build a post-oil economy before the wells run dry or the world stops buying.

That bet is under unprecedented stress. When Columbia and Levantia struck Persia on February 28, 2026, the retaliatory missile barrage hit Solaria's oil infrastructure directly. The Ras Tanura refinery — one of the world's largest — went into partial shutdown. For the first time in history, Iran attacked all six GCC states simultaneously. Solaria raised military readiness to maximum and activated the $16.5 billion arms package Columbia rushed through in mid-March. The kingdom that spent years cultivating "no enemies" diplomacy is now hosting the air war against its neighbor across the Gulf.

The OPEC+ dynamics are equally consequential. Solaria holds roughly 1.8-2 million barrels per day of spare capacity — over 50% of the global strategic reserve. With Persia's exports disrupted by war, Solaria is increasing production toward 10.2 mb/d, balancing the contradictory demands of keeping prices high enough to fund Vision 2030 (fiscal breakeven ~$81/barrel) while keeping them low enough to maintain Columbia's political tolerance for the Persia war. Every production decision reshapes the global economy.

The hedging strategy remains intact despite the war. BRICS+ membership signals multipolarity. Cathay buys a quarter of crude exports and provides technology Columbia will not share. Vision 2030's megaprojects depend on Cathay investment as much as Western expertise. Solaria's calculation: the war with Persia makes Columbia's security umbrella indispensable today, but the post-war order requires options.

What makes Solaria essential to the SIM: every country's economic health depends on oil prices, and Solaria controls the tap. The production dial is the single most consequential economic lever any solo country possesses.

## Strategic Role in the SIM

Solaria is the **energy pricing superpower** — the country whose production decisions most directly reshape every other country's economic conditions. The OPEC+ production dial is the single most consequential economic lever any solo country controls.

**Key strategic choices:**
- **Oil production level:** Increase output to stabilize prices and support Columbia's war effort, or restrain production to maximize revenue and fund Vision 2030? The prisoner's dilemma with other Cartel members is constant.
- **Hedging strategy:** How far to lean toward Columbia (security) vs. Cathay (technology, investment, oil customer)? The war pushes toward Columbia; the post-war future pulls toward diversification.
- **Nuclear threshold:** If Persia approaches nuclear capability, does Solaria pursue its own program? The threat alone is leverage.
- **War posture:** Currently a passive target. Does Solaria formally enter the war against Persia, or maintain the fiction of non-belligerency while hosting Columbia's logistics?
- **BRICS+ currency:** Support a BRICS+ payment system that undermines the dollar — and Columbia's economic power — or stay loyal to the petrodollar system that made Solaria rich?

**Wellspring's personality:** Patient, calculating, leverage-obsessed. Never commits to one side. Believes oil is a weapon and time is on Solaria's side. Modernizer who imprisoned dissidents. Visionary who ordered a journalist killed. The contradictions are the character.
