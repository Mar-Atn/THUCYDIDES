## Thucydides Trap SIM — SEED Specification
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-29 | **Status:** Active
**Classification: FACILITATOR ONLY — Do not distribute to participants. Individual role slices are generated from this master document.**

---

## Purpose

This document maps the **key relationships** for every role in the SIM. Each role gets 3-6 relationships — only the most game-driving ones. Participants cannot hold more than that in working memory.

**How to use:** Slice by role to extract the relationship section for each role's brief. Each relationship is written from THAT role's perspective — asymmetric by design (Dealer's view of Helmsman differs from Helmsman's view of Dealer).

**Visual version:** `SEED_B3_RELATIONSHIPS_v1.html` — interactive graph with all 37 roles and ~80 edges.

**Source of truth for role names:** B2 Role Seed files (`B2_ROLES/SEED_B2_ROLES_*.md`)

## Key Design Notes

1. **Asymmetric by design.** Dealer's view of Helmsman ("I respect the deal-maker") differs from Helmsman's view of Dealer ("structures pull us apart"). This asymmetry creates negotiation space.

2. **3-6 per role.** Participants can hold this many. The full map has ~87 edges but each role sees only their slice.

3. **Flippable relationships.** Several relationships can change during play: Dealer-Pathfinder (deal on Ruthenia?), Pyro-Dealer (engagement?), Lumiere-Sentinel (if Columbia abandons them, they unite), Beacon-Bulwark (peace removes Bulwark's campaign issue).

4. **Intermediary network.** Fixer, Vizier, Wellspring, and Spire serve as bridges between adversaries. These roles create the "back-channel" gameplay that produces the best moments.

5. **Every team has internal tension.** Columbia (succession + constitutional war), Cathay (purge fragility + collective vs. personal), Sarmatia (guns vs. butter), Ruthenia (three-way cage fight), Persia (kingmaker vs. king), Europe (autonomy vs. consensus vs. Atlanticism).
