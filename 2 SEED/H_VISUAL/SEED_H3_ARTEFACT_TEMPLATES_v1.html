Template 3 — Personal Letter

Used for: private communications between leaders, personal appeals, back-channel proposals. Handwritten feel.

PRIVATE — EYES ONLY
Broker
Former President of Heartland
June 2026 — delivered by personal courier

Dear Chancellor Forge,

I write to you not as a politician seeking advantage, but as someone who has watched his country bleed for three years and believes there is another way.

You know my position. Beacon will fight until there is nothing left to fight for — and the army will follow Bulwark, not him. The election will come in months, not years. When it does, Heartland will have a new leader. Whether that leader pursues war or peace depends on what Europe offers in the next six months.

I am asking for one thing: a clear signal that EU accession remains on the table. Not a promise — I know you cannot promise. A signal. A meeting in Brussels. A joint statement. Something I can show my people that says: there is a future beyond this war, and Europe is part of it.

If that signal comes, I can build a coalition for negotiation. If it doesn't, Bulwark wins, and the war goes on until there's nothing left to integrate into your Union.

You have my number. I'll be in Warsaw next week.

With respect and urgency,

Broker

Used for: news bulletins, press releases, public information. Distributed to all participants or specific audiences.

THE GLOBAL DISPATCH
Independent Reporting from the World's Capitals
Vol. XLII No. 7 H2 2026 PUBLIC
Gulf Gate Crisis Enters Fourth Month as Oil Tops $140
Global Dispatch Staff — Muscat Bureau

The Persian blockade of the Gulf Gate shipping lane has now lasted four months, pushing Brent crude past $140 per barrel and triggering emergency energy summits across three continents.

OPEC+ members convened in Riyadh last week but failed to agree on a coordinated response. Wellspring proposed a production increase to compensate for lost Persian exports; Cathay's Abacus offered to release strategic reserves. Neither measure passed.

In Columbia, the economic impact is becoming political. Tribune introduced a resolution demanding the administration present a plan to reopen the Gulf Gate within 90 days. "We cannot fight a war in Persia and pretend the gas pump doesn't exist," Tribune told reporters.

Military analysts estimate that breaking the blockade would require ground forces — air power alone cannot neutralize Persia's coastal missile batteries. Shield has reportedly presented Dealer with three options, none of which can be executed before Round 2.

Meanwhile, countries with overland oil supply routes — notably Cathay, which receives 48% of Persia's exports via pipeline — are largely insulated. "The Gulf Gate crisis is a Western problem," one Cathay diplomat remarked privately. "We send our condolences."
