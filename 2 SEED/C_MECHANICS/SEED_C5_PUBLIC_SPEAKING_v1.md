# Public Speaking & Communication Protocol
## Thucydides Trap SIM — SEED Specification
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-28

---

## Principle

Communication is FREE. The SIM does not regulate who talks to whom, when, or how. The value is in CAPTURING what was said and making it available as history/context — not in controlling it.

---

## Types of Communication

| Type | Visibility | Captured? | Engine Effect |
|------|:----------:|:---------:|:-------------:|
| **Corridor negotiation** | Parties only | No (private) | None — this is the game |
| **Organization meeting** (NATO, EU, BRICS+, OPEC+, UNGA) | Members present | Yes — by secretary/AI | Context for AI + analytics |
| **Formal public speech** | All participants | Yes — transcribed | Creates PUBLIC COMMITMENT |
| **Press conference** (optional, player-initiated) | All participants | Yes — transcribed | Creates PUBLIC COMMITMENT |
| **Moderator announcement** | All participants | Yes — automatic | World narrative |
| **AI participant communication** | Per interaction | Yes — automatic (all AI speech is transcribed by default) | Full context for AI cognitive model |

## Capture Protocol

### Human Speeches
A **secretary function** (human assistant or AI transcription) records all public speeches in real-time. Transcripts go to the event log with:
- Speaker (role_id)
- Round and phase
- Visibility: PUBLIC
- Full text

This is the KING model — proven to work.

### AI Participant Speeches
All AI communication is **automatically transcribed** — every conversation, every meeting statement, every negotiation. No additional capture needed.

### What's NOT Captured
Private corridor conversations between human participants are NOT recorded unless participants choose to make a public statement about what was discussed. This preserves the "back-channel" dynamic.

---

## Public Commitments

When a participant makes a **public speech**, the content becomes a PUBLIC COMMITMENT:
- Other participants (human and AI) can reference it
- AI participants receive the transcript as context and factor it into their decisions
- Breaking a public commitment has no ENGINE penalty — but has SOCIAL penalty (trust erosion, alliance damage, electoral vulnerability)
- The moderator may note broken commitments in the world narrative

This is intentional: commitments are enforced by the SOCIAL SYSTEM, not by the engine.

---

## Press Conference (Optional)

Any participant can request a "press conference" through the moderator:
- Moderator announces: "[Role] is making a public statement"
- All participants can listen (or choose not to)
- Transcribed and logged
- No cost, no mechanic — just a communication channel
- Useful for: diplomatic signals, ultimatums, alliance declarations, peace proposals

---

## What This Specification Does NOT Do

- Does NOT regulate private conversations
- Does NOT assign speaking slots or time limits (moderator manages pacing)
- Does NOT create engine mechanics for speech effects (no "speech boosts support by X%")
- Does NOT require participants to speak publicly
- The EFFECT of speech comes from human social dynamics, not formulas

---

## Integration with AI System

AI participants process public speeches as part of their cognitive Block 3 (Memory):
- Speech transcripts added to the AI's event memory
- AI evaluates: "Dealer publicly committed to defending Formosa — this constrains Columbia's options"
- AI can reference speeches in negotiations: "You said in the UNGA session that..."
- AI's own public statements are generated through the conversation engine and automatically logged

---

*This is intentionally the simplest specification in the SEED. Public speaking works best when it's free, captured, and consequential through social dynamics — not through mechanics.*
