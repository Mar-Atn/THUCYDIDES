Team Dynamics

Columbia: Overstretch Trajectory

GDP grows from $285.5T to $354.4T (+24%), but global commitments strain capacity. Succession dynamics (elections) create policy oscillation. Support never recovered from early war fatigue. The "arsenal of democracy" identity conflicts with economic pragmatism.

Cathay: Calculated Restraint

GDP surges from $207.5T to $304.1T (+47%). Naval ratio crosses parity in Round 2 and reaches 1.27. Helmsman's Formosa window score hits 0.92 but the threshold is never crossed — consistent with strategic patience. The regime prioritizes economic overtaking over military adventurism.

Nordostan: Paranoid Grind

Stability decays from 4.14 to 1.0 over 5 rounds. The autocratic paranoia profile drives aggressive deal-seeking (contributes to high deal counts) while simultaneously maintaining combat operations. Collapses but doesn't surrender — consistent with authoritarian persistence.

Heartland: Fastest Collapse

Hits stability 1.0 by Round 3 — the fastest collapse of any country. Initial stability of 3.58 was already low. The regime's war tiredness accumulates too aggressively. This is the clearest calibration signal: Heartland should sustain war effort longer given its autocratic resilience.

5 Design Conclusions & Next Steps

What Works

  • Power transition dynamics — GDP gap closes consistently across all 9 tests
  • Nuclear deterrence — 3-confirmation rule effective; zero use in 72 rounds
  • Gulf Gate ground-based blockade — holds across tests, creates genuine economic pressure
  • Economic interdependencies — tariffs/sanctions produce visible GDP effects
  • Role-brief-driven reasoning (LLM test) produces credible strategic behavior

What Needs Calibration

  • Stability decay too aggressive for war countries — Heartland hits 1.0 by Round 3 in LLM test. Recommend: slower war tiredness accumulation (0.2/round vs 0.4), higher autocracy/democracy resilience for wartime leaders
  • Heuristic test overrides don't differentiate — need fundamentally different decision logic per test scenario, not just parameter tweaks
  • Cathay stability too high (9.0 in heuristic tests) — needs downward pressure from economic slowdown, tech competition stress
  • Support mean-reversion too weak — Cathay at 70% and Bharata at 100% are unrealistic

What's Missing

  • Theater-level combat — tests use global hexes, not the new theater maps
  • Covert operations — not firing in most tests
  • Election mechanics impact — elections happen but don't visibly change country behavior
  • Internal team dynamics in heuristic tests — only LLM test models faction conflict

Recommended Next Tests

  • LLM test with recalibrated stability — slower decay, test if war countries survive to Round 6+
  • Theater-level test — focus on Eastern Ereb using 15-hex map
  • Gulf Gate breakout attempt — Columbia ground invasion scenario
  • Claude subagent test — actual Claude instances for each team (the ultimate validation)
