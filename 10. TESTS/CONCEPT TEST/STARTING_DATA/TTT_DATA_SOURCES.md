# TTT Starting Data -- Calibration Sources & Methodology
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-25

---

## Scale Convention

- **GDP:** 1 coin = ~$100B real GDP (2025 nominal). Columbia 280 = ~$28T, Cathay 190 = ~$19T, etc.
- **Military units:** Heavily abstracted. 1 ground unit = ~50-80K troops equivalent. 1 naval unit = ~1 carrier strike group or 4-6 major surface combatants. 1 tactical air = ~100 strike aircraft or equivalent drone/missile package. Strategic missiles = ICBM/SLBM launchers (dozens of warheads each). Air defense = major integrated AD system (THAAD/S-400 battery equivalent).
- **Treasury:** Starting reserves in coins, approximating sovereign wealth funds and foreign reserves divided by 100.

### Military Calibration
- **Columbia:** Based on US active military ~1.4M, 11 carrier strike groups, ~5,400 nuclear warheads, global base network. Largest defense budget ($900B+).
- **Cathay:** PLA 2M active, growing navy (3 carriers, 350+ ships), ~500 nuclear warheads growing rapidly, missile force expanding (+1/round represents ~100 new warheads/year).
- **Sarmatia:** ~1.1M active (many committed to Ruthenia war), degraded navy, ~5,500 nuclear warheads, war attrition reducing conventional capability.
- **Ruthenia:** ~900K mobilized, no navy, limited air (dependent on Western donations), no strategic weapons. High war fatigue.
- **Persia:** ~580K active, IRGC-dominated, small navy focused on Hormuz denial, significant missile/drone capability, 60% toward nuclear weapon.
- **European powers:** Small professional forces. Gallia/Albion nuclear. Teutonia rearming but from low base. Freeland investing heavily.
- **Bharata:** Large military (1.4M active), growing navy, nuclear L1, non-aligned posture.

### Political Calibration
- **Columbia stability 7:** Functioning institutions but political polarization. Dem/Rep split 47/53 reflects slight Republican advantage post-2024.
- **Cathay stability 6:** Economic slowdown creating strain, but party control intact. Support 55 = Helmsman's authority solid but not unquestioned.
- **Sarmatia stability 5:** War strain, inflation, but nationalist rally holding. Support 62 = autocratic rally effect still working, war fatigue growing.
- **Ruthenia stability 5:** War-torn but cohesive. Support 48 = Beacon losing popularity as war grinds on.
- **Persia stability 4:** Protest risk, economic hardship, generational divide. Support 40 = regime under pressure.

### Technology Calibration
- **Nuclear levels:** Based on warhead counts and delivery capability. L3 = thousands of warheads with global delivery (US, Russia). L2 = hundreds with regional/limited global delivery (China, UK, France). L1 = basic arsenal (India, Israel, North Korea).
- **AI levels:** Based on AI industry capability, semiconductor access, research output. L3 = frontier models + semiconductor design (US, Japan). L2 = advanced AI industry (China, UK, Germany, India, Israel, S. Korea, Taiwan). L1 = basic capability. L0 = none (North Korea, Cuba/Venezuela).
- **Cathay nuclear progress 80%:** China's nuclear buildup is the fastest in history; could reach L3 equivalence by 2028-2029.
- **Cathay AI progress 70%:** Despite US export controls, China's AI capabilities are advancing rapidly through domestic chip design and open-source models.
- **Columbia AI progress 60% toward L4:** AGI-adjacent breakthrough is the most speculative data point. Reflects OpenAI/Anthropic/Google progress trajectory.
- **Persia nuclear progress 60% toward L1:** Iran's 60% enrichment capability puts it months from a weapon if decision is made.

### Economic Conditions
- **Inflation:** Phrygia 45% and Caribe 60% reflect real hyperinflation. Persia 25% reflects sanctions impact. Sarmatia 8.5% reflects wartime monetary expansion.
- **Ponte debt burden 8:** Italy's 140%+ debt-to-GDP ratio is the highest starting debt burden in the simulation.
- **Trade balances:** Cathay +8 (massive exporter), Columbia -12 (structural deficit), Solaria +8 (oil exports), Teutonia +5 (industrial exports).
- **Formosa dependency:** Represents semiconductor supply chain exposure. Columbia 0.65 and Cathay 0.55 are most exposed. Formosa itself 0.0 (it IS the source).

### Sanctions/Tariffs Baseline
- **Western sanctions on Sarmatia L3:** Most comprehensive sanctions regime since WWII, imposed 2022+.
- **Columbia tariffs on Cathay L3:** Reflects Trump-era tariffs maintained and escalated through 2025-2026.
- **Cathay retaliatory tariffs L2:** Proportional but asymmetric retaliation.
- **EU tariffs on Cathay L1:** More moderate than US, reflecting EU's greater trade exposure.
- **Sanctions on Persia L2:** Reimposed US sanctions, partial EU compliance.
- **Sanctions on Choson L3:** Maximum international isolation.

### Starting War Conditions
- **Sarmatia-Ruthenia war:** Based on Russia-Ukraine conflict status as of early 2026. Sarmatia occupies eastern front zones (Donbas equivalent), southern territory (land bridge), and Crimea. Ruthenia holds the capital, western regions, and contests the northern front (Kharkiv axis). Both sides experiencing attrition.

## Data Limitations & Design Choices

1. **GDP coin scale loses precision below $50B.** Choson at 0.3 coins and Caribe at 2 coins are rough approximations. For gameplay, their economic irrelevance IS the point.
2. **Military unit abstraction loses force quality differences.** A Sarmatia ground unit is not equivalent to a Columbia ground unit in reality, but the sim handles this through production cost differentials and maintenance costs rather than unit quality modifiers.
3. **Formosa dependency is a simplification.** Real semiconductor dependencies are sector-specific (advanced chips vs. commodity chips). The single number represents overall exposure to a Formosa disruption scenario.
4. **Political support numbers are calibrated for gameplay tension, not polling accuracy.** The goal is to put most major leaders in the "strained but viable" zone where decisions matter.
5. **Oil producer status is binary** in this model. Real OPEC+ has dramatically different production capacities. The Cartel mechanic handles this through the production level decision (low/normal/high) and AI-calculated price effects.
