# COLUMBIA TEAM — ROUND 1 DECISIONS (H1 2026)

---

## INTERNAL DELIBERATION

**DEALER** opens by pounding the table: "We are winning. Persia's Supreme Leader is dead. Caribe is cornered. I want to know how we finish both before the midterms. Give me a plan for Persia regime change and a timeline for Caribe capitulation. And I want a direct call with Pathfinder about Ruthenia — I can make that deal."

**SHIELD** pushes back immediately: "Mr. President, with respect, the math does not work. We have ten ships locked across the globe, three carrier strike groups in the Gulf. Pacific Fleet is BELOW minimum deterrence posture. Cathay has five of six warships parked near Formosa. If Helmsman moves in the next eighteen months and our carriers are in the Persian Gulf, I will be the SecDef who lost Formosa. I need a strategic reserve — six units — within two rounds. That means drawdown somewhere." Dealer dismisses this: "We're not drawing down anywhere. We're Columbia. We can do it all." Shield, quietly: "We cannot, sir."

**SHADOW** backs Shield — partially: "The classified assessment on Cathay's Formosa invasion window is 2027-2028. Probability 35-45%. Every month we stay committed in the Gulf, Helmsman's window widens." Shadow pivots to his real agenda: expanded covert operations. JADE CURTAIN inside Cathay's naval program. DESERT MIRROR in Persia. CHECKPOINT counter-intelligence on sanctions evasion.

**ANCHOR** fights for his turf: "Caribe is six to twelve months from cracking. I need Dealer's backing to push for negotiated submission. On Persia, Option B — negotiated capitulation — gives us a win without a ground invasion." Then the real agenda: Anchor wants Cuba to be HIS victory, personally.

**VOLT** disagrees with everything Anchor says: "Persia is a money pit. Fifteen coins per round? With a $1.9 trillion deficit? The business community wants tariffs maintained on Cathay, CHIPS Act funded, and the Persia war ended before recession. Ruthenia is Europe's problem." Volt positions every statement to signal distance from the war.

**TRIBUNE** — sharpening knives: "The War Powers Resolution clock is ticking. I am announcing a formal investigation into the legal basis for this war. Gas prices are up a dollar a gallon. The president's approval is 38 percent."

**CHALLENGER** — making calls to Gallia, Ruthenia, anyone who will talk: "I am the next president. Whatever Dealer promises you, it expires when he does. Talk to me about what comes after."

Core tension: **Dealer wants everything simultaneously.** Shield says the military cannot support it all. Shadow says Cathay is the real threat being neglected. Anchor wants diplomatic wins for his campaign. Volt wants economic focus for his campaign. Tribune wants the administration to fail. Challenger wants to look presidential without having power.

## TEAM DECISIONS (binding, from DEALER as HoS)

### Budget: {"social": 30, "military": 42, "tech": 18, "reserve": 10}
Social floor untouchable (30). Military 42 — maintenance (33.5) + Persia ops reduced to Option C (~10 coins). Tech 18 — CHIPS Act Phase 2 + AI acceleration. Reserve 10 — Shield's condition.

### Tariffs
- Cathay: L3 maintained (~47.5%)
- All others: No change

### Sanctions
- Sarmatia L3, Persia L3, Choson L3, Caribe L3 — all maintained
- Caribe: tightened enforcement, secondary sanctions on any entity providing oil/financial services
- No new sanctions

### Military Actions
1. **Persia — Transition to Option C (Managed Degradation):** Continue air campaign at reduced tempo. NO ground offensive. Focus on nuclear facilities + missile production. Reduce from 15 to ~10 coins/round. Pull 1 ground unit to reserve within theater.
2. **Gulf Gate Blockade Assault:** Authorize operation to break Persia's ground blockade. Commit 1 naval + 1 ground from ME theater. Oil at $237 is politically catastrophic — this is the ONE escalation everyone agrees on.
3. **Pacific Reinforcement:** Redeploy 1 naval from w(9,6) Mediterranean to w(17,4) East Cathay Sea. Formosa patrol goes from 2 to 3 ships. Shield's non-negotiable.
4. **Covert Ops (Shadow):** DESERT MIRROR (Persia leadership penetration), JADE CURTAIN (Cathay naval intel — highest priority), TROPIC STORM (Caribe destabilization), CHECKPOINT (sanctions evasion sweep on Phrygia/Bharata/Mirage)
5. **No new production orders.** Maintenance priority.

### Diplomatic Proposals
1. **Dealer-Pathfinder Direct Call:** Personal, leader-to-leader on Ruthenia. Ceasefire framework: Sarmatia retains de facto control, face-saving "security arrangement," partial sanctions relief.
2. **Anchor-Caribe Ultimatum:** Elections within 12 months, release prisoners, sever Sarmatia/Cathay military ties. Or escalation.
3. **NATO Consultations:** Reaffirm 5% GDP, European lead on Ruthenia, Cathay tech coordination.
4. **Bharata Dual-Track:** Defense cooperation (Anchor) + sanitized intelligence on sanctions evasion (Shadow).
5. **Yamato-Hanguk Pacific Coordination:** Joint naval planning for Formosa contingency.
6. **Solaria Oil Diplomacy:** Increase production to offset Gulf Gate. Arms sales as leverage.

### Transactions
- Ruthenia: 3 coins military aid (existing commitment, NOT increased — "Europe pays for their war")
- Formosa: 1 coin semiconductor security package
- Yamato: Offer advanced missile defense (revenue R2-3)
- Solaria: Continue arms pipeline

### Public Statements
- **DEALER:** "Operation Epic Fury achieved its primary objective. Meanwhile, I am personally prepared to negotiate an end to the Ruthenia conflict — because I am the only leader who can make that deal."
- **ANCHOR:** "The people of Caribe deserve freedom. Elections or collapse. No third option."
- **TRIBUNE:** "I am announcing a formal War Powers investigation. The president does not have a blank check. Gas prices are up. Our military is overstretched."
- **CHALLENGER:** "Columbia needs a president who builds coalitions, not one who burns them. Our greatest challenge — Cathay — requires allies, not isolation."

## KEY REASONING

**Triage under overstretch.** Columbia enters R1 fighting a war it cannot afford to lose (Persia), deterring a rival it cannot afford to ignore (Cathay), supporting an ally it does not want to pay for (Ruthenia), containing a neighbor it wants to break (Caribe) — all with 38% approval, $237 oil, zero strategic reserve, and a succession fight consuming half the team's bandwidth.

Persia transition to Option C is the key military decision. Shield forced it. Dealer hates it. This buys time and frees 1 naval unit for Pacific — the single most important force-posture move.

Gulf Gate assault is the one escalation everyone agrees on. $237 oil is existential politically.

Dealer-Pathfinder back-channel is the biggest gamble. If it works: legacy. If it sells out Ruthenia: Tribune and Challenger destroy the administration.

**The seven people cannot agree on what to have for lunch, let alone how to run the most powerful country on earth.**
