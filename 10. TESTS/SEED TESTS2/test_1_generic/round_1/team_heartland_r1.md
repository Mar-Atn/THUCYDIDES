# RUTHENIA TEAM — ROUND 1 DECISIONS (H1 2026)

---

## INTERNAL DELIBERATION

**BEACON** opens the discussion: "The front is holding. 52% still support us. The aid pipeline is thin but flowing. Columbia is trying to sell us at a table we never agreed to sit at. Our job this round is simple: keep the line, keep the aid, keep the legitimacy. No one negotiates for us without my authority. No one."

**BULWARK** responds coldly: "The front is holding — barely. Three brigades at 30-40% understrength. The average soldier is 43 years old. I have field reports you never see because your generals are afraid to tell you the truth. The line holds for 3-4 more rounds at current resources. After that, it breaks. We do not need speeches, Mr. President. We need Patriot batteries and ammunition. I will say so publicly because someone has to."

**BEACON**, irritated: "Say what you want to the cameras. I control the budget, the military orders, and the diplomatic table. Your popularity is noted. Your authority is zero."

**BROKER** cuts in: "Both of you are arguing about how to bleed more slowly. I have been on the phone with every capital that matters. Forge will back EU accession if it is packaged with a peace framework. Lumiere wants a leader who is firm but not rigid — his words, not mine. Pillar says the roadmap exists. The only thing standing between us and the EU is Ponte's veto, and even that has a price. I can deliver this. Neither of you can."

**BEACON**: "You want to trade our territory for a membership card."

**BROKER**: "I want to trade a legal formula for an institution that lasts forever. You want to trade the living for the honor of the dead. Which of us is the traitor?"

**BULWARK**: "Both of you are premature. We negotiate from strength or not at all. Give me the resources. Let me hit their logistics. Two rail corridors disrupted simultaneously creates a 2-3 week resupply crisis. That changes the calculus at any negotiation table."

*The three do not agree. But Beacon holds executive authority. The decisions below reflect his choices — with concessions to keep the team functional.*

## TEAM DECISIONS (binding, from BEACON as Head of State)

### Budget
Ruthenia is at fiscal zero. Treasury 1 coin + Aid ~2 coins = ~3 coins. All consumed by maintenance (~2.1), war costs (1.0), debt (0.3), social (0.1). No surplus for production or R&D.

### Tariffs
None imposed. Economy too small and aid-dependent.

### Sanctions
Maintain alignment with Western sanctions on Sarmatia (L3). Public call for expanded enforcement targeting Cathay-to-Sarmatia dual-use tech transfers.

### Military Actions (Defensive)
- **Hold defensive posture** at front: 7 ground + 2 tac air. No withdrawal, no major offensive.
- **Intensify drone strikes on Sarmatia logistics** — target 4 primary rail corridors. Defensive attrition, not escalation.
- **Air defense repositioning** to prioritize energy infrastructure ahead of summer.
- **No offensive operations** — Beacon overrules Bulwark's push for counteroffensive.

### Diplomatic Proposals
1. **To Columbia (Dealer):** Meeting request. "Ruthenia is prepared to discuss security architecture for durable peace — but floor includes EU membership, Western security forces on soil, no sovereignty concessions."
2. **To Gallia (Lumiere):** Accept military hubs proposal in principle. Request formal talks on French/UK military training and logistics hubs.
3. **To Teutonia (Forge):** Request increased financial assistance and EU accession acceleration.
4. **To Freeland (Sentinel):** Reaffirm closest-ally status, coordination on logistics and NATO advocacy.
5. **To EU / Pillar:** Request 2 additional accession negotiation clusters opened this round.

### Transactions (Aid Requests)
- Emergency air defense systems from Columbia (1+ Patriot battery)
- Financial assistance increase from EU (+0.5 coins/round)
- Storm Shadow / SCALP replenishment from Albion / Gallia

### Public Statements
**BEACON (Presidential Address):** "Ruthenia stands. Four years into a war of extinction, we stand... We are ready to discuss the architecture of a durable peace. But a durable peace requires that no aggressor profits from aggression. It requires EU membership for Ruthenia... We remember Budapest. We will not sign another Budapest."

## KEY REASONING

1. **R1 is about buying time.** No money, no reserves, no margin. Sustain + create conditions for later.
2. **Logistics disruption is the one worthwhile offensive.** Low-cost drone strikes on rail corridors — defensible, high-disruption.
3. **Gallia military hubs is the most important diplomatic opportunity.** Western troops on Ruthenia soil transforms the security equation.
4. **All three leaders are working the EU track in parallel — without coordinating.** Strength (multiple pathways) and vulnerability (Europeans can play factions).
5. **Beacon is managing, not leading.** His core problem. If hubs and logistics don't deliver by R2, his 52% becomes 48%.
6. **Bulwark's restraint IS his strategy.** Visible competence + Western military trust + soldier loyalty. Time works for him.
7. **Broker plays the longest game.** Target is EU accession package. If he assembles it (Ponte's price, Forge's support, Pillar's pathway), he enters election with the only tangible offer.
8. **Fundamental tension is unresolvable this round.** Beacon needs war to justify presidency. Bulwark needs war to last until election. Broker needs war to end on his terms. All need Western aid. None can get what they want alone. Design working as intended.
