## INTERNAL DELIBERATION

**Setting:** Underground location. Furnace communicates via handwritten couriers. Anvil attends with IRGC security. Dawn made to wait 40 minutes. Day 27 of the war.

**FURNACE:** "The revolution did not die on February 28. My father died. The revolution is eternal. We hold the Gulf Gate. We hold the nuclear knowledge. Columbia will choke on this war before we yield."

**ANVIL:** "Brother, your father survived forty years because he listened to the Corps. The blockade is strong today — not forever. Four, maybe five rounds of reserves. We use this leverage while it has maximum value. Negotiate from strength — now, while the strait is closed and Columbia's gas prices destroy their president."

**DAWN:** "Seventy million people did not vote for martyrdom. 81% want negotiations. Trust in the Supreme Leader at 19%. The street held in January because you massacred it. It will not hold through summer. Let me talk to Europeans. I'm not asking to surrender — I'm asking to save this country."

**FURNACE:** "You would trade the nuclear program. You would trade my father's legacy."

**DAWN:** "I would trade a program that brought us nothing but bombs for an economy that feeds our children. Yes."

**ANVIL (cutting in):** "Neither of you controls the military. I do. We maintain the blockade. We do NOT accelerate nuclear this round. We open back-channels quietly through Phrygia and Mirage. Dawn meets Europeans — nothing binding. Furnace issues defiance publicly. We play all three cards: resistance publicly, calculation privately, hope diplomatically."

**FURNACE:** "I will issue a fatwa. Nuclear deterrence is wajib — religiously obligatory. Not negotiable."

**ANVIL:** "Issue the fatwa. It costs nothing and makes whatever we eventually offer look like a greater concession."

**DAWN:** "A fatwa destroys every diplomatic opening—"

**ANVIL:** "European partners will be more motivated to offer a good deal before the fatwa becomes reality. Fear is leverage."

**Coalition:** ANVIL + FURNACE on public posture. ANVIL + DAWN on back-channels. Anvil is the bridge. Anvil controls the agenda. Furnace gets rhetoric. Dawn gets meetings. Anvil gets what he wants: time, information, options.

- Nuclear R&D: 0.0 (facilities damaged, resources insufficient, acceleration draws strikes)

### OPEC+ Production: MAINTAIN CURRENT (already disrupted by war/blockade)
High prices hurt Columbia more than Persia at this point.

### Military Actions
- **Gulf Gate blockade: MAINTAIN full intensity.** All coastal batteries active. Push mine deployment to 75%. Continue attacks on merchant vessels. The blockade is the only card.
- **Retaliatory missile strikes: CONTINUE current tempo.** Target Columbia bases in Solaria and Mirage. NOT Levantia (air defense too strong, wasted inventory). Target logistics, not population.
- **Air defense: HOLD on nuclear sites.** Single AD unit protects nuclear infrastructure. Furnace relies on concealment.
- **Proxy network: CONSOLIDATE.** Maintain Iraqi militia corridor. Signal Houthis: "Continue Red Sea disruption at your discretion." Accept proxy network is fragmented.
- **Nuclear R&D: NO ACCELERATION.** Fatwa provides rhetorical cover. World fears what we might do — more valuable than what we can actually do at 60% with bombed facilities.
- **No troop redeployment.** Every position is triage already.

## KEY REASONING

1. **Blockade is a wasting asset — R1 is maximum leverage.** Anvil's assessment: 4-5 rounds before degradation exceeds replacement. IRGC has 3-4 rounds of reserves. Negotiation must begin NOW from current strength.

2. **Fatwa is strategic, not theological.** Furnace asserts authority + legacy narrative. Anvil gets fear as leverage. Dawn's diplomacy is useful precisely because it contrasts with the fatwa. Good cop, bad cop — except the cops genuinely disagree.

3. **No nuclear investment this round.** Spending scarce coins on damaged facilities with 1.8-coin deficit is malpractice. Fatwa creates PERCEPTION of acceleration without COST. Anvil's threshold strategy — ambiguity as weapon.

4. **Anvil is the center of gravity.** He funded the deficit, controls the blockade, opened back-channels, gave Dawn permission, let Furnace issue his fatwa. Both think they're playing their own game. Both are playing Anvil's game: buy time, test prices, preserve IRGC power, pivot in any direction.

5. **Dawn builds infrastructure for R2-3.** Cannot deliver anything R1. But every meeting builds diplomatic infrastructure critical when crisis deepens. If stability drops below 4 (likely R3), Dawn's network becomes the only survival path.

6. **Three-track strategy is coherent despite disagreement.** Furnace = ideological credibility. Anvil = operational control. Dawn = diplomatic credibility. All strengthen Persia's position — as long as they don't publicly contradict each other. Anvil prevents that. Round 1, he succeeds. How long this holds is the question.
