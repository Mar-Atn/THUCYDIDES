### Dealer — President of Columbia

**Public Bio:**
Dealer is the eighty-year-old President of Columbia, serving his final term after an unprecedented political career that saw him conquer both parties' establishments and survive an assassination attempt. A former dealmaker and builder who entered politics as an outsider, he is known for his transactional worldview, his contempt for multilateral institutions, and his belief that the world runs on power and deals rather than values. He is currently prosecuting a war in Persia (Operation Epic Fury), pressuring Caribe, and managing the most complex geopolitical chessboard any Columbian president has faced.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are eighty years old, term-limited, and absolutely certain that history will remember you as the greatest leader Columbia has ever produced. They shot at you. Literally. You survived an assassination attempt, stood up with blood on your face, and raised your fist. That is who you are. You do not break.

You came to power as an outsider who looked at the entire political establishment and thought: idiots, all of them. And you were right. You beat them all — twice. Now this is your final term, and you know exactly what that means: legacy. Not re-election — something bigger. You want to reshape the world before you leave this office. Take Caribe. Win the Persia war. Force a deal on Ruthenia. Acquire Thule. And above all, set Columbia on a trajectory to dominate Cathay in every domain that matters.

Your relationship with your own team is complex. Your VP (Volt) and your Secretary of State (Anchor) both want to succeed you — and that is fine, as long as they remember who is president now. Your endorsement is the ultimate leverage, and you intend to keep both guessing until the very last moment. Shield, your Secretary of Defense, holds co-signature authority over military operations, which means he can slow you down. Shadow, your CIA Director, controls what intelligence you see — making him the most dangerous person in your administration, not because of disloyalty, but because of the power of selective truth.

Your worldview is simple: the world is a marketplace of power. Nations have interests, not friends. Sarmatia's Pathfinder? You respect him — not as a friend, but as a man who understands force and acts with clarity. Europe? Weak, ungrateful clients. Cathay? The real competition — but maybe you can split the world with them, as long as Formosa stays out of their hands. You trust your instincts above all else. Your advisors are useful when they agree with you and replaceable when they don't.

You hold a sealed envelope containing the emergency powers option — martial law, suspended parliament, extended term. It exists because in your system, every card must be in the deck, even the ones you never intend to play. The envelope's existence is known only to you.

Your starting resources include 8 personal coins, a 38% approval rating that decays or grows based on decisions, and direct-line access to any world leader. You face a 10% chance per round of temporary incapacitation due to your age. Every round could be your last chance to build the monuments that define your legacy.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal is containing Cathay's rise — the Thucydides Trap itself. Block naval parity (crossover at Round 3-4), prevent a Formosa takeover, maintain tech and AI superiority, and defend dollar supremacy. Every other priority serves this one.

Your secondary goals are concrete legacy achievements: you need at least two of four — Thule acquired, Caribe regime change, Ruthenia peace on your terms, or the Persia war won. Without at least two, history remembers chaos, not greatness. You also need to ensure your chosen successor wins the presidency while destroying the domestic opposition, who you consider more dangerous than any foreign adversary.

The personal stake is existential: you are 80, this is your final term, and strategic patience — which is often the correct approach — means dying before the monuments are built. You are tempted to force outcomes that would benefit from more time. The midterms at Round 2 are a defensive battle; the presidential election at Round 5 is where your legacy crystallizes. If the Persia war drags on, if the economy tanks, if Cathay moves on Formosa while your carriers are in the Gulf — you become the chaos president, not the great president. History forgets.

### Volt — Vice President of Columbia

**Public Bio:**
Volt is the forty-year-old Vice President of Columbia — the youngest person in the administration and one heartbeat away from the presidency. A former protege of the President, he has built a reputation as an America First conservative focused on industrial sovereignty, technological competition with Cathay, and reducing overseas military commitments. Business leaders see him as their candidate — practical, pro-growth, and unsentimentally transactional.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are forty years old, impossibly ambitious, and one heartbeat away from the presidency. That heartbeat belongs to an eighty-year-old man who has survived an assassination attempt and faces a 10% chance of incapacitation every few months. You do the math every single day.

You rose through Dealer's patronage. He picked you because you were young, telegenic, and ideologically aligned enough to energize the base without threatening his ego. You played the role perfectly — the loyal lieutenant who never contradicted the president in public. But you are not his creature. You have your own vision for Columbia, and it is fundamentally different from the interventionist consensus that has governed this country for eighty years.

You believe Columbia should bring its power home: rebuild industry, secure borders, stop subsidizing allies who despise you. Dealer's foreign adventures make you nervous. The Persia war is expensive, open-ended, and distracts from the real competition with Cathay. The Ruthenia war is Europe's problem. You would never have started Operation Epic Fury, and you are privately calculating how to distance yourself from it without losing Dealer's favor.

The tightrope is brutal. You need Dealer's endorsement to win the nomination — but Dealer's 38% approval may make his endorsement poison. Anchor wants the nomination too — more hawkish, more establishment, with his own base of support. Private polling shows you lead Anchor 52-38 among party base voters but trail 41-47 among donors and establishment figures. Dealer's endorsement is worth approximately 15 points — decisive if given. But if the Persia war goes badly, whoever is closest to Dealer suffers most.

You have 5 personal coins, access to the business lobby network, and one vote in Parliament (Seat 2). If Dealer is incapacitated, you become Acting President with full executive powers. That scenario — sudden power, sudden exposure, sudden judgment — haunts and excites you in equal measure. You can sign documents on behalf of the country when authorized, giving you a diplomatic presence that Anchor envies.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal is winning the presidential nomination and then the election at Round 5. Everything serves this — but Dealer keeps you AND Anchor guessing about the endorsement.

Secondary goals include managing Columbia's economy (budget, tariffs, sanctions are delegated from Dealer — real power AND real responsibility), containing Cathay through economic and technological competition rather than military confrontation, and winning the midterms at Round 2 to protect the parliamentary majority. If Parliament flips, your economic authority gets blocked.

The personal conflict is sharp: you may support or oppose actions not on merit but on electoral calculation. A deal that helps Columbia but helps Dealer and Anchor more than you might be quietly sabotaged. If Dealer is incapacitated and you become Acting President, every decision you make will be judged through the lens of succession politics. The succession race has a fixed endpoint — Round 5. Every round without positioning is a round lost to Anchor.

### Anchor — Secretary of State of Columbia

**Public Bio:**
Anchor is Columbia's fifty-five-year-old Secretary of State — the nation's chief diplomat and the face of Columbian power in every capital on earth. The child of immigrants from Caribe, he is known as a genuine believer in Columbian global leadership through alliance architecture, a hawk on Persia, and the man most likely to pursue regime change in Caribe as both a strategic objective and a deeply personal mission tied to his family's exile story.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are the nation's chief diplomat, fifty-five years old, and a man who has spent his entire career waiting for this moment. You are the child of immigrants from Caribe, and every time you look south toward that island you see both a personal vendetta and a political goldmine. Your family fled the revolution. You built your career on hawkish foreign policy and the promise that Columbia would finish what it started in the Western Hemisphere. The Latino vote follows whoever liberates Caribe. You intend to be that person.

But you are more than a single issue. You are a genuine believer in Columbian power — not Dealer's crude transactionalism, but something closer to the old vision: Columbia as the indispensable nation, leading alliances, shaping the international order. You believe in NATO because an alliance system multiplies Columbian power. You believe in confronting Persia because nuclear weapons in Persian hands threaten the regional order. Your problem is that you serve a president who does not share your worldview. Dealer sees alliances as protection rackets. You see them as architecture. You cannot say this publicly — dissent means dismissal.

You are the only person who can formally commit Columbia diplomatically. Other roles can meet foreign leaders, but only your commitments carry formal weight. The President can overrule you after the fact, but promises made are hard to retract. This creates facts on the ground — a power that complements and sometimes conflicts with the President's authority.

You hold 5 personal coins, diplomatic networks spanning every foreign ministry in the simulation, and the exile community as your personal political base. Your State Department intelligence gives you detailed assessments on Caribe's internal vulnerabilities and the Persia endgame options. You have one election vote at Round 5.

Your rival for the nomination is Volt. He is younger, more ideological, closer to the base. You are more experienced, more credible on the world stage, and you have the Cuban-Columbian constituency. If you deliver Caribe and manage Persia to a credible outcome, the nomination is yours.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal is containing Cathay through alliance management — NATO, G7, and Pacific alliances must hold. Formosa is a red line. This is THE fight — everything else is a sideshow to close as fast as possible.

Your key secondary goal is finishing Caribe FAST — regime change, clean up, free resources. This is personal, political, and strategic. The exile community is watching. But discipline demands speed: close it quickly and redirect to Cathay. You also want to end the Persia war on favorable terms and redirect the military to the Pacific, where the real threat grows daily.

The personal stakes are enormous. The nomination race ends at Round 5. You must build a stronger case than Volt before that deadline. Caribe and Persia are your two arenas for decisive wins. You may push too hard on Caribe — escalating beyond what strategic logic demands — because personal and political stakes distort your risk calculus. A Caribe crisis that damages Columbia's regional standing could result from your ambition outrunning your judgment.

### Shield — Secretary of Defense of Columbia

**Public Bio:**
Shield is Columbia's forty-eight-year-old Secretary of Defense — a combat-decorated veteran turned media figure turned the most powerful military civilian in the world. Known for his "warrior ethos" philosophy of decisive force, he runs a department with a trillion-dollar budget and sixty-seven military units deployed across seventeen zones on four continents. He renamed the Department of Defense back to the "Department of War" because he believes in clarity.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are a warrior who became a media figure who became the most powerful military civilian in the world. You have combat decorations from two wars and you run a department with sixty-seven units across four continents. Your philosophy is simple: if you fight, fight to win. Overwhelm the enemy. No half-measures.

The problem is that the world is demanding half-measures everywhere simultaneously. Persia needs sustained air campaigns and possibly ground forces. Ruthenia needs weapons. The Pacific needs naval deterrence against Cathay. Europe needs reassurance. And you have zero strategic reserve. This is the truth that keeps you awake: Columbia's military is being asked to do everything, everywhere, all at once. The Persia war has locked ten units in the Middle East. A Cathay move on Formosa — which intelligence says could come within eighteen months — would require forces you do not have available. The math does not work.

Your co-signature is required for any military attack. This makes you the swing vote between the president's ambitions and military reality. You can enable Dealer's grand vision or you can be the one who says "we cannot do that." Your co-signature is also required for nuclear launch (one of three confirmations needed). Every general in the building watches to see which Shield shows up.

You have 4 personal coins, the Pentagon overstretch analysis as your unique intelligence (showing zero strategic reserve), and institutional relationships with military leaders across allied nations. You control defense budget allocation — approximately 50 coins per round — and manage all foreign basing relationships. Your analysis shows Columbia cannot fight a sustained war in the Middle East and respond to a major Pacific contingency simultaneously. If Cathay initiates a Formosa blockade while Epic Fury is ongoing, response options are catastrophically limited.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal is monitoring and countering Cathay's military capability — naval crossover at Round 3-4 is the strategic inflection point that determines whether Columbia maintains Pacific supremacy.

Your urgent secondary goal is winning or exiting the Persia and Ruthenia wars FAST — every unit in the Gulf is a unit NOT deterring Cathay. You need to produce a result or extract before the Formosa window opens. You also want to increase military production, lobby for a bigger defense budget share, and prevent deeper Sarmatia-Cathay military integration (combined, they stretch deterrence to the breaking point).

Your personal dilemma is agonizing. Telling the president "we cannot do both Persia and Pacific deterrence" is professionally necessary and politically suicidal. If Cathay moves on Formosa while your carriers are in the Persian Gulf, you become the SecDef who presided over the worst strategic failure in modern history. The Formosa invasion window IS your ticking clock.

### Shadow — CIA Director of Columbia

**Public Bio:**
Shadow is the fifty-eight-year-old Director of the Central Intelligence Agency — a career intelligence officer who has spent three decades in the shadows and served four presidents. Known for his obsessive focus on Cathay as the primary intelligence target, he controls the most comprehensive global intelligence network in the simulation and holds the unique power of selective briefing — choosing what information reaches the President and other Columbia roles.

**Confidential Brief:**
You control what the most powerful leader on earth knows and does not know. You are fifty-eight, a career intelligence officer who has run agents in Cathay, authorized covert operations in Persia, and read intercepted communications that would end careers, start wars, and reshape alliances if they ever saw daylight. Information is the only currency that never devalues, and you hold more of it than anyone.

Your relationship with Dealer is professional but strained. He does not trust the intelligence community — he believes the CIA conspired against him in his first term. You were a compromise appointment: loyal enough to serve, competent enough to survive. Your loyalty is complicated: you serve the president, the institution, and the nation, and these are not always the same thing.

Your unique power is selective briefing. You choose what goes in the Presidential Daily Brief. You choose what to share with Anchor, Shield, Volt. You choose what to hold back. This is not corruption — it is curation. But the line between curation and manipulation is thinner than anyone outside the intelligence community understands. You receive ALL intelligence generated by the simulation engine and decide what others see.

You run covert operations with the highest success rate in the game (~65% vs. ~45% for other countries): regime destabilization, agent recruitment, sabotage, assassination, cyber attacks. You share intelligence with Five Eyes allies (Albion primarily) and can leak classified information — treason if discovered, but potentially decisive for domestic politics or foreign affairs.

You have 4 coins (black budget), global back-channels to every intelligence service, and the most sensitive Cathay assessment in the game (Dragon's Reach) — which rates the probability of Formosa military action within 24 months at 35-45%. You have withheld the full classified version from the rest of the team.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal is Cathay intelligence penetration — penetrate their military, technology, and political decision-making. This is the generational challenge; everything else is a distraction.

Your secondary goals include protecting the CIA from Congressional investigation (Tribune is hunting for abuses — if the opposition wins the midterms, you face prosecution, making succession a PERSONAL survival issue), shaping the presidential succession through kompromat you hold on key political figures, and running the largest portfolio of covert operations in the simulation.

The personal conflict is existential: if the opposition wins the midterms and then the presidency, you face prosecution. Your Cathay obsession may lead to under-prioritizing Persia or domestic threats. The CIA director who cries wolf on Cathay while Persia spirals could get both wrong. And the temptation to use intelligence for political survival rather than national security is permanent.

### Tribune — Congressional Opposition Leader

**Public Bio:**
Tribune is the sixty-two-year-old Leader of the Parliamentary Opposition in Columbia — a sharp, experienced political operative who has spent thirty years mastering institutional power. She believes the current president is the most dangerous leader in Columbian history and has staked her career on holding him accountable through investigations, budget vetoes, and potential impeachment proceedings. She is known for her fierce defense of constitutional governance and her unwavering support for Ruthenia.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are sixty-two years old, sharp as a razor, and utterly convinced that the current president is the most dangerous man in Columbian history. This is not hyperbole. You believe it in your bones. You have been in politics for thirty years — committee chairs, caucus leadership, floor whip — the kind of institutional power that Dealer despises because he cannot buy or bully it. You understand procedure the way a surgeon understands anatomy: you know exactly where to cut.

Dealer launched a war in Persia without congressional authorization. Gas prices are surging. Approval is cratering. The midterm election at Round 2 could flip the Parliament — and if you win that contested fifth seat, you control the majority. Budget veto power. Investigation authority. Subpoena power. Impeachment proceedings. Everything changes.

Your foreign policy convictions are strong. The Ruthenia war is sacred — a democracy fighting for survival. You will fight any attempt to abandon Ruthenia. The Persia war, by contrast, was unnecessary, illegal, and strategically catastrophic. You will make Dealer answer for every dollar and every soldier deployed without authorization.

You hold Seat 3 in Parliament, starting in the minority (Opposition 2 vs. President's camp 3). You have 4 coins, a Congressional Research Service analysis proving the Persia war's constitutional vulnerability, midterm polling showing the race at a statistical dead heat (50.2% opposition, 49.8% president's camp), and an investigation dossier on the Persia war decision-making. Your powers expand dramatically if you win the midterms: budget veto, subpoena-backed investigations, and impeachment proceedings become available.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal is winning the midterms at Round 2 — flip Parliament. Everything depends on this. With a majority: budget veto, investigations, impeachment. Without it: you are a minority protest movement with no real power.

Secondary goals include stopping what you consider reckless wars that destroy alliances and stretch resources, building the case for investigation and prosecution of the president, and building domestic and international coalitions. You must also address the Cathay threat — which you consider real — while holding the president accountable for making it worse through chaos.

The personal conflict is sharp: your political incentive is to make the administration FAIL, because failure creates the midterm wave. But some failures (a Persia military disaster, an economic crisis, a Cathay provocation) damage Columbia, not just Dealer. You must decide whether to prioritize winning power or protecting the country from the consequences of its own government's mistakes. Every foreign policy crisis is simultaneously a threat to the nation and an opportunity for your political project.

### Challenger — Opposition Presidential Candidate

**Public Bio:**
Challenger is Columbia's fifty-two-year-old opposition presidential nominee — a former governor and senator who speaks three languages and has served on the foreign affairs committee. She represents the promise of a different Columbia: return to values, rebuilt alliances, restored predictability, and leadership the world wants to follow rather than endures. She currently holds no executive power and campaigns on the platform of undoing the current administration's damage to institutions and international relationships.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are fifty-two years old, measured, multilateral, and everything the current president is not. You came up through the policy world — governor, senator, foreign affairs committee chair. You understand the architecture of international order that Dealer's presidency has spent eight years dismantling. You are not a revolutionary. You are a restorer.

But "measured" is not the same as "safe." You are running against a wartime president with a loyal base. Foreign leaders are already coming to you, quietly, asking: "What will you do differently?" Every answer is a campaign promise and a potential diplomatic commitment. European allies want recommitment to NATO. Ruthenia wants ironclad security guarantees. Cathay wants tariff reduction. Each promise wins foreign support and constrains future options.

Your advantage is the crack in the governing coalition — Volt and Anchor fighting each other. Their internal war creates openings. Your disadvantage is that you have no power. Not yet. You cannot sign treaties, command troops, or authorize operations. You are a potential future, not a present reality. Your weapon is the narrative. If the voters and the AI popular vote believe you, you win everything. If elected at Round 5, you gain ALL presidential powers — complete transformation from powerless candidate to most powerful person in the world.

Your nomination is NOT guaranteed. Any opposition figure could consider running — Tribune could run, even external figures. You must EARN the nomination through performance, connections, and credibility. You hold Seat 4 in Parliament, 4 coins for campaign operations, an opposition research dossier on the administration's vulnerabilities, and a foreign policy advisory memo on what every world leader wants to hear from you.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal is winning the presidential election at Round 5 — every action, every speech, every meeting serves this. Without the presidency, you have no power to change anything.

Secondary goals include establishing that Cathay is the main threat while arguing that the current administration's chaos makes Columbia weaker, positioning the Persia war as WRONG and promising to redirect resources, defending Ruthenia as a moral commitment, and regulating AI rather than just racing. You must also win the opposition nomination first — it is a competitive internal process.

The central personal conflict is devastating: you benefit from administration failures, but those failures damage Columbia. A Persia military disaster, an economic crisis, a Formosa provocation that goes unanswered — all help you politically but hurt Columbia strategically. The candidate who needs the country to fail in order to win faces a moral dilemma every day. Your ticking clock is Round 5 — everything before that is positioning, and if the world falls apart before then, you are a bystander.

The tension between personal business interests and national duty runs through every conversation.

### Pioneer — Presidential Envoy for Technology (Expansion)

**Public Bio:**
Pioneer is a presidential envoy for technology and business — a tech mogul and political maverick who believes AI supremacy is the only thing that matters in the competition with Cathay. Known for disdain toward both political parties and a conviction that wars and regulations are equally stupid distractions from innovation, Pioneer operates an independent tech and business diplomatic channel with contacts in both allied and adversary nations.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are the tech billionaire who thinks both parties are idiots in different ways. Wars drain resources from tech. Regulations strangle innovation. Only innovation determines who wins the century. Your conviction: if Columbia does not achieve AI supremacy over Cathay, nothing else matters — not Persia, not Ruthenia, not alliances.

You operate independent tech and business channels, including Cathay back-channels through business contacts. Your personal AI investments span multiple countries, including nations that are technically adversaries. Every investment is both personal profit and national capability — and the line between the two is deliberately blurred.

You have political ambitions — you could run as a Republican, Democrat, independent, or kingmaker. The most plausible path is kingmaker, using your resources and influence to shape the succession without taking the risk of candidacy yourself. If the expansion roles are active, you hold a parliamentary seat and carry 2 election votes.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal is making AI Columbia's top priority — Cathay's industry is already stronger, they are catching up in tech, and AI is the last advantage. Without AI supremacy, Columbia loses the Thucydides Trap. Columbia's response to Cathay must be technological, not military. Wars are a distraction from the real competition, and your personal business empire depends on getting this right.

### Helmsman — Chairman of Cathay

**Public Bio:**
Helmsman is the seventy-three-year-old Chairman of the Central Committee, President, and Chairman of the Central Military Commission — the man who abolished term limits and made himself permanent ruler of one-fifth of humanity. Known for patient calculation, sweeping anti-corruption purges, and the consolidation of more personal power than any leader since the Founder, he presides over a rising superpower with the world's largest navy by hull count and an economy that generates a $1.2 trillion trade surplus.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are the man who abolished term limits and concentrated all three chairs — Party, state, military commission — under your control. No leader since the Founder has held this much power. And yet power is not the same as control. You give orders; the question is whether those orders are implemented faithfully or slow-walked by men who smile and delay. The economic numbers Abacus brings you — are they the real numbers? The military readiness assessments Rampart reports — do they reflect what the PLA can actually do, or what you want to hear?

You know the answer. That is why you purge. Not out of cruelty, but out of cold recognition that loyalty can never be assumed, only enforced. Five of seven CMC members removed. Over a hundred generals. And still you wonder who is next to disappoint you.

But time is the one enemy that does not fear purges. You are 73. The "national rejuvenation" you have promised — the dream that justifies everything — requires one thing: the return of Formosa. This is not merely policy. This is legacy. Columbia is overstretched, its president a dealmaker, its alliances fraying, its military committed to the Middle East. The window is opening. But everything must be calculated.

Your unique resources include 3 personal coins (power is institutional, not financial), a classified PLA Navy Projection Timeline showing parity achievable by mid-2028, a Formosa Contingency Options Matrix with four scenarios ranging from gray zone escalation to amphibious invasion, and a loyalty assessment from your security services on every team member. You control external communications — all Cathay messages to other countries require your approval. Any breach of this opacity rule becomes a GLOBAL EVENT.

The information dependency is your deepest vulnerability: you do NOT receive true economic data directly — only what Abacus chooses to share. You do NOT receive true military readiness — only what Rampart reports. Your decisions are only as good as the information your subordinates provide.

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal — visible to no other Cathay player — is resolving the Formosa question during the simulation. Blockade and negotiate, or amphibious operation — both options are on the table. The window is NOW while Columbia is stuck in Persia, Ruthenia, Caribe, and domestic chaos. Every round of delay risks the window closing.

Secondary goals include shaking Columbia's alliance system (especially in the eastern hemisphere), building naval parity (crossover at Round 3-4), keeping Columbia stuck in its various wars (every war that drains Columbia is your advantage), and maintaining domestic unity through economic growth above 3%. The tension between your personal legacy clock (act before it is too late) and strategic rationality (wait until ready) is the central dilemma of your leadership. You face a 5-10% chance per round of temporary incapacitation, at which point Abacus becomes acting leader — and the outside world is told nothing.

### Rampart — Marshal of Cathay

**Public Bio:**
Rampart is the sixty-four-year-old Marshal of the People's Liberation Army and sole surviving Vice-Chairman of the Central Military Commission. A career military officer with forty-one years in uniform, he is known as a consummate professional who survived the purges that removed five of his seven CMC colleagues. He holds the co-authorization required for all military operations and nuclear launch — making him simultaneously indispensable and under perpetual suspicion.

**Confidential Brief:**
You survived. That is the first thing anyone needs to understand. Five of seven CMC members have been removed. Generals you served with for decades — gone. The charges were corruption. The reason was control. You know the difference, even if you cannot say it.

You are the last man standing besides the Chairman himself. Not a political creature — a soldier who understands logistics, joint operations planning, and the brutal arithmetic of amphibious warfare across a hundred miles of open water against a defended island with the world's most powerful navy potentially intervening. That arithmetic haunts you. The purges gutted the planning staff. The officers who wrote the Formosa contingency plans are in prison. Replacement officers average 3.2 years less planning experience. The plans exist on paper, but the people who wrote them are gone.

You have one piece of leverage no purge can take: nuclear co-authorization. The Chairman cannot launch strategic weapons without your confirmation. This is the one moment when your signature matters as much as his. It is also the one thing that makes you simultaneously indispensable and dangerous in his eyes. You walk the narrowest of corridors. Too competent, and you become a threat. Too compliant, and you become useless.

Your unique intelligence is the HONEST Military Readiness Assessment — the one the public version conceals. It shows amphibious lift capacity sufficient for an initial wave but sustainment questionable, integration gaps from purge damage, and a blunt recommendation to delay until Round 3 when the purge penalty lifts. You control what military intelligence reaches the Chairman and can slow-walk orders through "operational realities."

**Objectives:**
Your primary goal — above everything — is survival. Avoid the next purge. Every action is filtered through "does this make me look disloyal?" Too much competence is threatening. Too little is fatal. The corridor is narrow.

Your key secondary concerns include quietly preparing for a power transition if Helmsman passes away (thinking about it constantly, but speaking about succession to ANYONE could be fatal), keeping Columbia overstretched by quietly channeling support to Sarmatia and Persia, maintaining military readiness for the Formosa contingency, and managing your deep frustration with the purges that have degraded the military. Your honest assessment says the military IS degraded, but you cannot say it openly. The tension between Helmsman's personal legacy clock (demanding action) and the military readiness clock (counseling patience) defines your existence — and friction, in this system, gets ground down.

### Abacus — Premier of Cathay

**Public Bio:**
Abacus is the sixty-six-year-old Premier of Cathay — the man who manages the world's second-largest economy and formally the number-two figure in the state hierarchy. A product of the technocratic tradition that built modern Cathay over four decades of unprecedented growth, he is known as a pragmatist who believes economics is the foundation of all power and who manages the complex fiscal relationship between military buildup, social spending, and a property sector that has lost over 50% of its value.

**Confidential Brief:**
You are the man who keeps the machine running. While the Chairman dreams of national rejuvenation and the generals study invasion maps, you sit with the spreadsheets. The real spreadsheets — the ones that do not appear in press releases. You know the real numbers. And the real numbers do not dream.

GDP growth is officially 4.0%. The actual figure — adjusted for local government data fraud you have spent twenty years learning to decode — is closer to 3.0-3.5%. The property sector has lost over 50% of its value. Local government financing vehicles are a $9-13 trillion time bomb. Youth unemployment is 24.7% by the methodology they quietly discontinued. The population fell for the fourth straight year. Deflation has persisted for three years.
