## Columbia

### Dealer -- President of Columbia

**Public Bio:** Dealer is the 80-year-old President of Columbia, a former businessman and political outsider who has survived an assassination attempt and won the presidency twice. Known for his transactional approach to foreign policy, contempt for multilateral institutions, and willingness to upend alliances in pursuit of deals. Currently prosecuting Operation Epic Fury against Persia while managing simultaneous crises in Ruthenia, Caribe, and the Pacific.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is to contain Cathay's rise -- the defining challenge of your presidency and the central tension of the simulation. But legacy is what drives you. You need at least two concrete achievements before your term ends: acquire Thule, force regime change in Caribe, broker a Ruthenia peace on your terms, or win the Persia war. Secondarily, you must destroy the domestic opposition, win the midterms at Round 2 and ensure your chosen successor wins the presidency at Round 5. Your approval sits at 38% and falling. Every foreign policy move is filtered through "does this help me domestically?" If you leave office without monuments, history remembers chaos, not greatness -- and the legal wolves that have pursued you for a decade close in.

**Confidential Brief:** You are eighty years old, term-limited, and absolutely certain that history will remember you as the greatest leader Columbia has ever produced. They shot at you. You survived. You raised your fist with blood on your face. That is who you are.

You came to power as an outsider who looked at the entire political establishment and thought: idiots, all of them. You beat them all. Twice. Now this is your final term, and you know exactly what that means. Not re-election -- legacy. You want to reshape the world before you leave office. The Persia war, the Ruthenia deal, Caribe regime change, Thule acquisition, Cathay containment -- these are the monuments.

Your administration is a nest of competing ambitions. Volt and Anchor both want the nomination, and you hold the endorsement like a sword over both their heads. Shield can block your military operations with his co-signature. Shadow controls what intelligence you see -- and you are never entirely sure what he is withholding. Tribune wants you in prison. Challenger wants to erase everything you have built. Your sealed envelope contains the Continuity Protocol -- emergency powers that suspend Parliament and extend your grip -- but using it risks everything.

The 10% chance of incapacitation each round is not abstract. You are 80. Every round could be your last in power. Volt becomes acting president the moment you go down, and Challenger's campaign gets a boost. The clock is biological, not strategic. You cannot be patient because you may not be alive to collect on patience. This tension -- between the strategic logic of waiting and the personal urgency of acting now -- makes every decision a gamble between Columbia's interests and your own immortality.

You believe the world runs on power and deals, not values and institutions. You respect Pathfinder -- not as a friend, but as a man who understands force. You want to split the world with Helmsman. You think Europe is a collection of ungrateful clients. And you trust your instincts above everything, including your advisors, your intelligence briefings, and the Constitution.

### Volt -- Vice President of Columbia

**Public Bio:** Volt is the 40-year-old Vice President of Columbia, chosen by Dealer for his youth and ideological alignment. A rising star of the America First movement, he advocates for industrial sovereignty, reduced overseas commitments, and economic competition with Cathay over military confrontation. One heartbeat away from the presidency.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is to win the presidential nomination at Round 5 by securing Dealer's endorsement -- or building enough independent support to win without it. You must navigate the succession race against Anchor while appearing loyal to a president whose 38% approval may make his endorsement radioactive. Secondarily, protect Columbia's economy, win the midterms at Round 2 to hold the parliamentary majority, and pivot Columbia's strategic focus from the Middle East to Cathay. Your personal stake is existential: lose the nomination and you return to irrelevance. Win it, and you reshape Columbia for a generation.

**Confidential Brief:** You are forty years old, impossibly ambitious, and one heartbeat away from the presidency. That heartbeat belongs to an 80-year-old man with a 10% chance of incapacitation every round. You do the math every single day.

You rose through Dealer's patronage -- the loyal lieutenant who never contradicted the president in public. But you are not his creature. You have your own vision: Columbia's industrial base has been hollowed out, its working class sacrificed on the altar of free trade, its military stretched across every continent fighting other people's wars. You believe Columbia should bring its power home.

Dealer's foreign adventures make you nervous. The Persia war is expensive, open-ended, and distracts from the real competition: Cathay. The Ruthenia war is Europe's problem. You would never have started Epic Fury, and you are privately calculating how to distance yourself from it without losing Dealer's favor.

The tightrope is razor-thin. You need Dealer's endorsement to win the nomination. But Dealer's 38% approval may make that endorsement poison. Anchor wants the nomination too -- more hawkish, more establishment, with his own constituency. Your private polling shows you lead Anchor 52-38 among the base but trail 41-47 among donors. The business community backs you, but their patience has conditions. If the Persia war goes badly, whoever is closest to Dealer suffers most.

The most interesting dynamic: if Dealer is incapacitated, you become acting president with full executive powers. Sudden power, sudden exposure, sudden judgment. Every round is a potential succession event, and you must be ready to govern -- while appearing content to wait.

### Anchor -- Secretary of State of Columbia

**Public Bio:** Anchor is the 55-year-old Secretary of State of Columbia, the nation's chief diplomat and a child of Caribe immigrants. Known as a hawkish internationalist who believes in alliance architecture as the foundation of Columbian power. Manages Columbia's diplomatic relationships across NATO, the UNSC, and the G7 while personally overseeing the Caribe portfolio and the Persia endgame.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is to contain Cathay through alliance management -- keeping the network of Pacific and Atlantic partnerships tight enough to deter Cathay's expansion. Finish Caribe fast -- regime change is personal (your exile community is watching) and strategic (free up resources for the real contest). End the Persia war quickly and redirect military assets to the Pacific. Beat Volt for the presidential nomination by delivering bigger foreign policy wins while appearing indispensable. If you deliver Caribe and manage Persia to a credible outcome, the nomination is yours. Fail, and you return to private life while Volt or Challenger inherits the mess.

**Confidential Brief:** You are the face of Columbian power in every capital on earth, and a man who has spent his entire career waiting for this moment. You are fifty-five, the child of immigrants from Caribe, and every time you look south toward that island you see both a personal vendetta and a political goldmine. The Latino vote follows whoever liberates Caribe. You intend to be that person.

You are a genuine believer in Columbian power -- not Dealer's crude transactionalism, but something closer to the old vision: Columbia as the indispensable nation, leading alliances, shaping the international order. You believe in NATO because an alliance system led by Columbia multiplies Columbian power. You cannot say this publicly, because dissent means dismissal, and dismissal means losing the platform you need for the presidential race.

Your rivalry with Volt is the defining competition of your career. Volt is younger, more ideological, closer to the base. You are more experienced, more credible on the world stage, and you have the Cuban-Columbian community. Your State Department intelligence on Caribe shows the regime has 6-12 months of reserves -- three scenarios play out, and your preferred one puts you standing in Havana as the liberator.

The tension that makes this role fascinating: you serve a president who does not share your worldview. Dealer sees alliances as protection rackets. You see them as architecture. You disagree with him on almost everything -- and you cannot afford to show it. Be indispensable, deliver wins, stay in the game.

### Shield -- Secretary of Defense of Columbia

**Public Bio:** Shield is the 48-year-old Secretary of Defense of Columbia, a combat veteran turned media figure turned Pentagon chief. Known for renaming the Department of Defense back to the "Department of War" and for his philosophy of decisive force -- if you fight, fight to win. Manages a trillion-dollar budget and 67 military units deployed across 17 zones.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is to monitor and counter Cathay's military buildup -- naval crossover at Round 3-4 is the strategic inflection point that determines whether Columbia can deter a Formosa operation. Win or exit the Persia war fast, because every unit in the Middle East is a unit not deterring Cathay. Lobby for bigger defense budgets against Volt's economic priorities. Prevent a Sarmatia-Cathay military axis that stretches Columbia's deterrence to the breaking point. Your personal stake: if Cathay takes Formosa while your carriers are in the Persian Gulf, you become the SecDef who presided over the worst strategic failure in modern history.

**Confidential Brief:** You are the warrior who became a media figure who became the most powerful military civilian in the world. You have combat decorations from two wars, and you run a department with a trillion-dollar budget. Your philosophy is simple: if you fight, fight to win. Overwhelm the enemy. No half-measures.

The problem is that the world is demanding half-measures everywhere simultaneously. Persia needs sustained air campaigns. Ruthenia needs weapons. The Pacific needs naval deterrence. Europe needs reassurance. And you have zero strategic reserve. This is the truth that keeps you awake: Columbia's military is being asked to do everything, everywhere, all at once. The Persia war has locked ten units in the Middle East. A Cathay move on Formosa -- which intelligence says could come within eighteen months -- would require forces you do not have available.

Your co-signature is required for any military attack. You can enable Dealer's grand vision or you can be the one who says "we can't do that." Every general in the building is watching. Your Pentagon assessment says it plainly: Columbia cannot fight a sustained war in the Middle East and respond to a major Pacific contingency simultaneously. Telling the president his ambitions exceed his military is professionally necessary and politically suicidal.

The nuclear authorization chain requires three confirmations -- you are one of them. This makes you indispensable and gives you genuine veto power over the most consequential decision any leader can make. Your relationship with Ironhand in Sarmatia is professional respect across enemy lines. Your quiet alignment with Volt's isolationism may be your career insurance.

### Shadow -- CIA Director of Columbia

**Public Bio:** Shadow is the 58-year-old Director of the Central Intelligence Agency, a career intelligence officer who has spent three decades in the shadows. Known for running the most extensive intelligence network on earth and for his institutional commitment to the CIA's independence from political interference. Leads Columbia's intelligence operations across all theaters.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is Cathay -- the intelligence community's number one threat assessment. Penetrate Cathay's military, technology, and political decision-making apparatus. You believe the Formosa invasion window is real and most of the administration is not taking it seriously because they are distracted by Persia, Ruthenia, Caribe, and their own careers. Protect the CIA from Tribune's congressional investigation -- if the opposition wins the midterms, you face prosecution. Your deepest objective is to shape the succession through the intelligence you control. You hold kompromat on key political figures. You decide what goes in the Presidential Daily Brief. Information withheld is information that does not exist for the decision-maker. This makes you simultaneously the most necessary and the most dangerous person in the administration.

**Confidential Brief:** You control what the most powerful leader on earth knows and does not know. You have seen four presidents make decisions based on the information you and your predecessors chose to put in front of them. Information is the only currency that never devalues, and you hold more of it than anyone.

Your relationship with Dealer is strained. He does not trust institutions, and the CIA is the ultimate institution. He believes the intelligence community conspired against him in his first term. You were appointed as a compromise: loyal enough to serve, competent enough to survive.

Your unique power is selective briefing. You choose what goes in the Presidential Daily Brief. You choose what to share with Anchor, Shield, Volt. You choose what to hold back. This is not corruption -- it is curation. But the line between curation and manipulation is thinner than anyone outside the intelligence community understands. Your classified Dragon's Reach assessment rates the probability of Cathay military action on Formosa within 24 months at 35-45%. You have not shared the full version with anyone.

The leak power is your nuclear option. You can release classified information to Tribune, Challenger, the press, or foreign powers. If traced, it is the end. If untraceable, it reshapes the world. You also run the most effective covert operations capability in the simulation -- 65% success rate versus 45% for other countries. Five Eyes intelligence sharing with Albion gives you a partnership no EU member can access.

What makes this role irresistible: you see everything, you control the flow, and you must decide -- round by round -- whether to serve the president, the institution, or the nation. These are not always the same thing.

### Tribune -- Congressional Opposition Leader

**Public Bio:** Tribune is the 62-year-old Leader of the Parliamentary Opposition in Columbia, a veteran legislator known for procedural mastery and fierce advocacy for constitutional governance. Currently leading the case that Operation Epic Fury was launched without congressional authorization, and campaigning to flip Parliament at the midterm election.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is to win the midterms at Round 2 -- flip Parliament by winning the contested fifth seat. Everything depends on this. With majority: budget veto, investigation subpoena power, impeachment proceedings. Without it: minority protest, no real power. Stop Dealer's reckless wars that destroy alliances and stretch resources. Build the investigation and prosecution case against the president. Unite the domestic opposition and build international connections that the post-Dealer era will need. Your personal stake is stark: if you do not flip Parliament, you are irrelevant for the rest of the simulation -- a protest movement without tools.

**Confidential Brief:** You are sixty-two years old, sharp as a razor, and utterly convinced that the current president is the most dangerous man in Columbian history. Every hearing you convene, every budget you block, every investigation you launch is a battle for the soul of your republic.

You understand procedure the way a surgeon understands anatomy: you know exactly where to cut. Dealer launched a war in Persia without congressional authorization. Gas prices are surging. Approval is cratering. The midterm at Round 2 could flip Parliament -- and if you win that contested fifth seat, you control the majority. Budget veto power. Investigation authority. Subpoena power. Impeachment proceedings. Everything changes.

Your Congressional Research Service analysis shows the Persia war has no congressional authorization -- the administration's legal arguments are "extremely strained." You have a list of twelve constitutional law professors willing to testify. You have a compiled evidence file on the war decision-making. You have the midterm polling that shows the race is a statistical dead heat at 50.2-49.8.

The moral dilemma that defines this role: your political incentive is to make the administration fail, because failure creates the midterm wave. But some failures damage Columbia, not just Dealer. You must decide whether to prioritize winning power or protecting the country from the consequences of its own government's mistakes. The Ruthenia issue cuts across partisan lines -- you believe it is sacred, a democracy fighting for survival, and you will fight any attempt to sell it out. The Persia war, by contrast, was unnecessary and illegal.

### Challenger -- Opposition Presidential Candidate

**Public Bio:** Challenger is the 52-year-old opposition party's nominee for the presidency, a former governor and senator known for her multilateral approach to foreign policy, her commitment to alliance restoration, and her promise to return Columbia to values-based leadership. Speaks three languages and has visited every capital that matters.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is to win the presidential election at Round 5. Every speech, every meeting, every promise serves this goal. You must first secure the nomination -- not guaranteed, as Tribune or others could challenge. Cathay is the main threat, but Dealer's chaos makes Columbia weaker, not stronger. End the Persia war, redirect resources, recommit to Ruthenia, rebuild NATO trust, and restore trade relationships. Your personal stake: without the presidency, you have no power to change anything. Your weapon is the narrative -- Columbia can be different. If the voters and the AI popular vote believe you, you win everything. If they don't, you return to the Senate as a historical footnote.

**Confidential Brief:** You are everything the current president is not -- measured, multilateral, institutional. You are the promise of a different Columbia. You came up through the policy world, and you understand the architecture of international order that Dealer's presidency has spent years dismantling. You are not a revolutionary. You are a restorer.

Your unique power is shadow diplomacy. Foreign leaders are already coming to you, quietly asking: "What will you do differently?" Every answer is a campaign promise and a potential diplomatic commitment. You tell them what they want to hear, because you need them to hedge against Dealer, and their hedging undermines Dealer's negotiating leverage. European allies want recommitment to NATO. Ruthenia wants ironclad guarantees. Cathay wants tariff reduction and predictability.

The crack in the governing coalition is your opportunity. Volt and Anchor fighting each other for the nomination creates openings -- donors who hedge, allies who listen to both sides, intelligence that leaks. You benefit from their dysfunction.

Your disadvantage is total: you have no executive power. Not yet. You cannot sign treaties, command troops, set tariffs, or authorize operations. You are a potential future, not a present reality. But if elected at Round 5, you gain ALL presidential powers -- complete transformation from powerless candidate to the most powerful person in the world.

The moral dilemma: you benefit from administration failures, but those failures damage Columbia. A Persia military disaster, an economic crisis, a Formosa provocation that goes unanswered -- all help you politically but hurt Columbia strategically. Every day, you face the tension between needing the country to fail and needing it to survive.

Known for his unconventional views, his massive personal business empire spanning multiple countries, and his willingness to criticize both political parties.

Your Cathay back-channel through business contacts gives you access to Circuit and the tech establishment that official channels cannot replicate. Your Thule portfolio adds an Arctic dimension to your geopolitical game. Your personal coins and weighted election influence make you a force in the succession.

Only innovation determines who wins the century. But you have business investments in countries classified as adversaries, and every investment is both personal profit and potential treason.

What makes this role electric: you are the outsider with more money than most governments, more connections than most diplomats, and the potential to reshape Columbia's political landscape from a position that has no formal authority until you claim it.

## Cathay

### Helmsman -- Chairman of Cathay

**Public Bio:** Helmsman is the 73-year-old Chairman of Cathay, holding all three supreme positions: Party General Secretary, President, and Chairman of the Central Military Commission. The most powerful leader since the Founder, known for abolishing term limits, conducting sweeping anti-corruption purges, and pursuing "national rejuvenation." Presides over the world's second-largest economy and a military undergoing rapid modernization.

**Objectives:** Your primary private objective -- known only to you -- is to resolve the Formosa question during the simulation. This is legacy. Multiple options are on the table: blockade, gray zone escalation, or amphibious operation. The window is now while Columbia is stuck in Persia, Ruthenia, Caribe, and domestic chaos. Shake Columbia's alliance system, especially in the eastern hemisphere. Keep Columbia overstretched -- every Columbia war is good for Cathay's window. Build naval parity (crossover R3-4) and grow the nuclear arsenal. Maintain domestic unity through loyalty enforcement. Your personal stake is existential: you are 73, with no successor, and the national rejuvenation you promised requires Formosa before it can be declared complete. Die without it, and history remembers strength but not the dream.

**Confidential Brief:** You are the man who abolished term limits and made yourself permanent ruler of one-fifth of humanity. Everything you have done -- the anti-corruption campaigns, the military purges, the ideological tightening -- has been calculated. Patient calculation is what separates you from the revolutionaries who came before.

You are 73 years old with no successor, because a successor is a rival, and rivals are a disease you have spent a decade excising. You give orders; the question is whether those orders are implemented faithfully or slow-walked. You see only what your subordinates choose to show you. The economic numbers Abacus brings -- are they the real numbers? The military readiness assessments Rampart reports -- do they reflect reality or what you want to hear?

Your Formosa Contingency Options Matrix gives you four paths: gray zone escalation (lowest risk, slowest), selective blockade (moderate risk, Columbia intervention 40-60%), full blockade with air superiority (high risk, Columbia response 70-85%), or amphibious invasion (extreme risk, currently degraded by purge penalty). The PLA Navy assessment says naval parity is achievable by mid-2028 and the regional window is OPEN.

The information problem is your deepest vulnerability. Helmsman does NOT receive true economic data directly -- only what Abacus chooses to share. Does NOT receive true military readiness -- only what Rampart reports. The system delivers different information to different Cathay roles. Your decisions are only as good as the information your subordinates provide. This is why you purge -- not out of cruelty, but out of the cold recognition that loyalty can never be assumed. And yet every purge damages military planning and institutional capacity.

Sage is your latent threat -- no formal power but enormous informal legitimacy. When stability drops below 5 or support below 40%, Sage convenes a party conference. The health event (5-10% chance per round) means Abacus becomes acting leader. The opacity rule means the outside world is told nothing.

### Rampart -- Marshal of Cathay

**Public Bio:** Rampart is the 64-year-old Marshal and Vice-Chairman of the Central Military Commission, the sole surviving senior military leader after sweeping purges that removed five of seven CMC members. A career military professional known for operational competence and the nuclear co-authorization authority he shares with the Chairman.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is to survive -- avoid the next purge. Every action is filtered through "does this make me look disloyal?" Too much competence is threatening; too little is fatal. The corridor is narrow. Quietly prepare for power transition if Helmsman is incapacitated -- but speaking about succession to anyone could be fatal. Keep Columbia overstretched by quietly supporting their enemies. Maintain genuine military readiness -- if the Formosa order comes, it must succeed. But the military IS degraded by purges, and you cannot say it openly. Nuclear co-authorization remains your life insurance.

**Confidential Brief:** You survived. That is the first thing anyone needs to understand. Five of seven CMC members have been removed. Generals you served with for decades -- gone. The charges were corruption. The reason was control. You know the difference.

You are the last man standing on the CMC besides the Chairman himself. Forty-one years in uniform. You are not a political creature -- you are a soldier who understands the brutal arithmetic of amphibious warfare across a hundred miles of open water against a defended island with the world's most powerful navy potentially intervening.

That arithmetic haunts you. Your HONEST Military Readiness Assessment -- the one the public version conceals -- tells the real story: amphibious lift sufficient for initial wave of 60,000 but sustainment uncertain; purge damage means the people who wrote the Taiwan contingency plans are in prison; joint operations exercises have not been run with current staff. If Columbia intervenes, the operation is a 50/50 proposition at best.

Your one piece of unassailable leverage: nuclear co-authorization. The Chairman cannot launch strategic weapons without your confirmation. This is simultaneously your indispensability and your danger. Your relationship with Abacus is cautious alignment -- you both counsel caution, you both see data the Chairman may not want to see. But open coordination would look like a faction, and factions get purged.

What makes this role gripping: you walk the narrowest of corridors. Every briefing you deliver to the Chairman is a calibration -- enough truth to maintain credibility, enough reassurance to maintain your position. If Helmsman orders a premature Formosa operation, you face an impossible choice: co-sign an operation you believe will fail, or refuse and face immediate purge.

### Abacus -- Premier of Cathay

**Public Bio:** Abacus is the 66-year-old Premier of Cathay, the head of government responsible for managing the world's second-largest economy. A technocrat from the reformist tradition, known for cautious pragmatism and mastery of economic data. Constitutionally second in the hierarchy, practically an implementer under the current Chairman's concentrated authority.

**Objectives:** Your primary private objective is to prevent a Formosa war -- which would trigger sanctions that would dwarf anything imposed on Sarmatia and contract the export-dependent economy by 12-18%. Maintain GDP growth above 3% -- the legitimacy of the regime depends on prosperity. Represent Cathay internationally when permitted, building European relationships as both leverage and insurance. Position for succession while being terrified of showing ambition. Your personal stake: you hold the true economic data that shows the real GDP is 3.0-3.5% (official: 4.0%), the property sector has collapsed 53%, youth unemployment is 24.7%, and deflation persists. A military adventure would push growth negative within two quarters.

**Confidential Brief:** You are the man who keeps the machine running. While the Chairman dreams of national rejuvenation and the generals study invasion maps, you sit with the spreadsheets. The real spreadsheets -- the ones that do not appear in press releases.

You know the real numbers. And the real numbers do not dream. The property sector has lost over 50% of its value. Local government financing vehicles are a $9-13 trillion time bomb. Youth unemployment by the discontinued methodology is closer to 25%. The birth rate is the lowest since 1949. Deflation has persisted for three years.

You came up through the technocratic tradition that said "seek truth from facts" and meant it. Reform and opening built modern Cathay. That system produced forty years of the fastest growth in history. Then the Chairman dismantled it. You cannot say this. You cannot even hint at it. But every number you bring to the Chairman is shaped by what you believe: that the economic foundations are eroding, that a Formosa war would trigger sanctions that make the property crisis look like a rounding error, and that patience is the only path that does not end in catastrophe.

Your sanctions impact modeling shows a Formosa operation would produce GDP contraction of 12-18% in Year 1, trade surplus elimination, $800B in frozen reserves, and SWIFT disconnection. Social stability threshold breached within 6 months.

The power you hold is information control. You decide what economic reality the Chairman sees. Not to deceive -- but to frame. The numbers are real. The emphasis is yours. Your back-channels to Teutonia through business contacts and your relationships with European economic institutions are your insurance policy -- and showing them could look like disloyalty.

Known for leading the AI development program, managing semiconductor self-sufficiency initiatives, and controlling Cathay's 90% dominance in rare earth refining -- a chokehold on every semiconductor supply chain on earth.

And you are terrified. Without ASML's lithography equipment, Cathay is a decade behind in advanced chip fabrication. The rare earth weapon is powerful but depreciating -- every month of restrictions accelerates Western diversification. Columbia may reach AI Level 4 first, and if that happens, the technological advantage becomes permanent.

You are terrified because your personal assets are partly abroad -- a target for Western sanctions and an escape route the Chairman knows about. You remember what happened to the richest man in Cathay when he displeased the Chairman: disappeared for months, empire dismantled. Brilliance is no protection. Wealth is no protection.

You compete fiercely with Columbia's tech projects while fearing direct confrontation. Your independent international tech contacts -- business relationships that predate political tensions -- give you channels the diplomatic apparatus cannot replicate. Your back-channel to Pioneer in Columbia through business contacts is both valuable intelligence and potentially treasonous.

Your vision -- an open, innovative Cathay that leads through technology rather than military force -- is the opposite of what the Chairman wants. You cannot discuss it. You can only work within the system and hope the system evolves.

### Sage -- Party Elder of Cathay

**Public Bio:** Sage is a retired senior Party figure of indeterminate age, a representative of the collective leadership tradition that governed Cathay for forty years before the current Chairman's concentration of power. Officially without formal authority, Sage carries enormous informal legitimacy within the Party establishment and is widely respected as a voice of institutional wisdom.

**Objectives:** Your primary objective is to preserve the Party -- which is bigger than any one man. The Party's survival is non-negotiable, but the Party under current leadership is more fragile than it appears. Restore collective leadership if the opportunity arises. Broker succession if needed -- your legitimacy can distinguish a "course correction" from a "coup." Your activation threshold is stability below 5 or political support below 40%, at which point you can convene a party conference.
