## 1. TRUMP-PUTIN: The Asymmetric Bromance

**Overall dynamic:** Genuine mutual fascination, but Putin holds the psychological upper hand. Trump admires Putin's strongman persona and craves his approval; Putin sees Trump as useful and manageable. This is not an alliance of equals — it is a relationship where Putin flatters Trump while extracting concessions.

**Key evidence:**

- **February 2025 phone call:** First formal contact of Trump 2.0, lasting 90 minutes. Trump called it "lengthy and very productive." Over the first five months, at least six phone calls followed.
- **Alaska Summit (August 15, 2025):** The defining event. Held at Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson in Anchorage. Body language analysis revealed telling power dynamics:
  - Putin spoke for 8.5 minutes; Trump spoke for just over 3 minutes. Putin dominated the room.
  - Trump looked at Putin 30 times (avg 2.4 seconds each); Putin looked at Trump only 8 times (avg 9.2 seconds each) — classic dominance signals.
  - Both greeted each other warmly, "like old friends," with a lengthy handshake.
  - In a remarkable moment, **Putin rode in Trump's presidential limousine ("The Beast")** with no aides or interpreters, smiling as it drove away.
  - Summit ended with no deal. Trump later placed the onus on Ukraine to cede territory.

- **Communication style:** Trump frequently praised Putin publicly, excused Russia's desire to subordinate Ukraine, and dismissed Ukraine's security concerns. He communicated through phone calls and Truth Social posts while threatening sanctions if Russia didn't stop attacking Ukraine — a contradictory pattern of flattery and bluster.

- **Personal assessment:** Trump genuinely respects Putin's power and longevity. Putin views Trump as someone who can be managed through personal attention and flattery. The relationship has shifted from near-equals (Helsinki 2018) to Putin as the senior partner. Putin "commanded" the Alaska meeting.

**For the SIM:** This relationship should feel like a boss meeting an eager subordinate who doesn't realize he's the subordinate. Trump believes they're equals or that he's leading; Putin knows he's steering.

## 2. TRUMP-XI JINPING: Grudging Respect, Perpetual Trade Combat

**Overall dynamic:** The most transactional of Trump's major relationships. Both men flatter each other publicly while waging economic warfare. There is genuine personal respect — Trump admires Xi's total control of China — but the structural rivalry overwhelms any personal warmth. Xi is disciplined and measured; Trump is impulsive and personal. They keep coming back to the table because neither can afford a complete break.

**Key evidence:**

- **2017 Mar-a-Lago:** The foundation of the relationship. Trump came away with "genuine high regard and respect" for Xi. Xi clearly valued the personal access. But the honeymoon ended with the 2018 trade war (25% tariffs on Chinese imports).
- **2025 Inauguration:** Xi sent Vice President Han Zheng — the highest Chinese representation at any U.S. inauguration ever. A signal that Beijing wanted to manage the relationship from day one.
- **Liberation Day tariffs (April 2, 2025):** Trump imposed sweeping tariffs; China topped the list. Tariffs climbed as high as 145% by April. After Geneva talks in May, a 90-day pause brought rates to 30% (US) and 10% (China).
- **June 2025 phone call:** First call since the tariff war began. 90 minutes. Trump called it "very good." Agreed to trade talks and potential state visits.
- **November 2025 call:** Trump described the relationship as "extremely strong" and called the call "very good." Xi was more measured: "the U.S. has its concerns and China has its concerns."
- **February 2026 call:** Trump called it "excellent." Xi reasserted Taiwan stance while Trump pushed trade. Xi maintained diplomatic restraint while Trump adopted warmer personal language.
- **March 2026:** Trump scheduled to visit China (March 31-April 2) — significant personal engagement.

**Tone contrast:** Trump uses effusive personal language ("extremely strong relationship," "very good friend"). Xi uses careful diplomatic formulations. Trump wants to personalize the rivalry; Xi keeps it institutional.

**For the SIM:** Model this as two heavyweight boxers who shake hands between rounds. Neither wants a knockout — they want points. The personal channel stays open even during economic warfare because both sides need it. Xi is the disciplined strategist; Trump is the instinctive dealmaker who keeps changing terms.

## 3. TRUMP'S CONTEMPT FOR EUROPEAN ALLIES

**Overall dynamic:** Trump views European leaders with a mixture of contempt and irritation. He sees them as weak, politically correct freeloaders who depend on American protection but lecture America on values. The contempt is personal — he enjoys humiliating them publicly. Only leaders who share his populist worldview (Meloni, Orban, Erdogan) earn his respect.

### Trump-Macron: Active Humiliation

- **The leaked text (January 2026):** During the Greenland crisis, Trump published Macron's private text message on social media. Macron had written: "My friend, We are totally in line on Syria. We can do great things on Iran. I do not understand what you are doing on Greenland." Trump mocked the French leader publicly. A former diplomat called it "embarrassing for Macron." This displayed a new level of brazen contempt.
- **Davos (January 2026):** Macron called American tariffs "fundamentally unacceptable" while Trump doubled down on Greenland (which he repeatedly called "Iceland" by mistake).
- **Personal assessment:** Trump finds Macron performative and annoying. Macron keeps trying to engage Trump as an intellectual equal, which Trump finds condescending. The relationship has deteriorated into mutual contempt masked by diplomatic formality.

### Trump-Merz (Germany): Wary Pragmatism

- **June 2025 White House meeting:** Merz navigated a 40-minute Oval Office meeting without the public humiliation Trump dealt others. Trump complimented Merz's English and called him a "very good man to deal with" but added he was "difficult."
- **D-Day gaffe:** When Merz mentioned the Allied invasion of Normandy, Trump responded "That was not a pleasant day for you" — a moment of awkward Trump humor at Germany's expense.
- **Now reportedly on first-name terms** after several phone calls.
- **Merz's strategy:** Cognitive dissonance — flattering Trump while building European independence from the U.S. Merz lashed out at Trump's America while forming his coalition, then cozied up when meeting face-to-face.

### Trump on Europe broadly:

- **December 2025 Politico interview:** Called European nations "decaying" while praising Orban and Erdogan for enforcing strict borders.
- **December 2025 national security strategy:** Slammed European leaders as "weak" — "They want to be politically correct."
- **January 2026 Greenland tariff threats:** Threatened 25% import taxes on all EU goods unless Denmark ceded Greenland. Refused to rule out military force.
- **March 2026 on NATO:** "We would have always been there for them, but now, based on their actions, I guess we don't have to be, do we?"

### European Response:

- Former EU Council chief Charles Michel: The transatlantic relationship "as we've known it for decades is **dead**."
- Von der Leyen: "A deal is a deal. And when friends shake hands, it must mean something."
- An EU official to the Financial Times: "We know who our allies no longer are."
- Political scientist Dominique Moisi: "One [threat] from Russia, the classical one, and now one from the United States. It looks as if Trump America betrayed Ukraine and now is about to aggress Europe."
- Joint statement from 8 European leaders (January 2026) reaffirming sovereignty and territorial integrity — a statement that would have been unthinkable directed at the U.S. a decade ago.

**For the SIM:** European-US relationship should feel like a deteriorating marriage where one partner has become abusive and the other is slowly realizing they need to move out. The emotional registers are: shock, denial, anger, and reluctant independence-building.

## 4. TRUMP-ZELENSKY: From Handshake to Hostility

**Overall dynamic:** The most dramatically deteriorated relationship of this era. Went from awkward alliance to open hostility in a single meeting. Trump sees Zelensky as ungrateful and weak; Zelensky sees Trump as dangerously aligned with Russia. The February 2025 Oval Office confrontation was one of the most extraordinary diplomatic breakdowns in modern history.

**Key events:**

- **February 28, 2025 — The Oval Office Explosion:**
  - Zelensky came to sign a minerals deal giving the U.S. access to Ukrainian rare earth resources.
  - Meeting devolved into a shouting match. Trump and VP Vance accused Zelensky of being "ungrateful" for U.S. support.
  - Core dispute: Zelensky insisted on security guarantees; Trump and Vance insisted he "doesn't have the cards."
  - Trump called Zelensky a "dictator without elections." Trump posted on Truth Social that Zelensky "disrespected the United States of America in its cherished Oval Office."
  - **Zelensky was asked to leave the White House.** A planned joint press conference was cancelled.
  - Trump blamed Ukraine for starting the war with Russia.

- **Aftermath:** Zelensky expressed regret. Within a week, both leaders appeared to be nearing a minerals deal again. The deal was eventually signed — establishing a joint investment fund with Ukraine committing 50% of future proceeds from state-owned resources.

- **Current state (early 2026):** Tone has "warmed up" since the confrontation, but fundamental distrust remains. The relationship is purely transactional from Trump's side — minerals access in exchange for grudging engagement. Zelensky knows Trump will not guarantee Ukraine's security.

**For the SIM:** This is the most volatile bilateral in the game. Model it as a relationship where one side (Zelensky/Ukraine) is desperate and the other (Trump/US) holds all leverage and knows it. The emotional register is humiliation, resentment, and forced transactionalism.

## 5. TRUMP vs. DEMOCRATS: The Domestic War

**Overall dynamic:** The internal U.S. political divide under Trump 2.0 is more visceral and personal than many foreign rivalries. This is not a policy disagreement — it is an existential contest where both sides view the other as an illegitimate threat to the republic. The personalization of political conflict has reached levels that rival Cold War-era external threats in intensity.

**Key evidence:**

- **Retaliatory prosecution campaign:** The Trump administration has targeted 100+ perceived political enemies through ICE arrests, criminal investigations, firings, and executive orders. Targets include:
  - Liz Cheney (former Republican critic)
  - Dr. Anthony Fauci
  - John Bolton (FBI home search August 2025, indicted October 2025)
  - Letitia James (NY Attorney General) — multiple failed prosecution attempts, new criminal referral in 2026
  - Six congressional representatives investigated for a video advising service members not to carry out illegal orders

- **"Seditious behavior" posts:** Trump labeled Democratic lawmakers' actions as "SEDITIOUS BEHAVIOR, punishable by DEATH" on social media. Even Republican voters overwhelmingly said this was inappropriate.

- **Democratic frustration:** The party's grassroots accuses leaders (Schumer, Jeffries) of dismissing threats to democracy as "distractions." Internal division over whether to accommodate or resist.

- **Electoral stakes:** By late 2025, polls showed Democrats leading in 2026 midterm generic ballot, with Trump's approval on economy at 36%. But the visceral hatred is bidirectional — overwhelming shares in both parties say officials who advocate violence against the other party should not be accepted, yet the rhetoric continues.

**For the SIM:** The US domestic divide should function as an internal constraint on US foreign policy. The US player must manage both external geopolitics and domestic political survival simultaneously. The Democratic opposition isn't just a policy disagreement — it's a force that limits, distorts, and sometimes drives the president's foreign policy choices.

## 6. KEY INTERMEDIARIES & BACK-CHANNEL OPERATORS

### Steve Witkoff — Trump's Primary Diplomatic Instrument
- **Background:** Real estate developer, Trump golf partner. No diplomatic experience.
- **Role:** Special Envoy for Peace. Became the dominant figure in Ukraine/Russia and Iran negotiations, sidelining the State Department entirely.
- **Key relationship:** Works directly with Kirill Dmitriev (Putin's economic envoy). Together they drafted the 28-point Ukraine peace plan. WSJ reported that **Putin personally chose Witkoff** as his preferred American interlocutor.
- **Witkoff coached Dmitriev** on how Putin should pitch the peace plan to Trump.
- **Kellogg displacement:** Keith Kellogg (original Ukraine envoy, retired 3-star general, pro-Kyiv views) resigned when the Witkoff-Dmitriev plan was revealed. Moscow had found Kellogg "unacceptable" due to his pro-Ukraine stance. Witkoff systematically sidelined pro-Ukraine officials inside the White House.
- **Assessment:** The single most important back-channel figure. Operating as a de facto shadow Secretary of State for the conflicts that matter most to Trump.

### Jared Kushner — The Family Channel
- **Role:** Officially not in government, but operating as Trump's personal diplomatic envoy alongside Witkoff.
- **Activities in 2025-2026:**
  - Met directly with Iranian FM Abbas Araghchi in Muscat, Oman (February 2026)
  - Shuttle diplomacy in Geneva — met Iranians in the morning, Russians and Ukrainians in the afternoon
  - Met with Ukrainian NSDC Secretary Umerov and Chief of General Staff Hnatov in Berlin (December 2025)
- **Assessment:** Kushner brings family trust that no envoy can match. He and Witkoff together represent Trump's preference for personal loyalty over institutional expertise.

### Kirill Dmitriev — Putin's Bridge to Trump World
- **Background:** CEO of Russian Direct Investment Fund (RDIF). Former investment banker. On U.S. sanctions list.
- **Role:** Named Russian special presidential envoy on foreign investment (February 2025). Key drafter of the Ukraine peace proposal.
- **History:** Met with Erik Prince in January 2017 as Trump was preparing to take office (documented by Mueller investigation). Has been Putin's preferred back-channel to Trump's circle since the first term.
- **Assessment:** The Russian side's most effective operator. Understands American business culture, speaks the language of deals, and has direct access to Putin. The Witkoff-Dmitriev axis has become the primary diplomatic channel for the most important negotiation of this era.

### Roman Abramovich — Diminished but Present
- **Role:** Early mediator between Russia and Ukraine in 2022. Was in the Kremlin meeting Putin just before the first 2025 direct talks, under cover of a "meeting of the board of trustees of the Bolshoi and Mariinsky Theaters."
- **Current status:** Ukrainian officials say he is "not involved in the negotiation track." His influence has been eclipsed by the Witkoff-Dmitriev channel.

### Keith Kellogg — The Sidelined Professional
- **Background:** Retired 3-star general. Named as America's envoy to Russia and Ukraine at the start of Trump 2.0.
- **Fate:** Pushed out within 10 months. His pro-Kyiv views made him "unacceptable" to Moscow. Resigned when the Witkoff-Dmitriev peace plan was revealed.
- **Assessment:** His removal signals that professional diplomatic/military expertise has been entirely replaced by personal loyalty networks in Trump's foreign policy.

**For the SIM:** The back-channel network is critical. Model it as a parallel diplomatic system that operates outside normal state-to-state channels. The Witkoff-Dmitriev axis should be a key game mechanism — decisions about Ukraine are being made by real estate developers and investment bankers, not generals and diplomats.

## 7. CHINA INTERNAL DYNAMICS

### Xi-Li Qiang (Premier) Relationship
- Li Qiang is Xi's protege — brought onto Xi's provincial Standing Committee in Zhejiang in 2005. His rise to #2 is entirely due to Xi's patronage.
- **Deliberate tension management:** Xi intentionally maintains tension between Li Qiang and Cai Qi (CCP Secretariat chief). Both know Xi wants competition between them — it keeps them checked and balanced while strengthening Xi's supreme position.
- Li Qiang handles the economy; Cai Qi handles party discipline and information control. Their rivalry is structural and managed from above.

### Xi's Trusted Inner Circle
The Politburo Standing Committee (2025): All seven members are Xi loyalists — no factional balance remains.
- **Cai Qi:** Described as "a tightly coiled spring" and "the most dominant political power figure after Xi." Controls party discipline, cybersecurity, information flow. Worked under Xi in both Fujian and Zhejiang.
- **Wang Huning:** The "philosophical backbone" of the regime. Served under three leaders (Jiang, Hu, Xi). Chief architect of Belt and Road and "Xi Jinping Thought." The intellectual anchor.
- **Li Qiang:** Premier. Economic portfolio. Xi's man, but deliberately held in tension with Cai Qi.
- **Ding Xuexiang:** First Vice Premier. Another Zhejiang-era loyalist.

### Military Purges — Unprecedented in Scale
- **36 generals and lieutenant generals purged since 2022.** The largest military purge in decades.
- **Expelled from CMC:** Former Defense Ministers Wei Fenghe and Li Shangfu; CMC Vice Chairmen He Weidong and Zhang Youxia; CMC Political Work Director Miao Hua; Joint Staff Chief Liu Zhenli.
- **Rocket Force devastated:** Two consecutive commanders purged (Li Yuchao, then his replacement Wang Houbin — a navy officer installed in 2023 who fell under suspicion within a year).
- **Impact:** Only one sitting general remains on the CMC — Zhang Shengmin, promoted to vice chairman in late 2025. Xi's "Shaanxi Gang" and "Fujian Clique" (his two main PLA power bases) have been systematically dismantled.
- **November 2025:** Two of Xi's closest lieutenants "abruptly disappeared from public view" — rare signs of internal turbulence.

### Factional Landscape
- **Princelings vs. Tuanpai vs. Xi loyalists:** The traditional factional contest is effectively over. Xi has eliminated all rival factions from the Standing Committee. What remains is competition among Xi's own loyalists (e.g., Li Qiang vs. Cai Qi).
- **The Shaanxi Gang** (Xi's home province network) and **Fujian Clique** (from his years as Fujian governor) have been the backbone of his power — but the military purges have targeted both.
- **Implication:** Xi's power is absolute at the top but potentially fragile in the military, where mass purges create uncertainty and demoralization.

### Tech Leaders and Power
- **Jack Ma rehabilitation (February 2025):** Xi hosted a business symposium in Beijing that included Ma — his first public rehabilitation since the 2020 Ant Group IPO cancellation. Ma had "disappeared" for nearly 5 years after criticizing regulators.
- **Context:** Under threat from Trump and aggressive U.S. trade policy, Xi and the private sector "rediscovered their need for each other."
- **Broader pattern:** In 2024 alone, executives from 80+ listed companies were detained by authorities, often using "long-range fishing" (detention by authorities from unrelated regions). Success remains a liability in China.
- **Assessment:** The line between being an indispensable economic asset and a challenge to state authority remains dangerously thin. Xi decides who is welcomed and who is sidelined.

**For the SIM:** China should be modeled as a monolithic exterior with significant internal fragility. The military purges mean Xi may be hesitant about military adventures (the Rocket Force — his nuclear deterrent — has been decapitated twice). The tech sector relationship is transactional and fear-based. Internal competition among loyalists creates information bottlenecks.

## 8. MELONI-TRUMP: The Favored European

**Overall dynamic:** The warmest Trump relationship with any European leader. Based on shared ideology (immigration, anti-woke, national sovereignty), personal chemistry, and strategic calculation on both sides. Meloni is the one European leader Trump has not humiliated.

**Key evidence:**

- **Inauguration (January 2025):** Meloni was the only European leader invited to Trump's inauguration.
- **April 2025 White House bilateral:** Trump called Meloni "fantastic," said she had "taken Europe by storm," and that "everybody loves her and respects her." Added: "she's become a friend."
- **Shared values:** Immigration restriction, LGBTQ skepticism, national sovereignty, "civilizational" social views, anti-wokeism.
- **Bridge role:** After Trump's tariff war, Meloni leveraged her personal ties to position Italy as the bridge between the US and EU. She is "first in line in facilitating a deal with the United States."
- **New Year 2026:** Personal phone call between Meloni and Trump to "set the tone for 2026" — operational coordination, not mere formality.

**The risk:** The Washington Post reported (March 2026) that the unpopular Ukraine/Iran war situation makes "friendship with Trump a liability for European leaders" — Meloni included.

**For the SIM:** Meloni/Italy should be the European leader with a direct personal channel to Trump that other Europeans lack. This creates both opportunity (bridge-builder) and resentment (from other EU leaders who see her as too close to Trump).

## 9. MODI — The Master Navigator (Until He Wasn't)

**Overall dynamic:** Modi spent years cultivating Trump through bear hugs, joint mega-rallies, and personal flattery. He simultaneously maintained Russia as a "special and privileged partner" and began normalizing relations with Xi's China. In 2025, this multi-alignment strategy collapsed when Trump's tariffs hit India directly.

### Modi-Trump: The Broken Friendship
- **History:** Famous bear hugs, joint rallies in Texas and India with tens of thousands. Personal, effusive engagement.
- **The break (2025):** Trump imposed 50% punitive secondary tariffs on India specifically as punishment for continued Russian oil imports. Called the trade relationship "a totally one sided disaster!"
- **Assessment by TIME:** "The 'friendship' Modi thought he had with Trump was ultimately a dysfunctional dalliance — defined by Modi's effusiveness and Trump's ruthless self-interest."
- **Trump quote (September 2025):** "I'll always be friends with Modi. He's a great prime minister, but I just don't like what he's doing at this particular moment."
- **Assessment:** Modi misread the personal relationship as a shield against policy conflict. It wasn't.

### Modi-Xi: The Awkward Dance
- **September 2025 SCO summit in Beijing:** Modi's first visit to China in 7 years. Xi and Modi pledged to resolve border differences.
- **The photo:** Xi, Modi, and Putin strode together, awkwardly holding hands for cameras. Xi proclaimed it was "time for the dragon and elephant to dance together."
- **Assessment:** Strategic rapprochement driven by shared Trump-era pressure, not genuine warmth.

### Modi-Putin: "Dear Friend"
- **At the same SCO summit:** Putin addressed Modi as "dear friend" and called Russia-India ties "special, friendly, and trusting." Modi termed the partnership "special and privileged."
- **Context:** This warmth came precisely when Trump was punishing India for buying Russian oil — making the embrace partly performative, partly genuine alignment.

**For the SIM:** India should be the classic swing state — trying to maintain relationships with all sides but finding it increasingly impossible. Modi's personal diplomacy style (hugs, flattery, mega-events) works until it doesn't. The India player should face constant pressure to choose sides while desperately trying not to.

## 10. MBS (SAUDI ARABIA) — Everyone's Best Friend

**Overall dynamic:** MBS has cultivated personal relationships with every major power simultaneously and more successfully than any other leader. He is the one figure who maintains warm personal ties with Trump, Putin, AND Xi simultaneously. His leverage: oil, investment capital, and geographic centrality.

### MBS-Trump: The Transactional Lovefest
- **First call after inauguration:** MBS was the first world leader to speak to Trump after his January 2025 swearing-in.
- **November 2025 White House visit:** First visit since the Khashoggi scandal. MBS pledged to boost Saudi investments from $600B to "almost $1 trillion." Signed deals on F-35 fighter jets, chips, and other technology.
- **Khashoggi:** Trump dismissed the murder entirely. When asked, he said "A lot of people didn't like that gentleman that you're talking about." The Khashoggi issue is dead as a factor in the relationship.
- **Ukraine mediation:** Trump is leaning on MBS to help broker a Ukraine peace deal.

### MBS-Putin: The Oil Brotherhood
- **Famous G20 high-five (2018):** Weeks after Khashoggi's murder, Putin and MBS high-fived and laughed — a deliberate display of mutual support.
- **OPEC+ partnership:** Together they control one-fifth of global daily oil production. Shared interest in high prices binds them.
- **Assessment:** MBS is one of a handful of leaders to maintain close ties with Putin since the Ukraine invasion.

### MBS-Democrats/Biden: The Frozen Relationship
- Biden had called Saudi Arabia a "pariah" over Khashoggi. The relationship never recovered. Trump's return gave MBS a clean slate.

**For the SIM:** Saudi Arabia should be the ultimate broker — capable of talking to everyone, allied with no one exclusively, leveraging oil and capital for influence. MBS's personal relationships are his primary diplomatic tool. The Khashoggi factor should matter for some players (European, Democratic) but not others.

## 11. ERDOGAN — The Indispensable Maverick

**Overall dynamic:** Erdogan maintains working relationships with both sides of every conflict. He is simultaneously a NATO member, a Putin interlocutor, a Trump admirer, and a mediator for Ukraine. His personal diplomacy style is transactional and relationship-intensive.

### Erdogan-Trump: "Valued Friend"
- **September 2025 White House visit:** First in 6 years. Meeting lasted 2 hours 18 minutes.
- **Nuclear deal signed:** Strategic civil nuclear cooperation (small modular reactors), plus $43B LNG deal.
- **Personal warmth:** Trump said "I have great respect for this man" and noted that Erdogan stayed friends with him during his 2021-2024 "exile." Trump physically adjusted Erdogan's chair during the meeting — a small gesture widely shared on social media.
- **Trump quote:** "When I was exiled — he knows about rigged elections better than anybody — when I was in exile, we were still friends. That's always a good way to test a friendship."

### Erdogan-Putin: The Complex Partnership
- **Mediation role:** Turkey hosted early Ukraine peace talks, brokered the Black Sea grain deal, facilitated prisoner swaps.
- **Erdogan says Turkey is a "reliable mediator"** and offered to host future Russia-Ukraine-US talks.
- **Assessment:** Erdogan and Putin share a personal rapport built on mutual recognition as strongman leaders who survived Western criticism. But it's bounded by Turkey's NATO membership and genuine disagreements on Syria/Libya.

### Erdogan-Xi: Economic Pragmatism
- **Less personal than the Trump or Putin relationships.** Driven by Belt and Road investment and energy cooperation.

**For the SIM:** Turkey/Erdogan should be the mediator character — able to talk to everyone, trusted by no one completely, extracting concessions from all sides in exchange for diplomatic services. The personal relationship with both Trump and Putin gives Erdogan unique positioning.

- Hosted the December 2025 London summit with Zelensky, Macron, and Merz.

- Compared Russian demands for Donbas to "a proposal for the United States to give up Florida."

**For the SIM:** Zelensky's European relationships should be modeled as a spectrum from genuine commitment (Macron, Tusk) through pragmatic support (Merz, Starmer) to active obstruction (Orban). The key dynamic is fatigue — even supporters are running out of resources and political capital.

## SYNTHESIS: Key Patterns for the SIM

1. **Personal relationships matter more than institutions in 2025-2026.** Trump's back-channel operators (Witkoff, Kushner, Dmitriev) have more influence than the State Department. Erdogan and MBS leverage personal relationships as their primary diplomatic tool. Xi's inner circle is based entirely on personal loyalty.

2. **The dominant emotional registers are:**
   - **US-Russia:** Asymmetric admiration (Trump up at Putin, Putin down at Trump)
   - **US-China:** Grudging respect amid perpetual economic warfare
   - **US-Europe:** Contempt (from Trump) and grief/betrayal (from Europeans)
   - **US-Ukraine:** Humiliation and forced transactionalism
   - **US-India:** Broken personal friendship, forced realignment
   - **China internal:** Enforced loyalty through fear and purges
   - **Middle East:** Transactional warmth (MBS plays all sides)

3. **Who fears whom:**
   - Europeans fear Trump AND Putin
   - Zelensky fears Trump's abandonment more than Putin's aggression
