## 2. US-CHINA RELATIONS (THE TRAP)

### Trade War Status

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Average US tariff on Chinese goods** | ~47.5% | Covers 100% of goods. Highest of any country. |
| **Effective tariff rate (many goods)** | ~30% | Base "fentanyl" (20%) + "reciprocal" (10%) tariffs in place |
| **Supreme Court IEEPA ruling** | Feb 20, 2026 | Court ruled president cannot use IEEPA to impose tariffs. Invalidated 10% fentanyl + 10% reciprocal tariffs on China. |
| **Section 301 investigations** | Initiated Mar 11, 2026 | New investigations into China, EU, 14 other countries over "structural excess capacity." Could become vehicle to re-impose struck-down tariffs. |
| **Trump's 100% tariff threat** | Announced Oct 2025 | Response to China's rare earth export controls. Set for Nov 1, 2025. |

**Key dynamic:** The Supreme Court ruling created a legal vacuum. The administration is scrambling to find alternative legal authorities (Section 301) to maintain trade pressure. Meanwhile, effective tariffs remain high due to layered pre-existing duties.

### Military Tensions

- **Taiwan:** PLA conducted "Justice Mission 2025" drills (Dec 29-30, 2025) — largest-ever simulated blockade of Taiwan, with live fire in Taiwan Strait for first time since 2022.
- **Drone incursion:** PLA flew surveillance drone through Taiwanese airspace over Pratas (Dongsha) Island on Jan 17, 2026 — possibly first confirmed violation of Taiwan's territorial airspace in decades.
- **South China Sea:** China accelerated land reclamation at Antelope Reef (since Oct 2025). Dredging activity at disputed coral features.
- **Three seas linkage:** Growing risk of coordinated PLA activity across East China Sea, Taiwan Strait, and South China Sea simultaneously.
- **China defense budget 2026:** ~$278 billion (+7% YoY).
- **US response:** Called on China to "halt military pressure on Taiwan." Japan and Philippines increasingly aligned with US.

### Technology Competition

- **AI chip export controls loosened:** BIS final rule (Jan 15, 2026) shifted from "presumption of denial" to "case-by-case" licensing for advanced chips to China. Nvidia H200 and AMD MI325X exports to China now permitted under certain conditions.
- **Trump Beijing visit:** Administration prioritizing stable trade talks, downplaying tech restrictions publicly.
- **Congressional pushback:** AI OVERWATCH Act (passed House Foreign Affairs Committee Jan 22, 2026) would give Congress veto over AI chip export licenses.
- **China's response:** Domestic AI chip market share projected to reach 50% in 2026 (TrendForce). Rare earth export controls on US.
- **25% tariff on "covered products":** Effective Jan 15 — advanced AI chips not destined for US supply chain.

### Power Balance Perception

China is perceived as both catching up AND being constrained. Key tensions:
- China's domestic semiconductor capability growing fast (50% AI chip market domestically)
- But still dependent on Western equipment/design tools
- Military spending growing at 7%/year but from a lower base
- Economic headwinds (property crisis, deflation) seen as structural weakness
- US technological lead narrowing but still significant in frontier AI

## 5. KEY FOREIGN POLICY ISSUES

### A. Ukraine/Russia

- **Status:** Active war, no ceasefire. Negotiations ongoing but stalled.
- **US policy shift:** Trump administration did "almost 180" from Biden's Ukraine support. Pushing for deal by June 2026.
- **Geneva talks:** US-Ukraine-Russia meetings held Feb 2026. Zelenskyy said US wants agreement by June.
- **Russia rejection:** Lavrov dismissed US-Ukraine 20-point peace plan.
- **"Coalition of the Willing":** Paris Declaration (Jan 6, 2026) — 35 countries committed to security guarantees for Ukraine post-ceasefire.
- **Reconstruction:** US-Ukraine finalizing bilateral security guarantees + up to $800B reconstruction agreement (requires Congressional ratification).
- **Net effect:** Ukraine has "fallen significantly off the American agenda." Europe being told to take primary responsibility.

### B. Iran — ACTIVE WAR

- **Operation Epic Fury:** Launched Feb 28, 2026. Joint US-Israel strikes on Iran.
- **Trigger:** Iranian nuclear program + 2025-26 protests + regime crackdown killing thousands.
- **Opening salvo:** Killed Supreme Leader Khamenei and senior officials.
- **Iran retaliation:** Hundreds of missiles + thousands of drones launched across Middle East.
- **Objectives (Trump stated):** (1) Prevent nuclear weapon, (2) destroy missile arsenal, (3) degrade proxy networks, (4) destroy Iranian navy, (5) regime change from within.
- **Civilian casualties:** 600+ killed in first days; 6,668 civilian structures targeted by Mar 7.
- **Domestic impact:** Approval rating at new lows. Gas prices spiking. Constitutional debate over war authorization.
- **DNI assessment (Mar 18):** Iranian regime "appears intact but largely degraded."

### C. Venezuela / Cuba / Caribbean

- **Venezuela:** Operation Absolute Resolve (Jan 2026) — US forces captured Maduro in Caracas, flew him to NY for drug trafficking trial. Framed as "counter-narcotics law enforcement."
- **Cuba energy crisis:** Following Maduro capture, US halted Cuban oil imports from Venezuela. Executive order (Jan 29) declared Cuba "unusual and extraordinary threat." Threatened tariffs on any country supplying oil to Cuban government.
- **Cuba negotiations:** Diaz-Canel confirmed (Mar 13) diplomatic talks with US over energy blockade.
- **Regional reaction:** Caribbean leaders alarmed. Mexico, Canada, Russia offering aid to Cuba.

### D. Greenland / Arctic

- **Trump's push:** Renewed demand to acquire Greenland, with or without Denmark's consent. Threatened tariffs on Denmark and 7 other European countries (10% starting Feb 1, escalating to 25% by June).
- **De-escalation:** Trump withdrew tariff threats Jan 21 after deal with NATO SecGen Rutte.
- **Strategic rationale:** Arctic ice thinning opening new sea routes; mineral resources; security approaches to North America.
- **Status:** Greenland government says "not for sale." Crisis defused but underlying ambition remains.

### E. NATO / European Relations

- US pushing Europe to lead own conventional defense ("burden sharing" → "burden shifting")
- Europe committed to 5% GDP defense spending at 2025 Hague summit
- US deploying additional forces to Europe (MDTF, 6th destroyer) while rhetorically demanding Europeans take over
- Iran war straining relations — some allies critical

## 6. SIM ROLE DYNAMICS (COLUMBIA TEAM)

Based on real-world dynamics, here are the character archetypes for Columbia's fictional roles. These are INSPIRATIONS, not portraits — all names in the SIM are fictional.

### DEALER (President) — Inspired by Trump dynamics

**Character archetype:** An aging, domineering leader in his final term with nothing to lose. Transactional worldview — everything is a deal. Demands personal loyalty above all. Approval ratings cratering (36-38%) but maintains iron grip on his party base. Currently at war with Iran while simultaneously pursuing trade deals with China.

**Key dynamics to capture:**
- Term-limited — cannot run again. This creates both freedom and lame-duck risk.
- "Strongman" persona — makes bold moves (Venezuela raid, Iran strikes, Greenland demands) but faces consequences (gas prices, legal challenges, approval collapse).
- Inner circle loyalty test — cabinet members who publicly disagree get fired.
- Age (79) — stamina/judgment questions swirl beneath the surface.
- Multiple simultaneous crises — Iran war, tariff chaos, debt, midterms approaching.

### VOLT (Vice President) — Inspired by Vance dynamics

**Character archetype:** Young (41), ambitious deputy positioning himself as the successor. Once a vocal critic of the Dealer, now his most articulate defender — gives "lawyerly" rationale for policies the Dealer announces by instinct. Privately navigating the Iran war's political cost, which could hurt his 2028 ambitions.

**Key dynamics to capture:**
- Succession ambition — widely expected to seek presidency in 2028. Early polls show commanding lead.
- The "enforcer" role — serves as Trump's attack dog while maintaining intellectual veneer.
- Iran war dilemma — supporting a war that's politically costly, but breaking with the Dealer would be fatal.
- RNC Finance Chairman — unusual role for sitting VP, shows party machinery control.
- Generational contrast with Dealer — tech-savvy, Appalachian roots vs. NYC real estate elite.

### ANCHOR (Secretary of State) — Inspired by Rubio dynamics

**Character archetype:** The diplomat who prefers negotiation but serves a leader who prefers ultimatums. Cuban-American heritage gives personal fire on Western Hemisphere issues (Venezuela, Cuba). Hawkish on China but emphasizes "obligation to communicate." Also a potential 2028 rival to Volt.

**Key dynamics to capture:**
- Walking a tightrope — "we always prefer to settle it in different ways" while the boss just launched a war.
- China complexity — sees CCP as existential threat but acknowledges need for dialogue ("geopolitical malpractice not to talk").
- Latin America expertise — deep personal investment in Cuba/Venezuela policy.
- Potential 2028 rivalry with Volt — both jockeying to be Trump's successor.
- Munich/G7 circuit — representing US to skeptical allies.

### SHIELD (Secretary of Defense / "War") — Inspired by Hegseth dynamics

**Character archetype:** The warrior-populist who became defense chief. Former combat veteran and media personality with no senior Pentagon experience before appointment. Confirmed by the narrowest margin in history. Renamed the department "War" as a statement. Prioritizes "warrior ethos" and lethality over technology and institutional expertise. Currently managing the Iran campaign.

**Key dynamics to capture:**
- "Warrior ethos" above all — dismissive of bureaucracy, academia, DEI, mandatory training.
- Managing a $1 trillion budget with combat operations in Iran, Venezuela aftermath, and Pacific deterrence simultaneously.
- Anti-institutional streak — eliminated Harvard fellowships, renamed DoD, purging "woke" culture.
- Faith-based justifications — has invoked Christian faith to justify military operations.
- NDS barely mentions technology — a philosophical choice that creates friction with tech-focused military leaders.

### SHADOW (CIA Director) — Inspired by Ratcliffe dynamics

**Character archetype:** The intelligence chief who straddles the White House and Langley. First person to serve as both DNI and CIA Director — uniquely wired into the intelligence ecosystem. Pledged to intensify covert ops against China. But reportedly "hands-off" at CIA headquarters, spending most time at the White House. The rank-and-file aren't sure who's really running the agency.

**Key dynamics to capture:**
- China-obsessed — "dramatically increased intelligence resources devoted to China" as DNI, continuing as CIA Director.
- White House shadow — physically present at WH more than CIA. This creates loyalty questions and operational gaps.
- Has so far insulated CIA from political meddling — but for how long?
- Iran intelligence — was the CIA's intelligence on Iran's nuclear program the basis for war? Questions about accuracy.
- Quiet operator — low public profile compared to other cabinet members.

### TRIBUNE (Congressional Opposition Leader) — Inspired by Jeffries/Schumer dynamics

**Character archetype:** The opposition leader(s) who must decide whether to fight everything or pick battles. Leading a party that's favored in midterm polling (+4.8%) but struggling to articulate a clear counter-narrative on Iran (won't say "no war" but criticizes lack of "plan"). Two leaders (House and Senate minorities) who coordinate daily but have different temperaments and occasional strategic disagreements.

**Key dynamics to capture:**
- Midterm opportunity — Democrats favored to gain significant ground in Nov 2026.
- Iran dilemma — progressive base demands "no war" but leadership won't commit, fearing "soft on Iran" attacks.
- Calls for Dealer impeachment/censure over war authorization — but politically risky.
- Coalition under pressure — progressive/moderate split on Iran, economy, immigration.
- Power of investigation — if midterms flip the House, massive investigative powers unlocked.

### CHALLENGER (Potential Presidential Opponent) — Inspired by 2028 Democratic field dynamics

**Character archetype:** The outsider positioning to challenge the Dealer's party in 2028. Could be a governor from a red state who wins against the odds (Beshear archetype), a celebrity-adjacent progressive (Buttigieg), or a California-style progressive executive (Newsom). The Challenger isn't in government — they're building a movement from outside.

**Key dynamics to capture:**
- Electability argument — "I won in Trump country, I can win nationally" (Beshear model).
- Anti-war positioning — Iran war creates an opening for a candidate who opposes it.
- Economic populism — inflation, gas prices, debt create fertile ground.
- Generational argument — against an aging ruling party (79-year-old president, 41-year-old successor).
- Primary vs. general tension — need to win progressive primary but centrist general.
