# Research Brief: HEARTLAND (Ukraine) — Q1 2026
**Compiled:** 2026-03-26 | **Purpose:** Country seed development for Thucydides Trap SIM

---

## 1. ZELENSKY SITUATION

### Approval Ratings
- Zelensky's approval has fallen dramatically from wartime highs (~90% in early 2022) but remains above 50%. Latest Ipsos polling shows 72% approval for "the job he is doing as president." However, presidential race polling tells a different story: only 22.6% would vote for him in a first-round election (March 2026 poll).
- His approval spiked to 68% after his public confrontation with Trump in the Oval Office, but this was a rally effect that has since faded.
- Trump falsely claimed Zelensky had a 4% approval rating; multiple fact-checks confirmed this was fabricated.
- A major corruption scandal in late 2025 involving senior officials — including the resignation of longtime chief of staff Andriy Yermak — significantly damaged his standing.

### Term and Legitimacy
- Zelensky's presidential term officially expired in May 2024. Elections were postponed under martial law, which is constitutionally permissible but politically contentious.
- He faces growing external pressure from Washington to organize elections in 2026, which would require constitutional/legal changes under martial law.
- Zelensky has said he is "ready" to hold elections if Washington and Brussels can guarantee their security.
- Opposition leaders (Poroshenko, Tymoshenko) argue elections should only be held after peace is established.

### Leadership Evolution
- From comedic outsider to wartime hero to embattled wartime president under domestic and international pressure. The heroic image from 2022 ("I need ammunition, not a ride") has given way to a more complicated picture: corruption allegations, conscription resistance, and forced compromises with Trump.

## 2. WAR STATUS (as of March 2026)

### Front Lines and Territorial Control
- Russia occupies approximately 20% of Ukraine's territory (including Crimea, annexed in 2014).
- From March 2025 to March 2026, Russia captured approximately 1,977 square miles (~0.8% of Ukraine's territory) — grinding but slow.
- Rate of advance has slowed considerably: averaging ~297 meters per day in late 2025/early 2026 (slower than the Battle of the Somme).
- Ukrainian forces clawed back ~100 square miles in the first two months of 2026.
- Key areas: Russia concentrating around Kostiantynivka (entered December 2025), pressing toward Kramatorsk and Sloviansk — the last major Ukrainian-held cities in Donetsk.

### Territorial Breakdown (Occupied Zones)
- **Luhansk Oblast:** ~90-95% Russian-controlled
- **Donetsk Oblast:** ~60-65% Russian-controlled, major cities contested
- **Zaporizhzhia Oblast:** ~60-65% Russian-controlled, city of Zaporizhzhia remains Ukrainian
- **Kherson Oblast:** ~70% Russian-controlled (eastern bank of Dnipro); western bank including Kherson city remains Ukrainian
- **Crimea:** Fully occupied since 2014

### Casualties
- Combined Russian and Ukrainian casualties approaching 2 million by spring 2026.
- Russia: ~1,292,170 total combat losses (Ukrainian General Staff figures; likely inflated but indicative of scale).
- Ukraine: estimated 500,000-600,000 casualties between February 2022 and December 2025.
- Casualty ratio roughly 2-2.5:1 in Russia's disfavor, but Ukraine cannot sustain even its lower losses given smaller population.

### Force Strength
- Ukraine's Armed Forces: approximately 677,000 active servicemen (some sources cite up to 900,000 active with 1.2 million reserves).
- Ranked 6th globally in active personnel (IISS) and 20th in overall military power (Global Firepower 2026).
- Equipment: 1,100+ tanks (5x fewer than Russia), supplemented by Western-supplied Leopard 2, Challenger 2, M1 Abrams, HIMARS, Patriot air defense.
- Domestic production now covers ~76% of weapons/equipment needs: drones (tactical and long-range strike), missiles, artillery, radar, electronic warfare, ammunition, armored vehicles.

### Conscription and Mobilization
- Acute conscription crisis. "Busification" — forced mobilization by state patrols dragging men from subways and streets.
- In 2024, 51,000 soldiers left units without permission (AWOL) — more than double the previous year.
- Draft evasion widespread. Young men actively avoid Territorial Recruitment Centers.

## 3. ZALUZHNY

### Current Position
- Serving as Ambassador of Ukraine to the United Kingdom (appointed March 2024 after being dismissed as Commander-in-Chief in February 2024).
- Also serves as Ukraine's Permanent Representative to the International Maritime Organization.

### Political Future
- In December 2025, reportedly informed Zelensky of his intention to resign the ambassadorship in January 2026.
- Discussed potential roles including: Prime Minister, Head of the Office of the President, Ambassador to the US, or returning as Commander-in-Chief. Did not express interest in any at the time.
- In February 2026, stated he could not consider his political future "until after the war" and martial law is lifted.
- European actors are reportedly promoting Zaluzhny as a political alternative to Zelensky.

### Polling
- In the latest poll (March 2026): 19.3% would vote for Zaluzhny in first round (vs. 22.6% for Zelensky).
- **In a hypothetical runoff against Zelensky, Zaluzhny would win decisively: 64% to 36%** (SOCIS poll, December 2025).
- Zaluzhny's party gaining parliamentary support.
- His profile as former military commander gives him enormous credibility with the armed forces and the public.

## 4. POROSHENKO

### Political Positioning
- Calling for a "coalition of national unity" and a government of national unity.
- Urging Zelensky to meet with democratic opposition.
- Positioning as the experienced statesman who can bridge divides.
- His European Solidarity party maintains significant parliamentary presence.

### Stance on Elections
- Opposes elections during martial law — "elections should take place only after peace has been established."
- Wants free, fair elections in accordance with European standards.

### International Engagement
- Maintaining contacts with both Democratic and Republican parties in Washington.
- Described as an "important and interesting interlocutor" by international actors.

### Polling
- 5.7% in first-round presidential polling (March 2026) — a distant fourth behind Zelensky (22.6%), Zaluzhny (19.3%), and Budanov (10.5%).
- Poroshenko's base is the nationalist/pro-European establishment voter.

## 5. ECONOMY

### GDP and Growth
- Pre-war GDP (2021): ~$200 billion. GDP collapsed by ~30% in 2022.
- 2025 GDP: ~$211.5 billion (nominal). Real GDP grew 1.8% in 2025.
- 2026 projected GDP: ~$225.6 billion. Real growth forecast: 2.4-2.5% (EBRD lowered from 5% due to continued war).
- Economy has shown resilience but remains severely stressed.

### War Damage and Reconstruction
- Total reconstruction and recovery cost: **$588 billion over the next decade** (~3x nominal GDP).
- Direct damage from full-scale war: $195 billion as of end-2025.
- 14% of all housing damaged or destroyed, impacting 3+ million households.

### Fiscal Situation
- Budget deficit: 18.8% of GDP in 2025, projected to narrow to 19.3% in 2026 (still enormous).
- Public debt: rose from ~101-102% of GDP in 2025 to projected 122.6% of GDP in 2026.
- Public debt increased by $47 billion in 2025 alone.
- Heavily dependent on international financial support to cover the deficit.

### Inflation
- 2025: ~8-12.7% (varying measures).
- 2026 forecast: 6.1-10.4% (IMF vs. government projections).
- Still elevated but trending down.

### Population Loss
- **5.9 million refugees recorded globally** (5.3 million in Europe) as of February 2026.
- **3.7 million internally displaced** within Ukraine.
- **One-third of Ukraine's total population has been forcibly displaced.**
- Top destinations: Germany (23%), Poland (19.5%).
- Young, educated population disproportionately represented among those who left.

## 6. PEACE NEGOTIATIONS

### Trump's 28-Point Peace Plan
- The Trump administration has proposed a 28-point peace plan.
- **Territorial provisions:** Crimea, Donetsk, and Luhansk to be recognized by the US as "de facto Russian territory." Parts of Donetsk still under Ukrainian control would also be ceded but designated a "neutral buffer zone."
- **NATO provision:** Ukraine would be barred from joining NATO and required to constitutionally enshrine this prohibition.
- **Security provisions:** Participants seeking concrete outcomes on ceasefire monitoring, multinational force deployment, defense cooperation.

### Ukraine's Position
- Zelensky's administration refuses to sign any deal that formally cedes occupied land to Russia.
- Seeking a guarantee that the US would intervene if Russia launches another invasion.
- Paris talks (January 2026) yielded "significant progress" — UK and France pledged troops for security guarantees, US backed security guarantees.

### Russia's Position
- Russia reiterates demand for Ukraine to abandon territory.
- Demands recognition of all four annexed oblasts (in their entirety, not just occupied portions).

### US-Ukraine Minerals Deal
- Signed April 30, 2025. Joint investment fund for reconstruction, capitalized by future mineral extraction revenues.
- US gets preferential mineral extraction rights; Ukraine retains subsoil ownership.
- 55 minerals listed including titanium, lithium, uranium, rare earths.
- Originally Washington demanded $500 billion share; Zelensky rejected, leading to Trump calling him "a dictator."
- Future US military assistance counts as US investment into the fund.

## 7. EU MEMBERSHIP

### Technical Progress
- As of March 2026, the EU Commission is opening all six negotiation clusters, albeit "informally," while the Hungarian veto remains in place.
- Substantial reform progress acknowledged by Brussels.

### Hungary's Blocking
- Hungary's parliament adopted a formal resolution rejecting Ukraine's EU membership, arguing that admitting a country at war risks making the bloc a party to the conflict.
- Hungarian Parliament voted specifically against Ukraine's membership and war support.
- Viktor Orban's government has consistently vetoed progress.

### EU Response
- European Parliament voted to use qualified majority voting (instead of unanimity) for intermediate accession steps.
- **Key variable:** Hungary's April 2026 parliamentary elections. If Orban loses, the veto may be lifted.
- Contingency plans: pressure Trump to lean on Orban, or strip Hungary of voting rights under Article 7.

### Five-Step Plan
- EU working on accelerated membership plan for Ukraine by 2027, contingent on overcoming Hungary's veto.

## 9. WESTERN SUPPORT

### American Support
- Since February 2022: US committed $66+ billion in security assistance.
- Under Trump (second term): **US has suspended all aid to Ukraine.**
- Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2026 ends USAID and reduces foreign assistance by ~$10 billion.
- US contributions dropped by up to 99%.

### European Response
- EU committed $70+ billion; UK $17+ billion.
- European military aid rose 67% above 2022-2024 average.
- Non-military aid increased 59%.
- Europe stepping up to partially compensate for US withdrawal.
- **But:** military aid concentrated in Northern Europe; financial aid primarily through EU institutions. Support rests on a small number of shoulders.

### Public Opinion on Support
- European public support for military aid: 65.2% (declining).
- Percentage saying their government has "done too much" for Ukraine increased 7.9% since 2024.
- Strong support still exists but fatigue is real and growing.

## 10. DOMESTIC MOOD

### War Exhaustion
- Physical and moral exhaustion "palpable" but cohesion and resistance remain "impressive, even heroic."
- The early euphoria of civic purpose and volunteerism has been replaced by fear, fatigue, and division.
- Conscription fear "haunts the streets like a ghost."

### Conscription Resistance
- Tens of thousands going AWOL, reflecting fear of becoming a casualty plus deep societal fatigue.
- 51,000 soldiers left units without permission in 2024 (double the prior year).
- "Busification" — forced mobilization through street patrols — widespread and deeply unpopular.
- Eroding social cohesion as prolonged conflict, economic difficulties, and contradictory attitudes to mobilization strain national unity.

### Public Opinion on Peace vs. Fight
- 63% of Ukrainians "ready to continue fighting" (but this has fallen from much higher levels).
- Territorial concessions: transferring Donetsk/Luhansk "unacceptable" to 61%; transferring Zaporizhzhia/Kherson "unacceptable" to 82%.
- Only 7% find ceding Donetsk/Luhansk "acceptable"; 11% "rather acceptable."
- Population wants peace but overwhelmingly rejects the terms being offered.
- The gap between wanting the war to end and being willing to accept territorial loss defines the domestic political trap.

### Civic Society
- Volunteer networks still active, supporting military units and displaced families.
- But the gap between those fighting and those not fighting is a growing source of tension.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR SIM DESIGN

1. **The heroic trap is real.** Zelensky cannot concede territory (82% opposition to ceding Zaporizhzhia/Kherson) but the country is exhausting itself. The man who saved the nation in 2022 may not be able to save it through 2026.

2. **Zaluzhny is THE political alternative.** He beats Zelensky 64-36 in a runoff. His military credibility is his strongest asset. The "fight harder with better leadership" narrative is potent.

3. **Poroshenko is the establishment alternative** but polling at only 5.7%. His role is more as a potential kingmaker or coalition partner than a front-runner.

4. **The economy is aid-dependent.** Budget deficit of 19% GDP, debt over 100% GDP, reconstruction needs of $588 billion. Without Western financial support, the state cannot function.

5. **US aid has collapsed under Trump.** Europe is stepping up but cannot fully compensate. The minerals deal ties future aid to extraction rights — a transactional relationship that feels exploitative to many Ukrainians.

6. **The 28-point peace plan is unacceptable to Ukraine** as written — it would cede territory and ban NATO membership. But the pressure to accept some form of deal is immense.

7. **EU membership is the strategic prize** but blocked by Hungary. April 2026 Hungarian elections are a pivotal variable.

8. **Population loss of one-third** (5.9M refugees + 3.7M internally displaced) is a demographic catastrophe that will shape Ukraine for decades.

9. **The conscription crisis is real.** "Busification" and mass AWOL undermine both military effectiveness and social cohesion.

10. **Time is not on Ukraine's side** — resources depleting, Western fatigue growing, demographic hemorrhage continuing, military attrition grinding. But Russia is also bleeding heavily, and the question is who breaks first.
