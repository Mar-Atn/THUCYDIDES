## 1. THE WAR: Operation Epic Fury / Operation Roaring Lion

### Timeline
- **February 28, 2026:** United States and Israel launch coordinated surprise airstrikes on Iran. US codename: Operation Epic Fury. Israeli codename: Operation Roaring Lion.
- **March 3:** Red Crescent reports 600+ civilian deaths; independent monitors estimate 742.
- **March 4:** Iran effectively closes Strait of Hormuz via IRGC warnings and attacks on merchant shipping.
- **March 9:** Mojtaba Khamenei announced as new Supreme Leader after rushed Assembly of Experts election (March 3-8).
- **March 12:** 21 confirmed attacks on merchant ships in Hormuz. Tanker traffic down ~70%. 150+ ships anchored outside strait.
- **March 13:** Israeli military estimates 3,000-4,000 Iranian soldiers/commanders killed.
- **March 17:** Limited ground operations begin (Israel in southern Lebanon, not Iran mainland).
- **March 21:** War enters fourth week with no clear end in sight.
- **March 23-25:** Trump issues and then walks back ultimatum on Iran power plants. Iran rejects 15-point US plan. Iran lays out 5 conditions including war reparations and sovereignty over Hormuz.
- **March 26 (Day 27):** Iranian health officials report 1,937 dead (including 240 women, 212 children), 24,800+ wounded. Oil at $110+/barrel, spiked past $120 after Hormuz closure. 13 US service members killed.

### Key Targets Hit
- Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei assassinated (Israeli strike on compound). Family members also killed.
- Defense Minister Aziz Nasirzadeh killed.
- IRGC commander Mohammad Pakpour killed.
- Defence Council secretary Ali Shamkhani killed.
- Four top Ministry of Intelligence officials killed.
- Seven senior security leaders confirmed dead by IDF.
- Nuclear facilities at Natanz, Isfahan, and Fordow extensively damaged.
- IRGC command infrastructure severely degraded.

### Iran's Response
- Missile barrages on Israeli cities and US bases in Gulf (UAE, Qatar, Bahrain).
- Effective closure of Strait of Hormuz (IRGC Navy warnings + attacks on shipping).
- Continued shelling of Gulf neighbors' infrastructure.
- Threat to strike energy and desalination facilities across the region.
- Diplomatic rejection of US ceasefire terms; demand for reparations and Hormuz sovereignty recognition.

### The Deadlock (as of Day 27)
- Columbia/Levantia cannot achieve regime change through air power alone.
- Iran cannot win conventionally but inflicts enormous economic pain through Hormuz blockade.
- Ceasefire negotiations stalled — Iran rejected Trump's 15-point plan, Trump walked back power plant strike ultimatum.
- Indirect talks via Pakistan as intermediary.
- No ground invasion of Iran mainland attempted — the coastal kill zone deters it.

## 2. IRANIAN LEADERSHIP

### Supreme Leader: Mojtaba Khamenei (new)
- Son of Ali Khamenei. Selected March 3-8, announced March 9.
- IRGC orchestrated his selection — pressured Assembly of Experts members.
- Seen as "more pliant version of his father" by IRGC.
- Lacks his father's religious authority within clerical hierarchy.
- Reliance on IRGC for legitimacy reinforces their power.
- Reports of his "disappearance" — possibly in hiding, possibly injured. Uncertainty about whether he is actually governing.
- Described as "his father on steroids" by some analysts — but this may reflect IRGC projection through him rather than his own authority.

### IRGC Commander: Ahmad Vahidi
- Former Quds Force commander, former defense minister.
- Emerging as most senior military figure.
- "New commander in chief" per reporting.
- IRGC now holds veto power over diplomatic decisions.
- Manages military campaign, Hormuz blockade, proxy coordination.
- The real power center since the war began.

### President: Masoud Pezeshkian
- Moderate/reformist president — elected 2024.
- Does NOT control the military.
- Attempted diplomacy: apologized for Iran's attacks on Gulf states, promised to end attacks unless strikes originated from their countries.
- Swiftly criticized by IRGC and hardliners — forced to walk back apology.
- Treading carefully to curry favor with new Supreme Leader.
- Represents engagement/reform direction but has no institutional levers.

### Foreign Minister: Abbas Araghchi
- Professional diplomat.
- Offered to dilute enriched uranium (CBS interview, March 15).
- Said Tehran will continue "resistance" and does not intend to negotiate (March 26).
- Caught between diplomatic instincts and IRGC pressure.

### Power Dynamic
- IRGC is the real power center — controls military, economy (~1/3 of GDP), and now veto over diplomacy.
- New Supreme Leader is an IRGC puppet, not an independent authority.
- President has popular legitimacy but no institutional power over military/nuclear.
- Traditional clerical hierarchy weakened — "serious power battle" between mullahs and IRGC.

## 3. HORMUZ BLOCKADE: THE GROUND-BASED KILL BOX

### How Iran Blocks Hormuz (NOT conventional naval blockade)

**Anti-Ship Missiles (coastal, mobile, ground-based):**
- Khalij Fars: 300km range, maneuvering reentry vehicle, electro-optical seeker for moving ships.
- Hormuz-2: Radar-homing/anti-radiation variant targeting emitting warships.
- Noor: Chinese C-802 derivative, sea-skimming cruise missile, mobile launchers, concealed coastal positions.
- Qader: Extended-range Noor (200-300km), sea-skimming, covers Strait AND Gulf of Oman approaches.

**Island Fortifications:**
- Three IRGC-controlled islands positioned along shipping corridors.
- Underground bunkers, hidden missile batteries, drone bases, radar installations.
- Keshm Island: heavy anti-ship missiles (P-15 Termit), drone bases, strategic anchor of coastal defense.

**Mines:**
- 5,000-6,000 naval mines in inventory.
- Types: contact, drifting, moored hull-impact, acoustic, magnetic influence, limpet.
- A few mines make shipping insurance uneconomical — "insurance blockade."

**Swarm Boats:**
- IRGC Navy fast attack craft.
- Can overwhelm naval defenses through numbers.
- Commander of IRGC Navy killed by Israel (March 26) — but structure survives leadership losses.

**Submarines:**
- Small submarines for mine deployment and coastal defense.

### Why Air Strikes Alone Cannot Break It
Iran's blockade is NOT a conventional naval blockade that can be sunk. It is:
1. **Ground-based coastal missile batteries** — mobile, hidden in mountains, dispersed, redundant.
2. **Underground island fortifications** — hardened against air attack.
3. **Mine fields** — already deployed, difficult to clear under fire.
4. **Layered A2/AD** — designed not to win but to "stretch U.S. missile defenses, logistics, and political tolerance."

Fortune describes the Strait as an Iranian "kill box" — preventing the US Navy from securing it and letting tankers pass freely. The design is "less about decisive victory and more about stretching US resources."

### Ground Invasion Required
- To permanently break the blockade, you must destroy/capture the coastal positions.
- But landing forces enter the "kill box" — exposed to the same missiles, artillery, and mines.
- This is the military deadlock: Persia can't project power beyond the strait, but Columbia can't break the blockade without accepting massive casualties in a ground invasion.
- As of Day 27, no ground invasion of Iranian mainland has been attempted.

## 4. NUCLEAR PROGRAM STATUS

### Pre-War (February 2026)
- Iran had accumulated 440.9 kg of uranium enriched to 60% U-235 — a short technical step from weapons-grade 90%.
- Only NPT non-nuclear-weapon state to have produced and accumulated 60% enriched uranium.
- IAEA had NO access to any of Iran's four declared enrichment facilities.
- Iran terminated the Cairo agreement (November 2025).
- IAEA could not verify enrichment activities, stockpile size, or facility status.
- Isfahan Fuel Enrichment Plant — IAEA did not know if it was operational.

### War Damage
- Natanz, Isfahan, and Fordow extensively damaged by strikes.
- But: underground facilities at Fordow may have survived partially.
- Damage sets program back but does not eliminate capability entirely.

### Diplomatic Signals
- FM Araghchi offered to dilute/downblend enriched material (March 15, CBS).
- Pre-war, US-Iran negotiations in Geneva (Feb 17, 26) with IAEA Director General present.
- Trump demands Iran "permanently abandon nuclear ambitions."
- Iran rejects — demands sovereignty recognition and reparations.

### SIM Translation
- Nuclear L0, 60% toward L1 maps well to reality: 60% enrichment achieved, significant stockpile, but no assembled weapon and delivery systems incomplete.
- War damage reduces progress but doesn't reset to zero.
- Three directions: pursue (hardliners), maintain threshold (IRGC pragmatists), trade for opening (reformists).

## 5. PROXY NETWORK STATUS

### Overall: Significantly Degraded but Not Destroyed

**Financial pipelines disrupted:** Estimated $700M annually to Hezbollah alone — now severed.
**Syria logistics corridor collapsed:** Primary resupply route gone.
**IRGC command infrastructure destroyed:** Secure communications severed.
**Result:** Tehran lost ability to coordinate and direct proxy operations centrally.

### By Group:

**Hezbollah (Lebanon):**
- Lost primary resupply route (Syria corridor).
- Lost meeting space for IRGC-commander coordination.
- Still operationally active — launched attacks on Israel.
- Israel launched ground invasion of southern Lebanon (March 17).
- Significantly weakened but not eliminated.

**Houthis (Yemen):**
- Declined to escalate militarily during 2026 war.
- Prioritizing peace process with Saudi Arabia.
- Now manufacturing arms domestically — less dependent on Iran.
- Ideological solidarity but operational independence.
- Still capable of Red Sea disruption.

**Iraqi Militias:**
- Actually gained relative power — direct border with Iran (no Syria corridor needed).
- Now Tehran's only accessible proxy force.
- Demanding greater independence.
- Emboldened by their increased importance.

### Network Fragmentation
- Each group making autonomous decisions based on local interests.
- Fundamental shift: fragmented, volatile, less coordinated.
- "Weaker than at any point in the past decade — yet more volatile, more fragmented."

## 6. ECONOMY

### Pre-War Crisis (Already Severe)
- Deepest and longest economic crisis in modern Iranian history.
- Rial collapsed: from 42,000 to over 1,100,000 against USD.
- Year-on-year inflation hit 60% in January 2026; annual ~45%.
- World Bank projected economic contraction in both 2025 and 2026.
- Oil exports declining: crude loadings fell to 1.39 mb/d in January (down 26% YoY).
- Oil revenue: only $20B in first 8 months of fiscal year — far below nominal value.
- UN sanctions restored (September 2025) via E3 snapback trigger.

### War Impact
- War damage to infrastructure.
- Hormuz blockade cuts Iran's own oil exports (self-harming weapon — but less so since exports already declining).
- Global oil price spike benefits remaining exports but overall economy devastated.
- War spending consuming whatever fiscal reserves remain.

### IRGC Economic Empire
- Controls estimated one-third of Iran's economy (estimates range 10-50%+).
- Construction (Khatam al-Anbia), telecom (51% of TCI), oil/gas, banking, agriculture, medicine, real estate.
- Over 100 companies, $12B+ annual revenue in business and construction.
- Sanctions relief = IRGC profit. This gives IRGC pragmatic incentive for selective deals.

### Key Numbers for SIM
- GDP: ~$400-450B (nominal, pre-war). War damage reduces this.
- Game coins: 5 (1 coin = ~$100B — reflects sanctions-diminished effective economy).
- Oil producer: Yes, OPEC+ member, but production severely constrained.
- Inflation: 45-60% — catastrophic for population.

## 7. PROTESTS AND DISSENT

### 2025-2026 Protests
- Most extensive since 2022 "Women, Life, Freedom" movement (Mahsa Amini).
- Began December 28, 2025 — triggered by sharp currency collapse.
- Gen Z Iranians most active — politicized by 2022 Mahsa Amini protests.
- Slogans shifted toward monarchist sentiments (new development).

### Government Response
- Massacres of protesters (January 8-9, 2026) — death toll in thousands.
- Deadliest period of repression in decades.
- Internet and telephone restrictions imposed (January 8).
- Massive crackdown suppressed visible protests.

### War Effect on Protests
- Nationalist rallying effect from being attacked (standard wartime dynamic).
- But: the rallying effect is shallow — built on exhaustion, not enthusiasm.
- Population was already protesting BEFORE the war started.
- War temporarily suppressed dissent through nationalism + intensified repression.
- If war drags on without results, protest potential resurges.

### Population Demographics
- ~88 million people.
- ~60% under 30 — young, urban, educated, connected, frustrated.
- Massive brain drain (educated professionals leaving).
- The "street" is Dawn's constituency — no institutional power but enormous latent energy.

## 8. RELATIONSHIPS (Real-World)

### With United States (Columbia): AT WAR
- Operation Epic Fury ongoing.
- Trump demanding permanent nuclear abandonment.
- Iran demanding reparations, sovereignty recognition.
- Deadlocked — indirect talks via Pakistan.
- Iran rejected 15-point plan.

### With Israel (Levantia): MORTAL ENEMY
- Joint operations with US.
- Israel killed Khamenei, multiple senior officials.
- Ground invasion of southern Lebanon (Hezbollah front).
- Israel conducting continuous strikes, targeting leadership.
- Iran retaliating with missile barrages on Israeli cities.

### With Russia (Nordostan): ALLY
- BRICS+ partner. Arms supplier (Shahed drones were key Russian weapon in Ukraine).
- Sanctions evasion partner.
- Russia condemned strikes but provided no military support.
- Strategic alignment but Russia won't fight for Iran.
- UNSC veto protection.

### With China (Cathay): ENERGY CUSTOMER
- Buys Iranian oil at discount (China was ~48% of Iranian crude exports pre-war).
- Provides some technology.
- BRICS+ partner.
- Diplomatically supportive but cautious about direct involvement.

### With Gulf States (Solaria/Mirage): ENEMIES
- Iran attacked US bases in UAE, Qatar, Bahrain after strikes began.
- Shelling of Gulf infrastructure threatened.
- Desalination and energy facilities at risk.
- Gulf states hosting Columbia military logistics.

### With India (Bharata): OIL CUSTOMER
- Quietly buys Iranian crude despite sanctions.
- ~37% of top-5 export revenues.
- Non-aligned stance provides diplomatic space.

### With Turkey (Phrygia): COMPLICATED
- Muslim solidarity dimension.
- NATO member but independent actor.
- Potential mediator — BRICS+ observer.

## 9. THREE DIRECTIONS (SIM Design)

### (a) Hardline/Fundamentalist → FURNACE
**Real parallels:** Mojtaba Khamenei (new SL), hardline clerical establishment, ideological IRGC faction.
- Nuclear weapons as destiny.
- Resistance to West at all costs.
- BUT: new Supreme Leader is weak, dependent on IRGC, possibly hiding/injured.
- The ideological direction — but one that lacks the personal authority Ali Khamenei once had.

### (b) Pragmatic/IRGC → ANVIL
**Real parallels:** Ahmad Vahidi (IRGC commander), IRGC institutional leadership.
- The real power center since the war began.
- Controls military + economy + now veto over diplomacy.
- Pragmatic about deals if they serve IRGC interests.
- Business empire gives financial incentive for selective engagement.
- The kingmaker — can support hardline or reform depending on calculation.

### (c) Reform/Moderate → DAWN
**Real parallels:** Pezeshkian (president), Araghchi (FM), reform movement, protest generation.
- Genuine desire for engagement — Pezeshkian tried to apologize to Gulf states, was shut down.
- Araghchi offered nuclear downblend on TV.
- No military authority whatsoever.
- Represents the majority of the population (60% under 30, massive protests).
- The "reasonable face" — but with no institutional levers.

## 10. THE DEADLOCK

Both sides are stuck:
- **Columbia** killed the Supreme Leader, destroyed nuclear sites, killed senior officials — but cannot break Hormuz without ground invasion, and Iran hasn't collapsed.
- **Iran** is being bombed, lost its Supreme Leader, nuclear sites damaged — but Hormuz blockade inflicts enormous global economic pain, oil at $110-120/barrel, Gulf allies being shelled.
- **Ceasefire rejected** — Iran demands reparations and sovereignty; Trump demands permanent denuclearization.
- **No regime change** — despite killing Khamenei, the IRGC installed his son and maintained control.
- **War entering week 5** with no clear end — exactly the deadlock scenario for SIM start.
