## 1. XI JINPING — STATUS & SUCCESSION

**Age:** 72 (born June 1953, turns 73 in June 2026)
**Term status:** In his unprecedented third term as General Secretary, State President, and CMC Chairman. No constitutional limit after 2018 amendment. Expected to begin a FOURTH term at the 21st Party Congress in 2027.
**Succession:** Deliberately has NOT named a successor. The logic: naming a successor creates a "second center of power" (as happened when Hu Jintao groomed Xi). This means there is NO clear succession plan — a structural vulnerability.
**Health:** Reported health concerns circulating but unconfirmed. The European Times reports "health woes and quiet search for a successor." Official line: Xi called for "decisive progress in Healthy China Initiative" in March 2026 — possibly routine, possibly significant.
**Priorities for 2026:**
- 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-2030) — released and being implemented
- Political runway to 21st Party Congress (2027) — Xi must present himself as indispensable
- Technological self-reliance — core governance pillar
- Party control and national security — permanent priorities
- "National rejuvenation" — the overarching Xi Jinping narrative, which includes resolving Taiwan

**Key insight for SIM:** Xi is 72-73 with no successor. He will likely rule until at least 79 (2032), possibly to 82 (2035) or for life. But the ABSENCE of succession planning means any health event creates a crisis. His personal legacy requires "national rejuvenation" — and Formosa/Taiwan is the unfinished piece.

## 2. US-CHINA TRADE WAR & TECH RESTRICTIONS

**Current tariff status:**
- Effective US tariff rate on Chinese goods: ~30% (highest of any country)
- Multiple overlapping tariff regimes: Section 301 (unfair trade practices), Section 232 (national security), plus 15% global tariff (from Feb 24, 2026)
- US Supreme Court struck down IEEPA-based tariffs (10% fentanyl + 10% reciprocal tariffs) — creating legal chaos
- USTR launched two NEW Section 301 investigations into excess capacity and forced labor

**Recent deal (fragile):**
- Temporary tariff reductions agreed
- US suspends 50% subsidiary rule for export controls for one year
- China postpones controls on five additional rare earths and extraterritorial regulations for one year
- Deal described as "tactical truce, not strategic shift"

**Tech restrictions:**
- Chip export controls on China loosened in Jan 2026 — "case-by-case" licensing replaces "presumption of denial"
- Congress pushing AI OVERWATCH Act to give legislature veto over chip export licenses
- Semiconductor and rare earth disputes remain the core flashpoint

**China's retaliation:**
- Rare earth export controls on critical minerals
- Licensing requirements on rare-earth oxides, metals, magnet products
- Both sides agreed to tentative one-year suspension — to be revisited in 2026

## 4. CHINA MILITARY

**Navy (PLAN):**
- ~395 combat vessels vs. US Navy's ~295 deployable battle force ships (China already OUTNUMBERS the US by total hulls)
- BUT: quality gap remains — US has 11 carrier strike groups, China has 3 carriers (Liaoning, Shandong, Fujian)
- Fujian (Type 003) equipped with electromagnetic catapults — comparable to advanced Western carriers
- China ramping nuclear-powered attack submarine production
- Projected: up to half of 80 attack boat fleet nuclear-powered by 2035
- 6 new aircraft carriers planned by 2035
- PLAN credibly challenging US maritime dominance — but full challenge projected by 2040s, not 2026

**Nuclear arsenal:**
- 600 warheads as of 2025 (more than doubled since 2019)
- DOD forecasts 1,500 warheads by 2035
- Rapid expansion of silo-based ICBMs, mobile launchers, SLBMs
- NOT yet a peer of US/Russia (~3,700/~4,500 warheads respectively), but closing gap fast

**AI/Military integration:**
- Integrating AI into unmanned systems
- AI-enhanced cyber operations mapping adversary networks
- 2027 benchmark: joint operations integration with AI
- Focus on "intelligentized warfare"

**CMC crisis (critical for SIM):**

- CMC reduced to just TWO members: Xi Jinping and Zhang Shengmin (anti-corruption commissar, NO operational command experience)
- This is the most devastating military leadership purge since Lin Biao (1971)
- PLA leadership expected to be in a state of instability for most of 2026
- Purges undercut planning capacity for complex operations (exactly what a Taiwan invasion requires)

**Defense budget:**
- $275 billion officially (2026), 7% increase
- ~1.3-1.7% of GDP (Pentagon says actual spending significantly higher than reported)
- For comparison: US defense budget over $1 trillion

**South China Sea:**

## 5. CHINA ECONOMY

**GDP:**
- 2025 actual growth: 5.0% (met official target)
- 2026 official target: 4.5-5% (lowest since 1991)
- IMF forecast: 4.5%
- Goldman Sachs forecast: 4.8%
- Real growth probably lower than official figures — structural slowdown is undeniable

**Property crisis:**
- Property sector has shed over 50% of its value
- May not find its floor until 2027 or 2028
- Massive drag on local government finances (land sales were primary revenue source)
- Consumer confidence crushed by housing wealth destruction

**Deflation:**
- Persistent deflationary pressures since 2022
- CPI approximately 0% in 2025
- IMF projects CPI recovery to 0.8% in 2026 (well below Beijing's 2% target)
- Three consecutive years of deflation/near-zero inflation

**Youth unemployment:**
- 16.3% in January 2026 (officially)
- Near 18% by broader measures
- The "generation that must drive the next consumption cycle" is struggling

**Demographics:**
- Population fell for 4th consecutive year — down 3.4 million to 1.405 billion
- Birth rate hit lowest since 1949: 5.6 per 1,000 people (fewer than 8 million babies in 2025)
- 23% of population aged 60+ (up from 22% in 2024)
- Working-age population peaked in 2015 and declining
- By 2050: could lose 250 million people; fewer than 2 working-age adults per person 65+

**Trade surplus:**
- Record $1.189 TRILLION trade surplus in 2025
- Jan-Feb 2026: surplus $213.62 billion (beating expectations)
- Exports surged 21.8% YoY in Jan-Feb 2026
- Redirecting exports: ASEAN +29.4%, Europe +27.8%, South Korea +27%
- "New Three" driving exports: EVs (BYD), lithium batteries, solar cells
- Exports to US declining ~20%, but overall surplus GROWING — rerouting works

**Fiscal policy:**
- Deficit-to-GDP ratio set at ~4% (aggressive for China)
- Public expenditure to reach 30 trillion yuan (~$4.17 trillion) for first time
- "Appropriately accommodative" monetary policy — rate cuts and RRR cuts expected
- Consumption transition remains the stated but unachieved priority

## KEY SIM IMPLICATIONS

1. **The Window Paradox:** Cathay's military is growing but its military LEADERSHIP is in crisis (purges). Economy is slowing. Demographics are declining. Technology is advancing but still behind. The "window" for action on Formosa is opening militarily (raw numbers) but may be closing institutionally (can the purged military actually execute?) and economically (deflation, property, demographics).

2. **The Succession Bomb:** No designated successor. Any Xi health event = systemic crisis. The outside world won't know what's happening (opacity rule). This makes Cathay both more unpredictable and more fragile than it appears.

3. **Economic Leverage vs. Vulnerability:** Record trade surplus ($1.2T) and manufacturing dominance give enormous leverage. But dependence on exports means tariffs and sanctions hurt. Deflation and property crisis mean the domestic engine is sputtering. The economy is simultaneously a weapon and a weakness.

4. **Tech Race:** DeepSeek + Huawei represent real AI progress. But semiconductor manufacturing remains a decade behind without ASML. Rare earths are China's strongest tech leverage card. The race to AI L4 is existential — whoever gets there first may gain permanent advantage.

5. **The Purge Paradox:** Xi is more powerful than ever — and more isolated than ever. The military can't plan complex operations. The technocrats can't speak freely. The elders are suppressed. Control is total, but competence may be degraded.
