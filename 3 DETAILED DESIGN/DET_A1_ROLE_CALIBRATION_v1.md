# Role Calibration Table
## Thucydides Trap SIM — All 40 Roles (37 Base + 3 Expansion)
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-30 | **Author:** SIMON (SIM Design Architect)
**Status:** Active — for review

---

## Methodology

Each role scored on three axes (1-10):
- **Objectives Clarity**: How clear is it what this role should DO? 10 = crystal clear, 1 = vague
- **Dilemma Challenge**: How challenging are the trade-offs? 10 = impossible, 1 = straightforward
- **Cognitive Load**: How busy/complex is this role? 10 = overwhelmed, 1 = mostly watching

Sources: B2 Role Seed files, B3 Relationships, roles.csv

## CROSS-ROLE ANALYSIS

### Most Overloaded Roles (Cognitive Load 9-10)

| Role | Load | Risk |
|------|:----:|------|
| **Dealer** | 10 | Three wars, succession fight, every world leader demanding meetings, domestic political battles. Risk of player burnout. Needs a highly engaged, fast-thinking player. |
| **Helmsman** | 9 | Controls all Cathay decisions, manages paranoid team dynamics, Formosa decision looming, opacity rule means all external comms flow through you. Psychologically exhausting. |
| **Pathfinder** | 9 | Running a war, managing paranoia, back-channel diplomacy, monitoring team loyalty, OPEC+ decisions. Every conversation has coup subtext. |

**Recommendation:** These three roles need the strongest, most experienced players. Consider providing role-specific time management guidance or a dedicated moderator liaison.

### Most Underloaded Roles (Cognitive Load 1-4)

| Role | Load | Risk |
|------|:----:|------|
| **Sage** | 3 | Dormant until activation threshold. May sit through 2-3 rounds with nothing to do except have private conversations. |
| **Dawn** | 3 | No real power until stability drops. Elected president who controls nothing. Early rounds are waiting. |
| **Challenger** | 4 | No executive power. Shadow diplomacy and speeches only. Mechanically thin until Round 5 election. |
| **Ledger** (expansion) | 3 | Compass-lite without frozen assets, Western contacts, or coup mechanic. |
| **Pioneer** (expansion) | 4 | Tech diplomacy concept is interesting but disconnected from main action unless tech race becomes central. |

**Recommendation:**
- **Sage**: Build in periodic "consultation request" events from other Cathay team members. Give Sage intelligence packets to analyze and share privately. The dormant mechanic is correct but the player needs structured activity between activation moments.
- **Dawn**: Consider adding a "public appeal" mechanic that gives Dawn something to do each round even before the threshold -- e.g., international media statements that accumulate diplomatic credibility for use when the window opens.
- **Challenger**: Assign more foreign leader meetings in early rounds. Create a "campaign trail" sub-mechanic where Challenger must visit X countries per round to build momentum. Without this, Rounds 1-4 are thin.
- **Ledger**: Only activate this expansion role if the group exceeds 37 players. Otherwise, Compass covers the economic function.
- **Pioneer**: Only activate if a tech-race subplot is central to the session. Otherwise, Fixer is the better expansion role.

### Least Clear Objectives

| Role | Clarity | Issue |
|------|:-------:|-------|
| **Pioneer** (expansion) | 5 | Objectives are vague -- "tech supremacy" and "business deals" lack the specificity of other roles. No personal stake or ticking clock. |
| **Ledger** (expansion) | 5 | "Follow Pathfinder" and "institutional continuity" are passive. No independent agenda. |
| **Challenger** | 6 | Clear end-goal (win election) but unclear round-by-round activity. What does a powerless candidate DO in Rounds 1-4? |
| **Tribune** | 7 | Clear until midterms. If midterms are lost, the role collapses to "protest without tools" for 6 rounds. |

### Potentially Boring or Frustrating Roles

| Role | Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------|------------|
| **Sage** | High boredom risk in stable early game. Player may feel irrelevant for 2-3 rounds. | Pre-plan events that bring Sage close to activation threshold. Ensure Cathay stability dips at least once. |
| **Dawn** | High frustration risk -- title without power. Being silenced by IRGC repeatedly is thematically correct but mechanically punishing. | Give Dawn a "diplomatic credibility" tracker that accumulates value. International meetings should feel productive even when blocked domestically. |
| **Challenger** | Moderate boredom risk. Narrative-rich but mechanically thin. Lots of waiting for Round 5. | Structure foreign leader meetings as events. Create "campaign momentum" tracker that gives tangible feedback each round. |
| **Volt** | Moderate boredom risk. Second fiddle to Dealer with limited independent action. | The 10% Dealer incapacitation mechanic is the relief valve. Ensure this triggers at least once. Give Volt more business lobby interaction as structured events. |
| **Bulwark** | Moderate. No formal power until election. Shadow diplomacy is informal and hard to structure. | Create "military influence events" where Bulwark's network produces intelligence or operational effects. The Soldiers' Petition is great -- add more such moments. |

### Balance Recommendations

1. **Team Size Imbalance**: Columbia (7-9 roles) vs. Ruthenia/Persia/Sarmatia (3 each). Columbia players will interact mostly with each other. Ensure cross-team events force Columbia players to engage with the world, not just their internal succession drama.

2. **Domestic vs. International Roles**: Tribune, Challenger, Volt, and Sage are primarily domestic. They risk being siloed away from the geopolitical action. Create structured "crossover" moments -- Tribune investigating foreign policy, Challenger meeting foreign leaders, Sage's network detecting external signals.

3. **Activation-Dependent Roles**: Sage (stability < 5) and Dawn (stability < 3) are dormant by design. If neither threshold is crossed, two players have a frustrating experience. **Recommendation:** Ensure at least one of Cathay's or Persia's stability dips below threshold during the SIM. Calibrate starting conditions or inject events to guarantee this.

4. **The Solo Country Challenge**: 10 solo players operating alone. They have rich mechanics but no teammates to talk to between meetings. **Recommendation:** Create structured "multilateral events" (BRICS+ summit, G7, OPEC+ meeting, Quad) that force solo players to interact with each other, not just with team leaders.

5. **Resource Inequality**: Wellspring (10 coins) and Spire (8 coins) vs. Havana (0.5 coins) and Pyro (1 coin). This is intentional and correct -- but Havana and Pyro players need to understand that their gameplay is leverage and provocation, not spending. Include this framing in the role brief.

6. **The Expansion Role Problem**: Fixer is excellent -- clear role, unique mechanic, organic engagement. Pioneer and Ledger are thin. If using expansion roles, **Fixer first**, then consider whether Pioneer and Ledger genuinely add value or dilute the experience.
